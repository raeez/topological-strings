## Convention Translation

CDGP use the family

```text
sum_i x_i^5 - 5 psi prod_i x_i = 0,
```

with `psi ~ alpha psi` and hence true coordinate `psi^5`. The large-radius
coordinate used in the frontier note is

```text
z = (5 psi)^(-5) = 1/(5^5 psi^5).
```

Under this convention:

- large complex structure / large radius is `psi=infty`, equivalently `z=0`;
- the conifold branch `psi=1` is `z_c=5^{-5}`;
- the fundamental period is
  `omega_0(z)=sum_{n>=0} ((5n)!/(n!)^5) z^n`;
- the unnormalised logarithmic period used in the frontier arithmetic is
  `Omega_1(z)=omega_0(z) log z + sum_{n>=1} ((5n)!/(n!)^5) 5(H_{5n}-H_n) z^n`;
- the normalized mirror coordinate differs by the conventional `2 pi i`
  factor: CDGP/HKQ-style formulas place the mirror map in the normalized period
  ratio, while the frontier comparison uses the raw unnormalised modulus
  `|Omega_1/Omega_0|`.

Therefore the `z`-Picard--Fuchs operator in
`frontier_mnop_framing_volume.tex:330-332` is the supported convention after
translation:

```text
theta^4 - 5 z (5 theta+1)(5 theta+2)(5 theta+3)(5 theta+4),
theta = z d/dz.
```

The `psi`-operator displayed in
`frontier_mnop_framing_volume.tex:738-742` is not source-grade as written if
`psi` denotes CDGP's original family parameter. It becomes source-grade only
after renaming the operator variable to the large-radius `z` above, or after
rewriting it in the original `psi` variable with the correct `psi^{-5}` form.

3. The existence of logarithmic periods and an integral symplectic period basis.

   `2 pi i` normalization to be imported carefully.

Not supported by CDGP alone:

   That is a local arithmetic/computation claim once the period convention is
   accepted.

   This requires the separate `N_AJ` normalization fixture.

   `A_BCOV = A_Schwinger = A_period`, any Stokes normalization `S_c=1`, or any
   global resurgent sheaf.
6. GV/PT integrality, compact all-genus BCOV closure, convergence, or
   `Ob_UKD(C_tar)=0`.
7. Any CoHA, Hall, centre, trace-map, `E_2`-rigidity, or compact target
   construction.
