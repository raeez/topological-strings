     Introduction In this discussion every module over a ring R will be
understood to be a left i2-module. R will always have a unit, and every

B. Eckmann and A. Schopf have shown that if M is a module over any
ring, then there exists a unique, minimal, injective module E(M) con-
taining it. The module E(M) will be a major tool in our investigations,
and we shall systematically exploit its properties.
     In § 1 we show that if a module M has a maximal, injective sub-
module C (as is the case for left-Noetherian rings), then C contains a
carbon-copy of every injective submodule of M, and MjC has no injective
