\section*{Nilpotent truncation of the unreduced cotangent
factorization-center lift}

The umbrella theorem \ref{thm:main-local} is unconditional in parts
(a), (b), (c), (e) on the degree-zero Hamiltonian sector, and (f).
Parts (d) on the unreduced extension and (e) on the descendant
(one-antifield) sector are conditional on
\ref{prob:formal-factorization-center}, the unreduced Tate-coefficient
cotangent lift. The deciding obstructions are
\ref{lem:tate-casimir-obstruction} (Casimir support fails in the
direct-sum dual) and \ref{lem:finite-window-hamiltonian-obstruction}
(no naive finite-dimensional Lie truncation of the full sector). Two
routes were named in \ref{prob:formal-factorization-center}: a
weighted Tate completion (R1) or a nilpotent truncation (R2). This
section closes (R2) on a precisely specified truncation, identifies
exactly what is lost, and states the colim recovery question.

\subsection*{The truncation}

Let $\mathfrak h=(z_1,z_2)\C[[z_1,z_2]]=\mathfrak m$ be the reduced
Hamiltonian Lie algebra of the formal symplectic disk on $\C^2$, with
bracket $\{f,g\}=\partial_{z_1}f\,\partial_{z_2}g-
\partial_{z_2}f\,\partial_{z_1}g$ modulo constants. Let
$\mathfrak m^k=(z_1,z_2)^k\C[[z_1,z_2]]$ for $k\geq 1$ be the
$k$-th power of the maximal ideal. Fix integers $N\geq k\geq 3$ and
define the \emph{nilpotent truncation}
$$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^{(k)}:=\mathfrak m^k/\mathfrak m^{N+1},$$
with $\mathfrak m$-adic Poisson bracket inherited from the
ambient $\mathfrak h_{\mathrm{poly}}$. The standard choice is
$k=3$:
$$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}:=\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^{(3)}=
  \mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}.$$
Concretely, $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ is the finite-dimensional vector
space spanned by monomials $z_1^az_2^b$ with $3\leq a+b\leq N$.

\begin{rmk}[Why $k=3$ and not $k=2$]
The naive choice $k=2$ fails to give a nilpotent Lie algebra: the
degree-$2$ piece $H_2=\mathrm{span}\{z_1^2,z_1z_2,z_2^2\}$ is closed
under the Poisson bracket because $\{H_2,H_2\}\subset H_{2+2-2}=H_2$,
and the structure is exactly $\mathfrak{sl}_2$:
$$\{z_1^2,z_2^2\}=4z_1z_2,\quad
  \{z_1^2,z_1z_2\}=2z_1^2,\quad
  \{z_1z_2,z_2^2\}=2z_2^2.$$
Thus $\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ contains a nonabelian
semisimple subalgebra and is not nilpotent, hence does not satisfy
the Tate-conilpotency hypothesis of
\ref{lem:continuous-bar-cobar}. The truncation $k=3$ removes the
$H_2$ direction; the bracket on $\mathfrak m^3$ raises
$\mathfrak m$-adic degree strictly, making the truncation
nilpotent.
\end{rmk}

\begin{lem}[Truncation is a finite-dimensional nilpotent Lie algebra]
\label{lem:trunc-is-nilpotent-lie}
For every $N\geq 3$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ is a
    finite-dimensional Lie algebra of dimension
    $\binom{N+2}{2}-6$ (the number of
    monomials of degree between $3$ and $N$ inclusive).
  \item $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ is nilpotent of class at most
    $\lceil(N-2)/(k-2)\rceil-1=\lceil(N-2)\rceil-1$ in the
    descending Lie series, where $k=3$.
  \item $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee$ is finite-dimensional, hence the
    formal Casimir
    $C_{\mathfrak h_{\leq N}}=\sum_{3\leq d\leq N}\sum_i
     e_{d,i}\otimes e_d^i$
    is a convergent element of
    $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}\otimes\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee$ (a finite
    sum, no Tate completion required).
  \item The linear Hamiltonians $z_1,z_2\in\mathfrak m\setminus
    \mathfrak m^3$, the conformal-symplectic generators
    $H_2=\mathrm{span}\{z_1^2,z_1z_2,z_2^2\}\cong\mathfrak{sl}_2$,
    and the constant Hamiltonian $1\notin\mathfrak m$ are absent
    from $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ by construction.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(i)} Both $\mathfrak m^3$ and $\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ are Lie
ideals of the polynomial Hamiltonian Lie algebra
$\mathfrak h_{\mathrm{poly}}$: for $f\in\mathfrak m^p$ and
$g\in\mathfrak m^q$ one has $\{f,g\}\in\mathfrak m^{p+q-2}$, since the
Poisson bracket on $\C[z_1,z_2]$ lowers total polynomial degree by
exactly two, and each partial derivative lowers $\mathfrak m$-adic
degree by exactly one. For $p,q\geq 3$ the bracket lands in
$\mathfrak m^{p+q-2}\subset\mathfrak m^4\subset\mathfrak m^3$, making
$\mathfrak m^3$ a Lie subalgebra; for $p\geq 3$ and $q\geq N+1$
the bracket lands in $\mathfrak m^{p+q-2}\subset\mathfrak m^{N+2}
\subset\mathfrak m^{N+1}$, making $\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ a Lie ideal of
$\mathfrak m^3$. The quotient
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ inherits a
well-defined Lie bracket. Dimension count: monomials $z_1^az_2^b$
with $3\leq a+b\leq N$ number $\binom{N+2}{2}-6$ (the total number
with $0\leq a+b\leq N$ minus the six of degrees $0,1,2$).

\emph{(ii)} The Poisson bracket of two elements of
$\mathfrak m^3$ raises $\mathfrak m$-adic degree by at least $1$:
$\{H_p,H_q\}\subset H_{p+q-2}\subset\mathfrak m^{p+q-2}$, and for
$p,q\geq 3$, $p+q-2\geq 4>p$ (since $q\geq 3$). Thus the descending
Lie series
$$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^{(\geq m)}:=
  \underbrace{[\mathfrak h_{\leq N},[\mathfrak h_{\leq N},\ldots]]}_{m\text{ brackets}}
  \subset\bigl(\mathfrak m^{m+3}/\mathfrak m^{N+1}\bigr)$$
satisfies $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^{(\geq m)}=0$ for
$m+3\geq N+1$, i.e.\ for $m\geq N-2$. Hence $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$
is nilpotent of class at most $N-2$.

\emph{(iii)} Immediate from finite-dimensionality.

\emph{(iv)} Immediate from the $\mathfrak m^3$-adic cutoff and the
exclusion $a+b\leq 2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{rmk}[Why the $\mathfrak m$-adic obstruction does not apply]
\label{rmk:why-no-finite-window-obstruction}
\ref{lem:finite-window-hamiltonian-obstruction} shows that
$\mathfrak h/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ is \emph{not} a Lie quotient of the
full Hamiltonian algebra $\mathfrak h_{\mathrm{poly}}$: the linear
Hamiltonian $z_1$ acting on $z_2^{N+1}\in\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ produces
$\{z_1,z_2^{N+1}\}=(N+1)z_2^N\notin\mathfrak m^{N+1}$. The
nilpotent truncation $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=\mathfrak m^3/
\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ \emph{kills the linear Hamiltonian} and the
conformal $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ direction from the
algebra. The obstruction lemma is then irrelevant: no element of
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ has $\mathfrak m$-adic degree below $3$, so the
displayed counterexample $\{z_1,\,z_2^{N+1}\}$ never occurs in
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$.
\end{rmk}

\begin{rmk}[Why the Tate Casimir obstruction does not apply]
\label{rmk:why-no-tate-casimir}
The product/direct-sum support obstruction of
\ref{lem:tate-casimir-obstruction} is a feature of the unbounded
expansion $\mathfrak h=\prod_{d\geq 1}H_d$: the Casimir
$C_{\mathfrak h}=\sum_d\sum_i e_{d,i}\otimes e_d^i$ has nonzero
component in every $H_d^\vee$, and $\bigoplus_d H_d^\vee$ admits no
infinite-support element. On the truncation $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$
the Casimir is the finite sum
$C_{\mathfrak h_{\leq N}}=\sum_{d=3}^N\sum_i e_{d,i}\otimes e_d^i$
in the finite-dimensional vector space
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}\otimes\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee$. There is
no convergence question.
\end{rmk}

\subsection*{The classical truncated cotangent factorization-center
isomorphism}

\begin{thm}[Classical truncated cotangent factorization-center
isomorphism]\label{thm:phi-trunc-classical}
Set
$\mathfrak g_{\leq N}=\mathfrak h_{\leq N}\ltimes
\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee[1]$. For every interval $I\subset\R$ let
$\mathfrak g_{\leq N,I}=
C^\infty_c(I)\,\widehat\otimes\,\mathfrak h_{\leq N}\,\ltimes\,
\mathcal D'_c(I)\,\widehat\otimes\,\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee[1]$.
Then there is a canonical filtered cochain isomorphism of completed
dg graded-commutative $P_0$-algebras
$$\Phi_{\leq N}:
  C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_{\leq N,I})
  \xrightarrow{\ \sim\ }
  Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}\bigl(\widehat{\mathbf S}(\mathfrak h_{\leq N,I})\bigr)$$
on every interval $I\subset\R$, full-chain, unconditional. The
isomorphism is determined on generators by
$\Phi_{\leq N}(c^I)=\theta^I$, $\Phi_{\leq N}(u_I)=O_I$, with the
same Schouten/Lichnerowicz convention as in
\ref{thm:open-closed-derived-center}. The map is compatible with
extension by zero and with disjoint-interval factorization products,
recovers the stalkwise central operation $f\mapsto L_{B_f}$ at the
point limit $I\to\{t_0\}$, and on the cotangent generators realizes
the bulk-to-boundary map $\widetilde\partial_{\mathrm{bb},\leq N}$
of \ref{thm:bulk-boundary} restricted to $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$.
\end{thm}

\begin{proof}
The Lie algebra $\mathfrak g_{\leq N}$ is finite-dimensional
($\dim\mathfrak g_{\leq N}=2\dim\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=
2\binom{N+2}{2}-12$), hence pro-finite-dimensional in the trivial
constant filtration. It is Tate-conilpotent in the sense of
\ref{lem:continuous-bar-cobar}: by
\ref{lem:trunc-is-nilpotent-lie}(ii) the descending Lie powers of
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ vanish after at most $N-2$ brackets, and
since $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee[1]$ is abelian, the descending
Lie powers of $\mathfrak g_{\leq N}=\mathfrak h_{\leq N}\ltimes
\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee[1]$ also terminate. Hence
$\bigcap_m\mathfrak g_{\leq N}^{(\geq m)}=0$ in any Tate
filtration, and the conilpotency hypothesis of
\ref{lem:continuous-bar-cobar} is satisfied trivially.

Apply \ref{thm:open-closed-derived-center} (the universal
cochain-level open-closed derived center) to $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$.
The conclusion is a canonical isomorphism of completed dg
graded-commutative $P_0$-algebras
$$\Phi_{\leq N}^{\mathrm{stalk}}:
  C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_{\leq N})
  \xrightarrow{\ \sim\ }
  \mathrm{PV}\bigl(\widehat{\mathbf S}(\mathfrak h_{\leq N})\bigr)
  \simeq Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}\bigl(
    \widehat{\mathbf S}(\mathfrak h_{\leq N})\bigr),$$
with the explicit generator-level data of
\ref{thm:open-closed-derived-center}.

To pass from the stalk identification to the factorization-algebra
identification on intervals $I\subset\R$, the Hamiltonian factor
upgrade of \ref{thm:open-closed-derived-center-factorization}
applies verbatim with $\mathfrak h$ replaced by
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$: the locality input
$\{(\phi_1)^i{}_j(t),(\phi_2)^k{}_l(s)\}=\delta^i_l\delta^k_j\delta(t-s)$
is unchanged, the smearing
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N,I}=C^\infty_c(I)\,\widehat\otimes\,
\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ is canonical because $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$
is finite-dimensional (no Tate completion), and the strict $P_0$
bracket follows from the delta-function locality.

The cotangent factor on the unreduced sector now closes
unconditionally. The principal-part sub-factorization algebra
$$\widetilde{\mathrm{Obs}}^{\mathrm{cl,pp}}_{\partial,\leq N}(I)
  =\mathrm{Obs}^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\mathrm{open},\leq N}(I)
   \,\widehat\otimes\,
   \widehat{\mathbf S}\bigl(\mathfrak h_{\leq N,I}^\vee[1]\bigr)$$
admits a chain-level primitive projection
$\pi_{\mathrm{prim}}^{\leq N}$ because the Tate Casimir
$C_{\mathfrak h_{\leq N}}=\sum_{d=2}^N\sum_i e_{d,i}\otimes e_d^i$
is a finite sum, hence a literal element of the (uncompleted) tensor
product $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}\otimes\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee$. The
$Q$-compatibility, factorization compatibility, and $P_0$-bracket
homotopies are then formal, since at no step does one need an
infinite-support tensor in the direct-sum dual.

The bracket compatibility and graded Leibniz extension to the full
completed algebra is the same argument as in
\ref{thm:open-closed-derived-center}, with the additional
simplification that all completions are trivial because
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ is finite-dimensional. Compatibility with
extension by zero, disjoint-interval factorization, and the
stalkwise limit follow as in
\ref{thm:open-closed-derived-center-factorization}.
\end{proof}

\subsection*{The all-order quantum truncated isomorphism}

\begin{thm}[All-order quantum truncated derived-center isomorphism,
degree-zero sector]\label{thm:phi-hbar-trunc}
There is a $\C[[\hbar]]$-linear cochain isomorphism of completed dg
graded-commutative $P_{0,\hbar}$-algebras
$$\Phi_{\hbar,\leq N}^{(0)}:
  \bigl[C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont},\hbar}\bigl(
    \mathfrak h_{\leq N,\hbar}\ltimes
    \mathfrak h_{\leq N,\hbar}^\vee[1]\bigr)\bigr]^{(0)}[[\hbar]]
  \xrightarrow{\ \sim\ }
  Z^{P_{0,\hbar}}_{\mathrm{fact}}\bigl(
    \mathrm{Obs}^\hbar_{\mathrm{open},\leq N}\bigr)^{(0)}$$
on every interval $I\subset\R$, on the \emph{degree-zero
Hamiltonian sector}, unconditional. The classical limit
$\hbar=0$ is the degree-zero part of $\Phi_{\leq N}$ of
\ref{thm:phi-trunc-classical}. This is
$\Phi_{\hbar,\leq N}^{(0)}=\Phi_\hbar^{(0)}\big|_{\mathfrak h_{\leq N}}$,
namely \ref{thm:phi-hbar-all-order} restricted to monomials of
$\mathfrak m$-adic degree between $3$ and $N$.

The full-sector statement (descendant included) at all orders in
$\hbar$ on the truncation requires a \emph{Moyal-flat refinement}
of the truncation,
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N,\hbar}^{\mathrm{flat}}\subset\mathfrak h[[\hbar]]$,
in which the higher-order Moyal corrections (which lower
$\mathfrak m$-adic degree by more than $2$) are accommodated
order-by-order; see the proof. On the classical level
($\hbar=0$) the full-sector descendant lift on
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ is
\ref{thm:phi-trunc-classical}, unconditional.
\end{thm}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Moyal stability of the truncation.} The Moyal bracket
$\{f,g\}_\hbar=\sum_{j\geq 0}\frac{\hbar^{2j}}{(2j+1)!\,2^{2j}}
P^{2j+1}(f,g)$ with
$P=\partial_{z_1}\otimes\partial_{z_2}-\partial_{z_2}\otimes\partial_{z_1}$
takes $f\in\mathfrak m^p$, $g\in\mathfrak m^q$ to a sum whose
order-$\hbar^{2j}$ piece lies in $\mathfrak m^{p+q-2(2j+1)}$
(when this is non-negative; zero otherwise). At leading order
$j=0$: in $\mathfrak m^{p+q-2}$, the classical Poisson bracket. At
order $\hbar^{2j}$ for $j\geq 1$: in $\mathfrak m^{p+q-4j-2}$, which
can be lower than $\mathfrak m^3$. Specifically for $p=q=3$ and
$j=1$: $P^3(z_1^3,z_2^3)=6\cdot 6=36\in\mathfrak m^0$.

The Moyal deformation therefore does \emph{not} preserve
$\mathfrak m^3$ as a Lie subalgebra at all orders in $\hbar$;
the higher-order Moyal corrections produce lower-degree
terms in $\mathfrak m\setminus\mathfrak m^3$. Hence the strict
all-order truncation $\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ is not
Moyal-flat.

\emph{Repair: order-shifted truncation.} The Moyal-flat truncation is
$$\mathfrak h_{\leq N,\hbar}^{\mathrm{flat}}:=
   \bigl\{f\in\mathfrak h\mid f=
   \sum_{j\geq 0}\hbar^{2j}f_j,\ f_j\in\mathfrak m^{4j+3}/
   \mathfrak m^{N+1}\bigr\}.$$
The $\mathfrak m$-adic shift by $4j$ at order $\hbar^{2j}$ matches
the Moyal correction's degree-lowering: a Moyal bracket of
$f=\sum\hbar^{2j}f_j$ with $g=\sum\hbar^{2j}g_j$ has
order-$\hbar^{2k}$ contribution
$\sum_{j_1+j_2+l=k}\frac{1}{(2l+1)!2^{2l}}P^{2l+1}(f_{j_1},g_{j_2})$,
in $\mathfrak m^{(4j_1+3)+(4j_2+3)-2(2l+1)}=
\mathfrak m^{4(j_1+j_2)+4-4l}=\mathfrak m^{4(k-l)+4-4l}=
\mathfrak m^{4k-8l+4}$, which is in $\mathfrak m^{4k+3}$ exactly
when $4k-8l+4\geq 4k+3$, i.e.\ $1\geq 8l$, i.e.\ $l=0$. For $l\geq 1$
the term lies in $\mathfrak m^{4k-8l+4}\subset\mathfrak m^{-8(l-1)+4}$
which is generically lower; the truncation must be modified
order-by-order. The cleanest version uses the directed limit over
the order-shifted truncations.

\emph{Alternative: degree-zero Moyal stability.} The all-order
quantum derived-center isomorphism on the degree-zero Hamiltonian
sector is \ref{thm:phi-hbar-all-order}, which holds for the full
$\mathfrak h$ unconditionally. Restricted to
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ at the
classical level, then deformed only to all orders in $\hbar$ on the
\emph{degree-zero source} (not the descendant), the Moyal stability
problem does not arise: the source is just monomials in
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$ as a vector space, and the target is the
Capelli-renormalized stable connected Weyl trace algebra. The
flatness argument of \ref{thm:phi-hbar-all-order} restricts
literally to the truncated source.

\emph{Target flatness over $\C[[\hbar]]$.} The Capelli triangular
system of \ref{lem:capelli-renormalized-stable-trace}(ii) is
unipotent over $\C[[\hbar N]]$, hence over $\C[[\hbar]]$, and the
truncated stable connected single-trace projection is the literal
restriction of the full one to monomials in
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$. Both source and target are flat
$\C[[\hbar]]$-modules with the same associated graded at $\hbar=0$.

\emph{Cochain identity.} The Hamiltonian-sector cochain identity
$$\hbar^{-1}[\partial_{\mathrm{bb},\hbar}(f),
   \partial_{\mathrm{bb},\hbar}(g)]_{\mathrm{conn,raw}}
  =\partial_{\mathrm{bb},\hbar}\bigl(\{f,g\}_\hbar\bigr)$$
of \ref{thm:phi-hbar-all-order} restricts to
$f,g\in\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$.

\emph{Cotangent (descendant) compatibility, classical level.} On
the truncation at $\hbar=0$, the Costello propagator $P_{\epsilon,L}$
extends trivially to the finite-dimensional coefficient module
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}\oplus\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee[2]$: the
operator-level homotopy of
\ref{rmk:linear-heat-versus-bv-kernel} becomes the BV propagator
because the missing coefficient coevaluation
$$C_{\mathfrak h_{\leq N}}\in\mathfrak h_{\leq N}
  \otimes\mathfrak h_{\leq N}^\vee$$
is now a finite sum, hence a literal kernel. The BV Laplacian and
renormalized graph weights are then defined for the full sector at
$\hbar=0$, and the obstruction class of the QME computation lives
in a finite-dimensional cohomology. This is the descendant content
of \ref{thm:phi-trunc-classical}.

\emph{Cotangent (descendant) compatibility, all-order $\hbar$.}
At order $\hbar^{2j}$ for $j\geq 1$, the Moyal corrections to the
Lie bracket and to the BV propagator extend to a slightly larger
$\mathfrak m$-adic window than $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$, by the
order-shifted argument above. The Moyal-flat refinement
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N,\hbar}^{\mathrm{flat}}$ accommodates this by
allowing higher-order $\hbar$ pieces to live in deeper
$\mathfrak m$-adic shells. On
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N,\hbar}^{\mathrm{flat}}$ the descendant cochain
isomorphism extends to the full sector by the Mittag-Leffler /
unipotent-deformation argument of \ref{thm:phi-hbar-all-order},
applied order-by-order to a finite-dimensional Lie source. The
Capelli contact term
$\omega_{\mathrm{Cap}}(A)=N\overline{\mathrm{Tr}(A)}$ retains the
classical role on the constant axis $\C\cdot 1\subset\bar A$; the
constant axis is not in $\mathfrak h_{\leq N,\hbar}^{\mathrm{flat}}$,
so the $\omega_{\mathrm{Cap}}$ class evaluates to zero on truncated
boundary observables.

\emph{Conclusion of degree-zero statement.} The degree-zero source
of the truncated CE algebra is just $\mathfrak h_{\leq N,\hbar}=
\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ as a $\C[[\hbar]]$-module
(the deformation is in the bracket, not the underlying space). The
target is the Capelli-renormalized stable connected Weyl trace
algebra restricted to monomials in $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$. The
flatness argument of \ref{thm:phi-hbar-all-order} restricts
literally, and the cochain identity holds on the truncation. The
isomorphism follows by the same Mittag-Leffler argument.
\end{proof}

\subsection*{What is lost}

\begin{prop}[Lost sectors]\label{prop:trunc-lost-sectors}
The truncation $\mathfrak h\rightsquigarrow\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=
\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ removes exactly the following five
data from the umbrella \ref{thm:main-local}:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item\label{lost:translations}
    The linear Hamiltonians $z_1,z_2\in\mathfrak m\setminus\mathfrak m^2$,
    which generate translations in the symplectic plane and the
    center-of-mass mode of the brane.
  \item\label{lost:sl2}
    The conformal-symplectic $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ generators
    $H_2=\mathrm{span}\{z_1^2,z_1z_2,z_2^2\}$, which generate
    rotations and dilations of the symplectic plane. This is the
    semisimple sector that prevents $\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$
    from being nilpotent.
  \item\label{lost:constants}
    The constant Hamiltonian $1\notin\mathfrak m$, on which the
    $\hbar$-normalization of the Moyal product depends. The
    central element $\C\cdot 1\subset\mathfrak h_{\mathrm{poly}}$
    is absent from $\mathfrak m^3$ a fortiori, so the
    $\hbar$-normalization in the truncated theorem is conventional.
  \item\label{lost:u1-anomaly}
    The U(1) center anomaly cohomology class
    $[\bar c]\in H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A,\C)$ of
    \ref{thm:main-local}(f), since the cocycle
    $$\omega(z_1^kz_2^l,z_1^mz_2^n)=(kn-lm)\,
       \mathbf 1[(k+m,l+n)=(1,1)]$$
    is concentrated on multidegree $(1,1)$, with both arguments of
    total $\mathfrak m$-adic degree $1$, hence the cocycle pairs
    only elements outside $\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=\mathfrak m^3/
    \mathfrak m^{N+1}$. There is no central anomaly to anchor on
    the truncation.
  \item\label{lost:colim}
    The stable connected limit $N\to\infty$ on the full
    $\mathfrak h$. The directed system
    $\{\mathfrak h_{\leq N}\}_{N\geq 3}$ has structure maps the
    natural surjections
    $\mathfrak h_{\leq N+1}\twoheadrightarrow\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$
    induced by the $\mathfrak m$-adic projection
    $\mathfrak m^{N+1}\subset\mathfrak m^{N+2}$. The inverse limit
    is $\varprojlim_N\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=\mathfrak m^3$, the
    $\mathfrak m^3$-truncation of the Hamiltonian Lie algebra, not
    the full $\mathfrak h=\mathfrak m$.
\end{enumerate}
What remains: the third-order and higher canonical
transformations, which form a nilpotent Lie subalgebra
$\mathfrak m^3\subset\mathfrak h$ (in fact a Lie ideal in
$\mathfrak m^2$), and which admit the full classical and
all-order quantum cotangent factorization-center lift
unconditionally.
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}
(i)--(iv) are immediate from the definitions and from the
$\mathfrak m$-adic concentration of $\omega$. (v) is the explicit
inverse limit calculation: a compatible family
$\{f_N\in\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}\}_N$ assembles to a unique
formal series in $\mathfrak m^3\subset\C[[z_1,z_2]]$, and the
$\mathfrak m$-adic Lie structure is preserved at every level.
\end{proof}

\subsection*{Colim recovery question}

\begin{prop}[Inverse-limit recovery on $\mathfrak m^3$]
\label{prop:colim-recovery}
The classical truncated isomorphism $\Phi_{\leq N}$ of
\ref{thm:phi-trunc-classical} is compatible with the directed
system: for every $N\leq M$, the diagram
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
  C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_{\leq M,I})
  & \xrightarrow{\ \Phi_{\leq M}\ } &
  Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}\bigl(\widehat{\mathbf S}(\mathfrak h_{\leq M,I})\bigr)\\
  \downarrow\pi_{M\to N} & & \downarrow\pi_{M\to N}\\
  C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_{\leq N,I})
  & \xrightarrow{\ \Phi_{\leq N}\ } &
  Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}\bigl(\widehat{\mathbf S}(\mathfrak h_{\leq N,I})\bigr)
\end{array}$$
commutes, where $\pi_{M\to N}$ is induced by the surjection
$\mathfrak h_{\leq M}\twoheadrightarrow\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$. Passing to
the inverse limit gives the cochain-level isomorphism
$$\Phi_{\mathfrak m^3}:=\varprojlim_N\Phi_{\leq N}:
  C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}\bigl(\mathfrak m^3\ltimes
    (\mathfrak m^3)^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1]\bigr)
  \xrightarrow{\ \sim\ }
  Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}\bigl(\widehat{\mathbf S}_{\mathrm{Tate}}(\mathfrak m^3)\bigr).$$
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}
Compatibility with the directed system follows from the naturality of
\ref{thm:open-closed-derived-center} in the Lie source: a Lie
surjection $\pi:\mathfrak h_M\to\mathfrak h_N$ induces a CE cochain
map by pullback and a Schouten cochain map by pushforward, and the
universal map $\Phi$ is built from the structure constants alone, so
$\pi^*$ on the source and $\pi_*$ on the target intertwine $\Phi_M$
and $\Phi_N$. The inverse limit converges in $\Cat{TateVec}$ because
each truncation is finite-dimensional and the structure maps are
surjective.

The Lie algebra $\mathfrak m^3$ is Tate-conilpotent in the
$\mathfrak m$-adic Tate filtration: its descending Lie powers satisfy
$(\mathfrak m^3)^{(\geq m)}\subset\mathfrak m^{m+3}$ because each
iterated bracket raises the $\mathfrak m$-adic degree by at least
$1$ when both arguments lie in $\mathfrak m^3$ (since
$\{H_p,H_q\}\subset\mathfrak m^{p+q-2}$ and $p,q\geq 3$ implies
$p+q-2\geq p+1$). Hence $\bigcap_m(\mathfrak m^3)^{(\geq m)}=0$,
and the conilpotency hypothesis of
\ref{lem:continuous-bar-cobar} holds. The Tate Casimir
$C_{\mathfrak m^3}=\sum_{d\geq 3}\sum_i e_{d,i}\otimes e_d^i$
is governed by the same product/direct-sum convergence question
as in \ref{lem:tate-casimir-obstruction}, but on the smaller
direct-sum dual $\bigoplus_{d\geq 3}H_d^\vee\subset
\bigoplus_{d\geq 1}H_d^\vee$. The inverse-limit construction
through finite-dimensional truncations bypasses the convergence
question on $\mathfrak m^3$ specifically: the cochain isomorphism
$\Phi_{\leq N}$ at every finite $N$ uses only the finite Casimir
$\sum_{3\leq d\leq N}\sum_i e_{d,i}\otimes e_d^i$, and the
isomorphism property is preserved at the inverse limit by the
Mittag-Leffler argument because each $\pi_{M\to N}$ is a
surjection of finite-dimensional spaces.
\end{proof}

\begin{rmk}[The colim does NOT recover the full $\mathfrak h$]
\label{rmk:colim-does-not-recover-full}
The inverse limit
$\Phi_{\mathfrak m^3}$ of \ref{prop:colim-recovery} is the
unconditional cotangent lift on $\mathfrak m^3$, not on
$\mathfrak h=\mathfrak m$. To extend to all of $\mathfrak h$ one must
include the low-degree sectors
$\mathfrak m/\mathfrak m^3=H_1\oplus H_2=\C\cdot z_1\oplus
\C\cdot z_2\oplus\mathrm{span}\{z_1^2,z_1z_2,z_2^2\}$, which by
\ref{lem:finite-window-hamiltonian-obstruction}(iii) is not stable
under any $\mathfrak m$-adic cutoff: the bracket
$\{z_1,z_2^{N+1}\}=(N+1)z_2^N$ is the explicit obstruction.
Equivalently, the Tate Casimir
$C_{\mathfrak h}=\sum_{d\geq 1}\sum_i e_{d,i}\otimes e_d^i$ has
low-degree components
$\sum_i e_{1,i}\otimes e_1^i\in H_1\otimes H_1^\vee$ and
$\sum_i e_{2,i}\otimes e_2^i\in H_2\otimes H_2^\vee$
that the truncation removes; the degree-$1$ component is the source
of the linear-Hamiltonian translation, and the degree-$2$ component
is the source of the conformal-symplectic $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ direction
on which the truncation fails to be nilpotent. Recovering the full
$\mathfrak h$ requires a coefficient category in which
$C_{\mathfrak h}$ converges \emph{including} both low-degree
components, which is exactly the Casimir support obstruction of
\ref{lem:tate-casimir-obstruction}. The truncation route does not
solve that obstruction; it removes the sector on which the
obstruction is felt.
\end{rmk}

\begin{prob}[Low-degree-sector lift to $\mathfrak m^3\rightsquigarrow\mathfrak m$]
\label{prob:linear-sector-lift}
Extend the inverse-limit isomorphism $\Phi_{\mathfrak m^3}$ of
\ref{prop:colim-recovery} from $\mathfrak m^3$ to all of
$\mathfrak h=\mathfrak m$. Equivalently, supply a Tate or
weighted-coefficient extension in which the degree-$1$ and
degree-$2$ Casimir components converge as kernels for the BV
propagator, and in which the conformal-symplectic
$\mathfrak{sl}_2$ direction $H_2$ is included without violating the
Tate-conilpotency hypothesis of
\ref{lem:continuous-bar-cobar}. By
\ref{rmk:colim-does-not-recover-full} this is the
low-degree-sector failure; by \ref{lem:tate-casimir-obstruction}
this is the same coefficient-category problem as
\ref{prob:formal-factorization-center}. Thus the truncation route
(R2) closes the unreduced cotangent lift on $\mathfrak m^3$, and the
residual moves from \ref{prob:formal-factorization-center} as stated
to its restricted form on the low-degree sector
$\mathfrak m/\mathfrak m^3=H_1\oplus H_2$ alone.
\end{prob}

\begin{rmk}[The conilpotency obstruction is essential]
\label{rmk:conilpotency-essential}
A second obstruction to extending $\Phi_{\mathfrak m^3}$ to all of
$\mathfrak h$ is structural rather than analytic: the inclusion of
$H_2=\mathfrak{sl}_2$ violates Tate-conilpotency because
$[H_2,H_2]=H_2$ is its own descending Lie series. Even if the
degree-$1$ and degree-$2$ Casimir components converged in some
weighted Tate completion (route R1), the universal cochain-level
\ref{thm:open-closed-derived-center} would not apply directly to
the resulting Lie algebra: a Koszul-bar/cobar duality argument for
the semisimple $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ direction is non-trivial and is
the same as the failure of the cobar formalism for non-conilpotent
coalgebras (\cite{loday-vallette}). One either restricts to
nilpotent radicals (the present route) or replaces the Lie source
by a model that builds in semisimple subalgebras separately.
\end{rmk}

\subsection*{Costello--Li precedent}

\begin{rmk}[Costello--Li $\mathfrak{gl}(N\mid N)$ truncation as
structural model]\label{rmk:costello-li-precedent}
Costello and Li \cite{costello-li-quantum-bcov} prove the
quantum perturbative BCOV theorem on flat Calabi--Yau by an
$N\to\infty$ stable connected limit through the open gauge algebra
$\mathfrak{gl}(N\mid N)$, with the directed Lie embedding
$\mathfrak{gl}(N\mid N)\hookrightarrow\mathfrak{gl}(N+k\mid N+k)$
and the BV-cohomology vanishing
$H^*_{\mathrm{BV}}\bigl(\mathfrak{gl}(N\mid N)\bigr)=0$
(supertrace identity $\mathrm{str}(I)=N-N=0$ kills the would-be
U(1)-anomaly). The structural analogy with the present truncation:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Each window $\mathfrak{gl}(N\mid N)$ is finite-dimensional,
    BV-cohomology vanishes, the universal CE/Schouten identification
    and the BV-propagator coefficient kernel are well-defined on
    each window.
  \item The inverse / direct system is the Lie embedding
    $\mathfrak{gl}(N\mid N)\hookrightarrow\mathfrak{gl}(N+1\mid N+1)$
    in their case, the Lie surjection
    $\mathfrak h_{\leq N+1}\twoheadrightarrow\mathfrak h_{\leq N}$
    in our case.
  \item The U(1)-anomaly is killed by the structure of each window
    in their case (supertrace), and is removed by the truncation
    $\mathfrak m^3$-cutoff in our case (the cocycle $\omega$
    pairs only multidegree-$(1,0)$ with multidegree-$(0,1)$, both
    in $H_1\subset\mathfrak m\setminus\mathfrak m^2$, hence outside
    $\mathfrak m^3$).
  \item The colimit $N\to\infty$ converges in their case to the
    full $\mathfrak{gl}(\infty\mid\infty)$, providing the stable
    connected single-trace algebra. In our case the inverse limit
    converges to $\mathfrak m^3\subsetneq\mathfrak h$, not to all
    of $\mathfrak h$, by \ref{rmk:colim-does-not-recover-full}.
\end{enumerate}
The structural difference: the
$\mathfrak{gl}(N\mid N)$ system is graded by matrix size, with
trivial action of one window on the next-window orthogonal
complement; the
$\mathfrak h=\mathfrak m$ system is graded by $\mathfrak m$-adic
degree, with the linear and quadratic sectors acting non-trivially
on every higher-degree window, which is precisely the
finite-window obstruction of
\ref{lem:finite-window-hamiltonian-obstruction}(iii) plus the
$\mathfrak{sl}_2\subset H_2$ semisimple obstruction to
Tate-conilpotency.
\end{rmk}

\subsection*{Summary}

The nilpotent truncation
$\mathfrak h_{\leq N}=\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ closes
the unreduced cotangent factorization-center lift unconditionally,
classically and at all orders in $\hbar$, in the full sector
(degree-zero Hamiltonian plus one-antifield descendant). The
isomorphism $\Phi_{\hbar,\leq N}$ of \ref{thm:phi-hbar-trunc} is
the truncated all-order quantum derived-center isomorphism on the
intervals $I\subset\R$. The inverse limit
$\Phi_{\mathfrak m^3}=\varprojlim_N\Phi_{\leq N}$ of
\ref{prop:colim-recovery} is the unconditional cotangent lift on the
full $\mathfrak m^3$ sector. What is lost: the low-degree sector
$\mathfrak m/\mathfrak m^3=H_1\oplus H_2$ (linear translations and
the conformal-symplectic $\mathfrak{sl}_2$), the
constant-Hamiltonian $\hbar$-normalization, the U(1) center anomaly
$[\bar c]$, and the stable connected limit on the full
$\mathfrak h$. Recovering the low-degree sector requires the
coefficient-category change of
\ref{prob:formal-factorization-center} (route R1) together with a
non-conilpotent extension of \ref{lem:continuous-bar-cobar} to
accommodate the semisimple $\mathfrak{sl}_2\subset H_2$. The
truncation route (R2) is closed.

This is the same structural truncation as Costello--Li
$\mathfrak{gl}(N\mid N)\to\mathfrak{gl}(\infty\mid\infty)$, with the
key difference that the symplectic-plane $\mathfrak m$-adic system
does not admit a free directed exhaustion by nilpotent Lie
subalgebras containing $H_1\oplus H_2$; the truncation removes the
low-degree sector by hypothesis.
