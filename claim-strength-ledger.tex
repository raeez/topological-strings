\textbf{Formal-local theorem data.}
The formal-local interface is attached to
the shifted cotangent Hamiltonian Lie algebra
\[
  \mathfrak h=\C[[z_1,z_2]]/\C\cdot1,\qquad
  \mathfrak g=\mathfrak h\ltimes
    \mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1].
\]
Its visible interface is stratified by proof strength:
\[
\begin{gathered}
  \text{finite cotangent CE/PV, proved}\\
  \Downarrow\\
  \text{coordinate formal-disk CE/PV, proved as dg algebra}\\
  \Downarrow\\
  \text{bracket-admissible \(P_0\) realizations, proved after \(B\)}\\
  \Downarrow\\
  \text{kernel-admissible MC kernels and Tate bar-cobar, conditional}.
\end{gathered}
\]
The reduced CE/PV central-operation generator map is fixed once and for all:
\[
  c^I\longmapsto\theta^I,\qquad u_I\longmapsto O_I .
\]
The admissible interface datum is
\[
  \mathfrak U_{\mathfrak h}
  =(\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}},\{\Phi^B_{P_0}\}_B,\{\Theta_B\}_B),
\]
where \(B\) ranges over bracket-admissible subalgebras in the second
component and kernel-admissible data in the third.  This is not a
categorical universal object unless a category of admissible completions
and a universal property are supplied.  The theory is
extended by the primitive higher-\(\psi\) extraction, the
\(\mathcal D_\hbar\) Fourier--Rees bridge, the weighted finite-window
regulator, the endpoint criteria, and the unreduced factorization-current
lift datum.

\smallskip
\textbf{Admissibility principle.}
A frontier extension is admissible only when a target theorem supplies
the matched-conventions datum and proves vanishing, with null-homotopies,
of the period, anomaly, QME, convergence, hyperdescent, six-relation,
descent, and operadic obstruction vector.

\paragraph{Admissibility stages.}
Stage A0 is the finite cotangent CE/PV identity.  Stage A1 is the
coordinate formal-disk CE/PV identity \(\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}\), proved
as a completed dg commutative algebra statement.  Stage A2 is a
bracket-admissible \(P_0\)-realization
\(\Phi^B_{P_0}\colon\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}^{-1}(B)\to B\).  Here
\(B\) is an explicitly topologized complete Hausdorff subalgebra with
continuous inclusion in the coordinate product, continuous
multiplication, continuous \(d_\pi^{\mathrm{coord}}\), and a continuous
closed Schouten contraction bracket whose transport to
\(\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}^{-1}(B)\) is again continuous.  Boundary
Hamiltonian uses also require \(B\) to contain the relevant \(O_f\)'s and
their forced differentials \(d_\pi^{\mathrm{coord}}O_f\).  Stage A3 is a
kernel-admissible Maurer--Cartan kernel \(\Theta_B\), conditional on a
specified completed convolution dg Lie algebra \(\mathcal K_B\) in which
the diagonal sums converge.
Stage A4 is an admissible Tate or relative bar-cobar/Quillen envelope.
Stage A5 is the stable scalar-reduced Dirac trace comparison.  Stage A6
is the conditional radial, one-antifield, and Costello/QME quantum
realization.  Stage A7 is any external matched-conventions descent
problem; it is not a consequence of the local stages.

\paragraph{Koszul comparison layers.}
Layer 0 is the operadic Lie/coComm Koszul input
\(\mathrm{Lie}^{!}\simeq\mathrm{coComm}[-1]\).  Layer 1 is the
Tate CE/bar-cobar identity
\(\Omega C^{\mathrm{CE}}_*(\mathfrak g)\simeq U(\mathfrak g)\) only in an
admissible lower-central Tate-pronilpotent envelope.  Layer 2 is the corresponding Tate
Quillen equivalence.  Layer 3 is the coordinate CE/PV reduced \(P_0\)
central-operation theorem with \(c^I\mapsto\theta^I\) and
\(u_I\mapsto O_I\).  Layer 4 is the PBW/Rees special-fibre shadow
\[
  U_\hbar(\mathfrak g)
  =
  T(\mathfrak g)[[\hbar]]/
  \langle xy-(-1)^{|x||y|}yx-\hbar[x,y]\rangle,\qquad
  U_\hbar(\mathfrak g)/(\hbar)\cong
  S(\mathfrak h\oplus\mathfrak h^\vee[1]).
\]
Layer 5 is the Matlis/Grothendieck cotangent module
\[
  \mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}\cong
  \mathcal P=\bigoplus_{a+b>0}\C\rho_{a,b},\qquad
  z_1^pz_2^q\cdot\rho_{a,b}
  =-(pb-qa+p-q)\rho_{a-p+1,b-q+1}.
\]
Layer 6 is the brane-line factorization-current comparison
\[
  \mathfrak h_I=\Omega^\bullet_c(I)\widehat\otimes\mathfrak h,\qquad
  u_{a(t)dt\otimes f}\longmapsto B_f(a).
\]
Layer 7 is the stable large-\(N\) bulk-boundary horizon; it is
conjectural here and not used to prove the local Hamiltonian BF/Moyal
core.

\paragraph{Theorem data.}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \emph{Stage A0: finite cotangent CE/PV.}
  For each finite-dimensional Lie algebra \(\mathfrak l\),
  \[
    \Phi_{\mathfrak l}\colon
    C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE}}
      (\mathfrak l\ltimes \mathfrak l^\vee[1])
    \xrightarrow{\sim}
    \mathrm{PV}(\mathbf S(\mathfrak l))
  \]
  with \(c\mapsto\theta\), \(u\mapsto O\).  The formal-disk coordinate
  comparison is reconstructed from admissible finite coordinate tests and
  pro-window coordinate cochains.  It becomes a completed bracketed
  statement only in a mixed or weighted topology where the contraction
  bracket is continuous.  The strict ambient product/direct-sum
  completion is obstructed by the infinite diagonal.

  \item \emph{Stage A1: coordinate formal disk CE/PV.}
  The completed formal-disk comparison is first a coordinate dg algebra
  isomorphism
  \[
    \Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}\colon
    C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{coord}}(\mathfrak g)
    \longrightarrow
    \mathrm{PV}_{\mathrm{coord}}(A_{\partial,\mathrm{coord}}).
  \]
  It becomes a \(P_0\) comparison only after choosing a
  bracket-admissible subalgebra \(B\).  If \(B\) is kernel-admissible, the
  diagonal kernel \(\Theta_B\) is an additional conditional datum.
  Its coordinate transition obstruction terms are
  \[
    \mathfrak o^{\mathrm{br}}_{M,N},\qquad
    \mathfrak o^{\mathrm{coad}}_{M,N}.
	  \]
	  The separate strict-kernel and ambient-product bracket obstruction is
	  \(\mathfrak o^{\mathrm{Cas}}_{M,N}\).

  \item \emph{Stage A2: bracket-admissible \(P_0\) realization.}
  A bracket-admissible \(B\subset
  \mathrm{PV}_{\mathrm{coord}}(A_{\partial,\mathrm{coord}})\) is complete,
  stable under \(d_\pi^{\mathrm{coord}}\), closed under multiplication, and closed under a
  continuous Schouten contraction bracket.  Its source
  \(\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}^{-1}(B)\) must be closed under the transported
  bracket.  Under these hypotheses
  \[
    \Phi^B_{P_0}\colon\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}^{-1}(B)
    \xrightarrow{\sim}B
  \]
  is the completed dg \(P_0\)-comparison.

  \item \emph{Stage A3: kernel-admissible Maurer--Cartan tensor.}
  A bracket-admissible \(B\) is kernel-admissible when the diagonal sums
  \[
    \Theta_B=\sum_I H_I\otimes\theta^I+\sum_I\eta^I\otimes O_I
  \]
  converge in the completed tensor product attached to \(B\).  Then
  \(\Theta_B\) is the corresponding Maurer--Cartan tensor, checked
  coefficientwise from the Jacobi and coadjoint identities after the
  diagonal sums converge:
  \[
    d\Theta_B+\frac12[\Theta_B,\Theta_B]=0.
  \]
  The strict product/direct-sum endpoint has no such kernel without an
  additional coefficient replacement.

	  \item \emph{Stage A4: admissible Tate bar-cobar/Quillen envelope.}
  For an admissible Tate replacement
  \(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{adm}}\),
  \[
    B_{\mathrm{adm}}=
    C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{adm}}),
    \qquad
    B_{\mathrm{adm}}^!\simeq
    \widehat U(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{adm}}).
  \]
  The envelope hypotheses are conilpotent CE coalgebra, PBW
  completeness, completed symmetric associated graded, and an admissible
  transferred \(P_0\)-algebra model structure.

  \item \emph{Reduced CE/PV central-operation generator map.}
  The Hamiltonian CE ghost \(c^I\) becomes the Schouten vector
  \(\theta^I\).  The shifted-cotangent CE coordinate \(u_I\) becomes the
  Hamiltonian boundary coordinate \(O_I\).  Boundary Hamiltonian
  observables come from \(u_I\).

  \item \emph{Dirac open Koszul model.}
  The brane stack is the derived commuting-pair locus:
  \[
    S_\partial=\int_\R\operatorname{Tr}
    (\phi_1\,d\phi_2+A[\phi_1,\phi_2]),\qquad
    Q\psi=[\phi_1,\phi_2].
  \]
  The displayed action is in the Dirac-normalized variables obtained from
  the raw shifted-Chern--Simons coefficient fields; the Koszul complex is
  the derived zero fibre of the commuting-pair equation, not an assertion
  that the finite-\(N\) moment-map components form a regular sequence.
  Stable trace invariants are taken degreewise; for total word degree
  \(d\), the stable range is \(N\ge d\).

  \item \emph{Primitive higher-\(\psi\) extraction.}
  The primitive Koszul complex has nonconstant Hamiltonian \(H_0\)
  classes and positive Hamiltonian one-\(\psi\) primitive \(H_1\)
  classes \(\Psi_{a,b}\) with \(a+b>0\).  The scalar
  \(\Psi_{0,0}=\operatorname{Tr}(\psi)\) is the removed odd scalar line,
  not a Matlis principal-part class.  The higher primitive sectors
  satisfy \(H_p=0\) for \(p\ge2\) in the stable finite-bidegree cyclic
  word model.  The
  one-\(\psi\) action is
  \[
    \{\overline{\operatorname{Tr}(\phi_1^p\phi_2^q)},
      \widetilde\Psi_{a,b}\}
    =(pb-qa)\widetilde\Psi_{a+p-1,b+q-1}.
  \]

  \item \emph{Matlis cotangent module.}
  The cotangent coefficient module is
  \[
    \mathcal P=
    \operatorname{Ann}_{\mathrm{res}}(\C\cdot1)
    \subset H^2_{\mathfrak m}(R)\,dz_1dz_2,
    \qquad
    \rho_{a,b}=z_1^{-a-1}z_2^{-b-1}dz_1dz_2 .
  \]
  Its coadjoint action is
  \[
    z_1^pz_2^q\cdot\rho_{a,b}
    =-(pb-qa+p-q)\rho_{a-p+1,b-q+1}.
  \]

  \item \emph{Polynomial-descendant obstruction.}
  Polynomial one-\(\psi\) representatives have locally nilpotent linear
  Hamiltonian action, while
  \[
    z_1\cdot\rho_{a,b}=-(b+1)\rho_{a,b+1},\qquad
    z_2\cdot\rho_{a,b}=(a+1)\rho_{a+1,b}
  \]
  is not locally nilpotent on principal parts.  Any polynomial
  realization of \(\mathcal P\) must therefore change the target, the
  action, or the category.

  \item \emph{\(\mathcal D_\hbar\) Fourier--Rees bridge.}
  With
  \[
    \mathcal D_\hbar=
    \C[[\hbar]]\langle z_i,\partial_i\rangle/
    ([\partial_i,z_j]=\hbar\delta_{ij}),
  \]
  the Fourier delta lattice gives
  \[
    z_1^az_2^b\mapsto\partial_1^a\partial_2^be_0
    \mapsto(-1)^{a+b}\hbar^{a+b}a!b!\rho_{a,b}.
  \]
  Before inverting \(\hbar\) the image is the Rees sublattice
  \(\bigoplus\hbar^{a+b}\C[[\hbar]]\rho_{a,b}\).  The bridge changes
  polarization; same-action Rees families from \(\Psi_{a,b}\) to
  \(\rho_{a,b}\) are obstructed.

  \item \emph{Quantum Hamiltonian and Moyal sector.}
  The Weyl convention is
  \[
    f*g=m\exp\!\left(\frac{\hbar}{2}P\right)(f\otimes g),
    \qquad
    \{f,g\}_\hbar=\hbar^{-1}[f,g]_* .
  \]
  The first and third raw commutator coefficients are \(1\) and
  \(1/24\).  The descendant sector is governed by the principal-part
  Moyal target and the no-\(\hbar\)-adic same-action intertwiner.

  \item \emph{Weighted finite-window regulator.}
  Admissible weights \(w,w'\) are related by diagonal finite-window
  transport \(R_{w,w'}^{w_0}\).  The regulator theorem identifies the
  Mittag-Leffler local-functional complexes and carries
  \(\mathrm{Ob}_w\) to \(\mathrm{Ob}_{w'}\).  Vanishing of the mixed
  brane-defect QME class is independent of the admissible weight.

  \item \emph{Endpoint criteria.}
  An endpoint for \(w_q(d)=q^d\) is admissible when conditions
  (E1)--(E5) hold.  The obstruction tuple is
  \[
    \mathfrak o_{\mathrm{BV,end}}=
    (\mathfrak o_{\mathrm{Cas}},
     \mathfrak o_{\mathrm{def}},
     \mathfrak o_{\mathrm{RG}},
     \mathfrak o_{\mathrm{QME}}).
  \]
  The strict product/direct-sum endpoint fails because no tensor
  projects to all finite-window Casimirs \(C_{\mathfrak h}^{w_0}\).

  \item \emph{Unreduced factorization-current lift.}
  Distributional principal-part currents give the target for cotangent
  boundary modes.  The lift datum consists of boundary representatives,
  product centrality homotopies, \(P_0\)-bracket homotopies, the
  coefficient kernel, the brane-defect propagator, and the QME solution.
  The scalar component is
  \[
    \operatorname{Ob}_{\mathrm{sc}}
    =\hbar N\,\omega(f,g)
      \int_\R a(t)b(t)\gamma_{\mathbf 1}(t)\,dt ,
	  \]
	  with associated graded formal class
	  \(\hbar N[\bar c]\in H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A;\C)[[\hbar]]\).
	  In the original scalar-reduced ordinary \(\mathfrak{gl}_N\) model,
	  closed-exchange data must supply the opposite leading class.  The
	  central-character and balanced-supertrace mechanisms instead replace the
	  source or open model before the reduced scalar class forms.

  \item \emph{Global and cross-volume descent.}
  A target theorem supplies a matched-conventions datum
  \(\mathfrak C_{\mathrm{tar}}\).  The admissibility vector is
  \[
    \operatorname{Ob}_{\mathfrak C_{\mathrm{tar}}}
    =
	    (\operatorname{per}_{\mathrm{tar}},
	     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{WR}},
	     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{anom}},
	     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{QME}},
	     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{conv}},
	     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{hyp}},
	     \operatorname{ob}_{6r},
	     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{MD}}).
  \]
  It must vanish, with compatible null-homotopies, before any compact
  BCOV, Vol III, Igusa/BKM, or global Calabi--Yau consequence is deduced
  from the formal-local theorem.
\end{enumerate}
