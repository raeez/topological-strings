\textbf{Claim a reader might infer} & \textbf{Status here} &
\textbf{Replacement statement}\\
\hline
\endhead
Full \(Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}\) theorem for the open brane algebra &
\emph{not asserted} &
The proved object is the reduced CE/PV Hamiltonian central-operation
model. The unreduced factorization-centre morphism and centrality
homotopies are open.\\
\hline
Full unreduced BV identification &
\emph{not asserted} &
Only the finite ghost-zero Dirac/Koszul model and reduced
principal-part extensions are used.\\
\hline
Conilpotent Koszul duality for the full formal Hamiltonian algebra &
\emph{not asserted} &
Bar-cobar arguments apply only under the stated Tate-conilpotent,
nilpotent, or admissible-envelope hypotheses. The full formal
Hamiltonian algebra is treated by direct CE/PV computation and by
conditional replacements.\\
\hline
Bare \(R\)-submodule Matlis annihilator wording &
\emph{not asserted} &
The object is the \(\C\)-linear residue annihilator
\(\operatorname{Ann}_{\mathrm{res}}(\C\cdot1)\) inside top local
cohomology, with the Hamiltonian coadjoint action.\\
\hline
Unconditional finite-\(N\) radial-parts theorem &
\emph{conditional on named QME/radial-parts/unweighted-regulator theorem} &
The formal Moyal coefficient theorem is algebraic. The finite-\(N\)
comparison uses the named Harish-Chandra--Wallach--Levasseur--Stafford
radial-parts input.\\
\hline
Normalized-Moyal vanishing beyond the Poisson bracket &
\emph{not asserted} &
The raw star commutator has only odd powers. The normalized bracket
\(\hbar^{-1}[f,g]_*\) generally has even powers
\(\hbar^{2s}P^{2s+1}\), including a possible \(\hbar^2P^3/24\) term.\\
\hline
Unconditional Costello realization from reduced Wick diagrams &
\emph{not asserted} &
The paper computes reduced open-line Weyl/Moyal boundary coefficients
to first and third order. A Costello graph realization requires the
analytic brane-defect graph/QME package.\\
\hline
Exactly three QME mechanisms &
\emph{not asserted} &
The manuscript lists known candidate mechanisms. Exhaustiveness would
require a computation of the relevant \(H^1\) obstruction complex.\\
\hline

\subsection*{Claim ledger}

\begin{longtable}{@{}>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.24\textwidth}
  >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.27\textwidth}
  >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.40\textwidth}@{}}
\hline
\textbf{Claim} & \textbf{Status} & \textbf{Scope and exclusions}\\
\hline
\endhead
Dirac brane probe &
\emph{proved in finite algebraic model} &
Finite-\(N\) constrained mechanics on the matrix probe. The BV/Koszul
variable satisfies \(Q\psi=[\phi_1,\phi_2]\).\\
\hline
Stable Hamiltonian trace sector and \(\Psi_{a,b}\) labels &
\emph{proved degreewise stable} &
Stable connected range only. The classes \(\Psi_{a,b}\) are primitive
one-\(\psi\) PBW special-fibre classes; they are not principal-part
cotangent modes and do not realize \(\mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}\).\\
\hline
CE/PV reduced Hamiltonian central-operation model &
\emph{proved degreewise stable} &
Cochain-level identity for
\(\mathfrak h\ltimes\mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1]\), with
\(c^I\mapsto\theta^I\) and \(u_I\mapsto O_I\). The conilpotent
bar-cobar statement is used only inside its stated Tate-conilpotent
hypothesis. This is not the full unreduced factorization-centre theorem.\\
\hline
Principal-part cotangent module &
\emph{proved degreewise stable} &
Residue and Matlis/local-cohomology identification
\(\mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}\cong
\operatorname{Ann}_{\mathrm{res}}(\mathbb C\cdot1)\subset
H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\mathbb C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2\). Polynomial
descendants do not realize the coadjoint module. The Fourier--Rees
bridge is only an associated-graded label comparison.\\
\hline
Brane-line factorization currents &
\emph{proved degreewise stable} &
Compactly supported de Rham-current convention
\(\mathfrak h_I=\Omega^\bullet_c(I)\widehat\otimes\mathfrak h\). The
length-one source of the boundary Hamiltonian observable is the
shifted-cotangent coordinate \(u_{a(t)dt\otimes f}\).\\
\hline
Reduced principal-part boundary sector &
\emph{proved degreewise stable} &
Reduced defect-current model after de Rham-current contraction only. This is not an
unreduced open observable algebra.\\
\hline
Primitive one-\(\psi\) projection &
\emph{proved degreewise stable} &
Indecomposable \(P_0\) shadow of the reduced comparison. It does not
construct the unreduced cotangent factorization-centre morphism or its
centrality homotopies.\\
\hline
Degree-zero Moyal/Weyl quantization &
\emph{proved in finite algebraic model} &
Scalar-reduced degree-zero Hamiltonian sector, with the Capelli/Weyl
normalization and formal Moyal commutator, modulo the distinguished
scalar anomaly line \([\bar c]\). No one-antifield descendant lift and
no graph realization are included.\\
\hline
Weighted Tate regulator &
\emph{proved in weighted Tate coefficient model} &
Internal coefficient-kernel and finite-scale graphwise regulator.
Independence of the admissible finite-window weight is proved; equivalence
with the strict unweighted product/direct-sum graph theory is not asserted.
The mixed brane-defect QME is separate.\\
\hline
Strict unweighted product/direct-sum regulator endpoint &
\emph{not asserted} &
The positive endpoint theorem is impossible under the Costello
coefficient-kernel and finite-window compatibility hypotheses:
Theorem~\ref{thm:strict-unweighted-no-go} proves that no tensor in the
strict product/direct-sum pair projects to all finite-window Casimirs.
Tail-sensitive ordinary cohomology of the weighted product is also not
identified with the strict direct-sum dual.\\
\hline
Scalar \(U(1)\) anomaly &
\emph{proved in finite algebraic model} &
Closed polynomial scalar cocycle, finite-\(N\) open centre, and
Capelli/Weyl scalar shift. It records the obstruction to unreduced
lifting; it is not a compact Calabi--Yau, BKM, Igusa, or sister-volume
transfer.\\
\hline
First and third Costello graph normalizations &
\emph{computed to stated order} &
Only the first and third graph normalizations are claimed. They match
the formal Moyal coefficients in the stated normalization.\\
\hline
All-order Costello graph realization &
\emph{conditional on named QME/radial-parts/unweighted-regulator theorem} &
Requires a mixed brane-defect QME theorem, the all-\(N\) radial-parts
source input, any unweighted-regulator comparison if the strict
product/direct-sum theory is used, and a centrality-homotopy theorem in
the same normalization.\\
\hline
Quantum descendant lift &
\emph{reduced target constructed; unreduced lift open} &
Construction~\ref{constr:quantum-principal-part-descendant-target}
constructs the reduced Moyal principal-part target.  The unreduced
cotangent factorization-centre morphism is not constructed here, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:no-hbar-primitive-descendant-intertwiner} rules out
strict \(\hbar\)-adic deformations of the classical
\(\Psi_{a,b}\mapsto\rho_{a,b}\) projection with PBW linear leading
action.\\
\hline
Stable bulk--boundary Koszul duality &
\emph{conjectural} &
Strategic large-\(N\) horizon only. It is not used to prove the local
Hamiltonian BF/Moyal core.\\
\hline
Cross-volume consequences &
\emph{not asserted} &
No compact Calabi--Yau, global BCOV, Vol III, chiral-homology,
Borcherds, BKM, Igusa, \(K3\times E\), or sister-volume theorem
follows from this local Hamiltonian BF/Moyal calculation without a
separate matched-conventions proof.  Lemma~\ref{lem:no-formal-disk-transfer}
and Lemma~\ref{lem:admissibility-not-globalization} state the
non-transfer claim; they are firewalls, not export theorems.  The
local theorem may be cited only as a \(d=2\) formal-disk normalization
after the target volume supplies its own global datum and proof.\\
\hline
\end{longtable}
