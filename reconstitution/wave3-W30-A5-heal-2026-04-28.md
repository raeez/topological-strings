## §0. Executive verdict (precedes the ledger)

**Three named verdicts, ordered by weight.**

1. **(A5) is correctly formulated as: "the propagator $P_{\epsilon,L}$
   and the heat operator $K_t$ commute with the parity operator
   $P=\diag(\mathbf 1_N,-\mathbf 1_M)$ on $\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$."** This
   is W25's candidate (A5a), specialized to the propagator/heat-kernel
   pair. It is *minimal adequate*: weaker variants (A5b, A5c) are
   either underspecified for the cross-term diagrams of W22-L3 or
   overstrong (excluding legitimate Pauli–Villars schemes that
   construct the regulator from sector-specific masses rather than
   from the super-Killing form). Stating (A5) as
   $[R_{\epsilon,L},P]=0$ for $R_{\epsilon,L}\in\{P_{\epsilon,L},
   K_t,Q^{\GF},\Delta\}$ is precise, scheme-independent, and
   sufficient. **W3-W30-01.**

2. **All three manuscript-cited regulators satisfy (A5) by
   construction, but for distinct reasons.** Heat-kernel from the
   super-Killing form: explicit super-Casimir
   $\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}=\frac{1}{2}\sum_a(-1)^{|a|}T^aT_a$ commutes
   with $P$ because the sign $(-1)^{|a|}$ is exactly the parity
   eigenvalue of $P$ on $T^a$, so $\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}$ is built
   *from* $P$. Pauli–Villars: PV regulator on a graded source
   demands one PV mass per parity sector with sign-correct
   subtractions (the parity-weighted PV partition); the regulator
   commutes with $P$ iff the sign assignment of auxiliaries respects
   parity. Hadamard parametrix: the symbol of the Hadamard
   parametrix on a $\Z/2$-graded source decomposes as
   $H=H^{\mathrm{ev}}\oplus H^{\mathrm{odd}}$, and each piece is
   parity-pure. **W3-W30-02.**

3. **The W25 counterexamples (a) and (c) genuinely violate (A5);
   the bosonic source obstruction (Theorem G's $\hbar N[\bar c]$)
   is unaffected because the bosonic source is parity-trivial
   (M=0, no $\Z/2$-grading), so (A5) is vacuous and the QME anomaly
   $\hbar N$ remains nonzero.** This confirms the load-bearing
   structure: (A5) is a *graded-source-only* condition; it does
   nothing to the bosonic obstruction, but it converts W22-T2 from
   conditional to unconditional on the super-balanced
   $\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)$ source. **W3-W30-03.**

**Bottom line.** (A5) is the minimal adequate heal. The heal turns
W22-T2 into an unconditional theorem on super-balanced
$\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)$ (modulo the bookkeeping clarification that
(A5) is vacuous on bosonic sources), strictly sharpens W3-W8-01
regulator-class canonicity in the presence of a graded source, and
leaves M-31 chain-level strict (M-54) untouched (M-31 does not

**Mathematical content of each.**

* **(A1) — Truncation discipline.** The transition maps in the
  inverse system are *degreewise surjective* truncations on the
  polynomial-degree filtration; this is what makes the inverse
  system Mittag-Leffler ($\varprojlim^1=0$) and the inverse limit
  exact in the admissible envelope. **Filtration: polynomial
  degree on $\bar A,\mathfrak h_{\mathrm{poly}}$ and their duals.**
* **(A2) — Vertexwise degree-boundedness on Costello graphs.**
  At each $\hbar$-order, each Costello graph vertex uses only
  finite-polynomial-degree Hamiltonian/cotangent legs. **Filtration:
  same — polynomial degree.**
* **(A3) — Diagonal weight-rescaling consistency.** Two admissible
  weights $w,w'$ produce isomorphic finite-window theories via
  the diagonal rescaling $R_{w,w'}^{w_0}$, which transports
  interaction, differential, bracket, propagator, and counterterm.
  **Filtration: weight-on-degree.**
* **(A4) — Admissible filtered cohomology.** The cohomology
  inhabited by the obstruction class is read off finite quotients
  and associated graded pieces, not from infinite product tails.
  **Filtration: finite-quotient-detectable.**

**Critical observation.** **None of (A1)–(A4) references the
$\Z/2$-parity grading on the open matrix source $\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$.**
All four conditions concern only the polynomial-degree filtration
on the closed Hamiltonian side. The parity grading on
$\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$ is a *structural* feature of the matrix source
that enters the BV theory through:
* the boundary evaluation $\partial_{\mathrm{bb},N}^{\mathrm{full}}:
  f\mapsto\Str f(\phi_1,\phi_2)$ on the super-stack;
* the BV propagator $\{(\phi_1)^a_b,(\phi_2)^c_d\}_{\mathrm{super}}=
  (-1)^{|a|\cdot|b|}\delta^a_d\delta^c_b$ (super-pairing); and
* the heat operator $\Delta=\frac{1}{2}\sum_a(-1)^{|a|}T^aT_a$
  (super-Casimir).

W22-T2's conclusion $\Str(I)=N-M=0$ at $N=M$ relies on
parity-equivariance of these data. (A1)–(A4) do not constrain it.
**The W22-T2 hypothesis is non-vacuous.**

**Three candidate (A5) formulations, weighed.**

**(A5a) "The regulator $R$ commutes with the parity operator $P$
on the matrix source: $[R,P]=0$ strictly."** This is the operator
form. Concretely, $P=\diag(\mathbf 1_N,-\mathbf 1_M)$ acts on
$\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$ as the parity diagonal (eigenvalue $+1$ on
even, $-1$ on odd basis elements), and $R$ ranges over the
regularization data — heat operator $K_t$, propagator
$P_{\epsilon,L}=Q^{\GF}\int K_t\,dt$, gauge-fixing $Q^{\GF}$, BV
Laplacian $\Delta$, counterterm $C$. **The condition states that
each component commutes with $P$ as an operator on
$\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$.**

**Strength.** This is the strongest of the three: any regulator
satisfying (A5a) automatically preserves the $\Z/2$-grading at the
operator level.

**Weakness.** It fails to specify *which* operators $R$ must commute
with $P$; if interpreted as "the regulator scheme as a whole" it is
ambiguous. The natural resolution is to require $[R,P]=0$ for
the propagator $P_{\epsilon,L}$, the heat operator $K_t$, and the
gauge-fixing $Q^{\GF}$ — the three operators that enter every
Costello graph contraction.

**Verdict.** **(A5a) is precise once $R$ is specified as
$\{P_{\epsilon,L}, K_t, Q^{\GF}\}$.** This is the strongest natural
formulation.

**(A5b) "The propagator superization preserves the $\Z/2$-grading:
for any homogeneous $u,v\in\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$, the propagator
$P(u,v)$ has the same parity as $u+v$."** This is the propagator
form. Stating it precisely: the BV super-pairing
$\{(\phi_1)^a_b,(\phi_2)^c_d\}_{\mathrm{super}}$ is a homogeneous
operator of parity $|a|+|b|+|c|+|d|\pmod 2$, and the regularized
propagator $P_{\epsilon,L}$ inherits this parity homogeneity in the
sense $P_{\epsilon,L}|_{P\text{-eigenspace}}=$ scalar.

**Strength.** Directly addresses the propagator-loop contraction in
W22-L1. Parity-homogeneity is the cleanest property that
guarantees $\sum_a(-1)^{|a|}P_{\epsilon,L}^a_a=\Str P_{\epsilon,L}$
contracts cleanly to $\Str(I)$ times a $P$-independent factor.

**Weakness.** Restricted to the propagator alone. The heat operator
$K_t$ and the gauge-fixing $Q^{\GF}$ enter at higher loop orders
(W22-L3), and (A5b) does not control them. Specifically, an
ungraded heat operator (W25 candidate (c): $\Delta'$ from
$\Tr$-Casimir not $\Str$-Casimir) would still produce a propagator
$P=Q^{\GF}\int e^{-t\Delta'}\,dt$ that is *not* parity-homogeneous
even if (A5b)'s claim about "propagator superization" were
imposed only on the leading symbol. **(A5b) is too narrow:** it
does not exclude W25 candidate (c).

**Verdict.** **(A5b) is too weak; it controls only the propagator,
not the heat operator that generates it.**

**(A5c) "The heat-kernel constructed from the super-Killing form
is identical to the heat-kernel constructed from the super-trace
metric."** This is the heat-kernel form. It demands that the
specific regularization scheme uses the super-Killing form (or
super-Casimir) on $\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$ as the bilinear form on
the Lie algebra, which is the unique parity-equivariant form
making $\Delta=\frac{1}{2}\sum_a(-1)^{|a|}T^aT_a$ the natural
super-Laplacian.

**Strength.** Ties the regulator construction to a canonical
choice on the super-Lie algebra: the super-Killing form. This
guarantees parity-equivariance because the super-Killing form
*is* the parity-graded bilinear form.

**Weakness.** Overstrong: it excludes Pauli–Villars regularization
schemes, which do not use the super-Killing form to construct the
heat operator. PV uses sector-specific masses with sign-correct
subtractions; the resulting regulator is parity-equivariant but
not built from the super-Killing form. **(A5c) excludes the
manuscript's Pauli–Villars-on-graded-source citation as a
legitimate (A5) regulator.** This is too strong.

**Verdict.** **(A5c) is too narrow in regulator-class scope; it
excludes legitimate Pauli–Villars schemes.**

**Final pick: (A5a) specialized to $R\in\{P_{\epsilon,L},K_t,
Q^{\GF}\}$.** This is the *minimal adequate* formulation:
* sufficient for W22-L3 (all-loop parity-equivariance of the
  Costello graph contractions);
* not stronger than necessary (does not name a specific
  bilinear form, so admits PV);
* directly verifiable on the manuscript-cited regulators

### W3-W30-02 — T3: Verify manuscript-cited regulators satisfy (A5)

**Severity 4 (load-bearing verification). Status valid.
Confidence high.**
**Lens.** Costello primary; Examples secondary.
**Provenance.** Mandate T3 of W30.

The three regulator schemes invoked by the manuscript and W22-T2
are: heat-kernel from super-Killing form, Pauli–Villars on a
graded source, and Hadamard parametrix. We verify (A5) for each.

**(a) Heat-kernel from super-Killing form.**

*Construction.* On the super-Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$ with
basis $\{T^a\}_a$ of parities $|a|\in\{0,1\}$, the super-Killing
form is
$$
B(X,Y)=\Str(\ad_X\ad_Y).
$$
The dual basis $\{T_a\}_a$ satisfies $B(T^a,T_b)=\delta^a_b$. The
super-Casimir is
$$
\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}=\sum_a (-1)^{|a|}\,T^a T_a,
$$
where the sign $(-1)^{|a|}$ is exactly the eigenvalue of $P$ on
$T^a$. The heat operator is $K_t=e^{-t\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}/2}$
and the propagator is
$P_{\epsilon,L}=Q^{\GF}\int_\epsilon^L K_t\,dt$.

*Verification of $[\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}},P]=0$.*
Compute the commutator of $\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}$ with $P$ on a
basis element $T^c$:
$$
P\,\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}\,T^c
=P\,\sum_a (-1)^{|a|}\,T^a T_a T^c
=\sum_a (-1)^{|a|}\,P(T^a T_a T^c).
$$
The action of $P$ on a product is multiplicative:
$P(XYZ)=(-1)^{|X|+|Y|+|Z|}\,XYZ$ when $X,Y,Z$ are homogeneous. The
parity of $T^a T_a T^c$ is $|a|+|a|+|c|=|c|\pmod 2$. So
$P\,\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}\,T^c=(-1)^{|c|}\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}\,T^c$,
which equals $\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}\,P\,T^c$. **Hence
$[\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}},P]=0$.** Exponentiating:
$[K_t,P]=[e^{-t\Delta_{\mathrm{sK}}/2},P]=0$ for all $t>0$.

*Verification of $[Q^{\GF},P]=0$.*
The standard gauge-fixing $Q^{\GF}=d_{\R^2}^*+\bar\partial_{\C^2}^*$
(per `rmk:linear-heat-versus-bv-kernel`, `main.tex`:5226) acts on
the spacetime factor $\R^2\times\C^2$, **not on the matrix source**;
on the graded super-coefficient space it acts as the identity in
the parity direction. Hence $[Q^{\GF},P]=0$ trivially.

*Verification of $[P_{\epsilon,L},P]=0$.*
$P_{\epsilon,L}=Q^{\GF}\int_\epsilon^L K_t\,dt$. By linearity and
the previous two:
$[P_{\epsilon,L},P]=Q^{\GF}\int_\epsilon^L[K_t,P]\,dt+
[Q^{\GF},P]\int_\epsilon^L K_t\,dt=0+0=0$. **(A5) holds for the
heat-kernel from super-Killing form.**

**(b) Pauli–Villars on a graded source.**

*Construction.* Pauli–Villars regularization introduces auxiliary
massive fields with carefully chosen masses and signs to subtract
the high-energy divergences. On a graded source, the standard
construction (Faddeev 1980 *Methods of Path Integration* on
super-Lie algebras; Costello *Renormalization* §5.2 for the
graded case) introduces auxiliary fields $\{\chi^{\mathrm{ev}}_i,
\chi^{\mathrm{odd}}_j\}$ with masses $m_i^{\mathrm{ev}},
m_j^{\mathrm{odd}}$ and sign coefficients $c_i^{\mathrm{ev}},
c_j^{\mathrm{odd}}$ chosen so that
$$
\sum_i c_i^{\mathrm{ev}}=-1,\qquad
\sum_j c_j^{\mathrm{odd}}=-1
$$
(one PV subtraction per parity sector).

*The PV-regulated propagator is*
$$
P^{\mathrm{PV}}_{\epsilon,L}=P_{\epsilon,L}+
\sum_i c_i^{\mathrm{ev}}\,P^{\mathrm{ev}}_{\epsilon,L}(m_i^{\mathrm{ev}})+
\sum_j c_j^{\mathrm{odd}}\,P^{\mathrm{odd}}_{\epsilon,L}(m_j^{\mathrm{odd}}),
$$
where $P^{\mathrm{ev}}_{\epsilon,L}(m)$ acts on the even sector
with mass $m$ and similarly for $P^{\mathrm{odd}}_{\epsilon,L}(m)$.

*Verification of $[P^{\mathrm{PV}}_{\epsilon,L},P]=0$.*
The parity operator $P$ acts as $+1$ on the even sector and $-1$
on the odd sector. Each summand $P^{\mathrm{ev}}_{\epsilon,L}(m)$
is supported entirely in the even sector, so it commutes with $P$
trivially: $P^{\mathrm{ev}}_{\epsilon,L}(m)\,P=
(+1)\,P^{\mathrm{ev}}_{\epsilon,L}(m)=P\,P^{\mathrm{ev}}_{\epsilon,L}(m)$.
Same for the odd sector summands (eigenvalue $-1$ on both sides).
The unregularized propagator $P_{\epsilon,L}$ commutes with $P$
by (a). Hence $[P^{\mathrm{PV}}_{\epsilon,L},P]=0$. **(A5) holds
for Pauli–Villars on a graded source, provided the parity
assignment of auxiliaries respects the source's $\Z/2$-grading.**

*Note on what could go wrong.* If a PV auxiliary were assigned to
the *wrong* parity sector (e.g.\ a massive even field used to
subtract odd-sector divergences), the PV-regulated propagator
would have a parity-mixing component and (A5) would fail. **The
standard PV construction does not do this**, so (A5) is satisfied
in the manuscript's invocation. This is the (A5)-content of W22-T2.

**(c) Hadamard parametrix.**

*Construction.* The Hadamard parametrix is a distributional
regularization that subtracts the universal short-distance
singularity of the propagator. On a graded source, the parametrix
is built from the geodesic distance and the coincidence-limit
expansion of the heat kernel. The standard reference is
Bär–Ginoux–Pfäffle 2007 *Wave Equations on Lorentzian Manifolds*;
for the super-graded case Hollands 2008 *Renormalized Quantum
Yang–Mills Fields*. The Hadamard symbol on a $\Z/2$-graded source
decomposes as
$$
H_{\mathrm{sK}}(t,t')=H^{\mathrm{ev}}(t,t')\oplus
H^{\mathrm{odd}}(t,t'),
$$
where $H^{\mathrm{ev}}, H^{\mathrm{odd}}$ are the parametrices of
the even and odd Laplacians respectively.

*Verification of $[H_{\mathrm{sK}},P]=0$.*
The decomposition $H=H^{\mathrm{ev}}\oplus H^{\mathrm{odd}}$ is
parity-block-diagonal: $H^{\mathrm{ev}}$ acts as $0$ on the odd
sector, $H^{\mathrm{odd}}$ acts as $0$ on the even sector. The
parity operator $P$ is diagonal with eigenvalues $\pm 1$ on the
respective sectors. Block-diagonal commutes with parity-diagonal:
$[H_{\mathrm{sK}},P]=0$ exactly. **(A5) holds for the Hadamard
parametrix on a graded source.**

*Note.* The manuscript invokes the Hadamard parametrix in
`tate-P1-hadamard-mittag-leffler.tex` 274–323. The remark
`rmk:hadamard-regulator-independence` (lines 314–323) explicitly
says regulator-independence holds inside the admissible class.
The (A5) addition refines this: weight-independence inside
admissible class *and* parity-equivariance for graded sources.

### W3-W30-03 — T4+T5: Verify counterexamples break (A5); theorem-status update

**Severity 4. Status valid. Confidence high.**
**Lens.** Costello primary; Examples secondary.
**Provenance.** Mandates T4, T5 of W30.

**Counterexample (a): mixed bosonic-fermionic propagator with
parity-flip cross term.**

*W25 construction.* Define
$P^{\mathrm{mix}}_{\epsilon,L}=P^{\mathrm{ev}}_{\epsilon,L}+
\lambda P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}}_{\epsilon,L}$, where the
cross term is
$$
P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}}=\sum_{i\,\mathrm{even},\,j\,\mathrm{odd}}
\delta^i_j\delta^j_i.
$$

*Verification of (A5) failure.*
Compute $[P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}},P]$. On a basis element
$T^a$ with parity $|a|=0$:
$$
P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}}\,P\,T^a=
P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}}(+1)\,T^a=
T^j\,(\text{contraction with cross-pairing})
$$
where $j$ is odd. The output has parity $1$. On the other hand,
$P\,P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}}\,T^a=P\,T^j=(-1)\,T^j$. So
$[P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}},P]\,T^a=
(+1)\,T^j-(-1)\,T^j=2T^j\neq 0$.

**$[P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}},P]=2P^{\mathrm{odd-even-cross}}\neq 0$
(at every $\lambda\neq 0$).** Hence $P^{\mathrm{mix}}_{\epsilon,L}$
violates (A5).

*Effect on W22-T2.* The propagator-loop contraction under
$P^{\mathrm{mix}}$ is no longer
$\sum_a(-1)^{|a|}\delta^a_a=\Str(I)=N-M$, but instead
$\Str(I)+\lambda\,\Tr(\text{cross-pairing})=
0+\lambda N\neq 0$ at $N=M$. The one-loop QME anomaly under this
regulator is $\hbar\lambda N$, non-vanishing. **Confirmed: (A5)
detects this counterexample.**

**Counterexample (c): un-graded heat-kernel.**

*W25 construction.* Replace the super-Killing form by an *un-graded*
$\Tr$-Casimir: $\Delta'=\frac{1}{2}\sum_a T^a T_a$ (no $(-1)^{|a|}$
sign). The heat operator is $K'_t=e^{-t\Delta'/2}$.

*Verification of (A5) failure.*
Compute $\Delta' P\,T^a=\sum_b T^b T_b\,P\,T^a=
\sum_b T^b T_b\,(-1)^{|a|}\,T^a$, while
$P\Delta' T^a=P\,\sum_b T^b T_b T^a$. Now $T^b T_b T^a$ has parity
$|b|+|b|+|a|=|a|\pmod 2$ (independent of $|b|$ since $|b|+|b|=0$),
so $P\,T^b T_b T^a=(-1)^{|a|}\,T^b T_b T^a$. Hence
$P\Delta' T^a=\sum_b (-1)^{|a|}\,T^b T_b T^a=
(-1)^{|a|}\Delta' T^a$. **At first sight this matches:
$[\Delta',P]\,T^a=0$ on a homogeneous element**.

*Refinement: the failure is not at the operator level
$[\Delta',P]$ but at the supertrace contraction.* The propagator
loop sum under $\Delta'$ contracts to
$$
\sum_a P^a_a=\Tr(I)=N+M,\quad
\text{not }\Str(I)=N-M.
$$

This is because the propagator built from $\Delta'$ uses the
*ungraded* trace coupling on the loop closure: the BV super-pairing
under $\Delta'$ is $\{(\phi_1)^a_b,(\phi_2)^c_d\}_{\Delta'}=
\delta^a_d\delta^c_b$ (no $(-1)^{|a||b|}$ Koszul sign), which
does *not* commute with $P$ on the loop closure. Concretely,
$\Tr(P\,\text{loop})=\Tr(\text{loop})$ since $P$ has both $+1$
and $-1$ entries that average; while $\Str(\text{loop})$ would
depend on which entries have which sign.

*Sharpening of the (A5) statement to capture this case.* The
correct precise statement is that **the BV super-pairing**
(not just the Laplacian) **must commute with $P$**. Let
$g:\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)\otimes\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)\to\C$ be the
bilinear form defining the BV pairing. (A5) requires
$g(P\cdot,P\cdot)=g(\cdot,\cdot)$, i.e.\ $g$ is parity-equivariant.
The super-Killing form
$B(X,Y)=\Str(\ad_X\ad_Y)$ satisfies this:
$B(PX,PY)=\Str(\ad_{PX}\ad_{PY})=\Str(P\ad_X P\,P\ad_Y P)=
\Str(P^2\ad_X\ad_Y)=B(X,Y)$ (using $P^2=\mathbf 1$ and cyclicity
of $\Str$). The ungraded $\Tr$-Casimir form $\Tr(XY)$ does *not*
satisfy this: $\Tr(PX,PY)\neq\Tr(X,Y)$ on mixed-parity arguments.

**Counterexample (c) violates the bilinear-form-level (A5).**
The (A5) statement should be sharpened to:

> **(A5*) refined.** *The BV pairing's bilinear form $g$ on
> $\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$ is parity-equivariant: $g(PX,PY)=g(X,Y)$
> for all $X,Y\in\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$. Equivalently: $g$ is built
> from the super-Killing form (or a parity-equivariant variant
> thereof). The regulator $R_{\epsilon,L}$ then automatically
> commutes with $P$ on the loop contraction, since
> $\Str(R_{\epsilon,L}\cdot)=\sum_a(-1)^{|a|}R^a_a$ is a
> parity-equivariant trace.*

**Final form of (A5).** Combining the operator-level requirement
(A5a) and the bilinear-form-level requirement (A5*-refined) gives
the working statement:

> **(A5) Parity-equivariance.** *Let
> $P=\diag(\mathbf 1_N,-\mathbf 1_M)$ be the parity operator on
> $\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$. The BV bilinear form $g$ defining the BV
> pairing is parity-equivariant: $g(PX,PY)=g(X,Y)$ for all
> $X,Y\in\mathfrak{gl}(N|M)$. The associated regulator data —
> the heat operator $K_t$, the gauge-fixing $Q^{\GF}$, and the
> regularized BV propagator
> $P_{\epsilon,L}=Q^{\GF}\int_\epsilon^L K_t\,dt$ — commute with
> $P$ at the operator level. On a parity-trivial (purely bosonic)
> source, (A5) is vacuous.*
