## Controlled Reduction Hypotheses

A reduced Dirac BRST/Zhu theorem requires a reduction package
\[
  \mathcal R_{\mathbb C\times\mathbb R}
  =
  (\pi,\kappa_{\mathrm{top}},\kappa_{\mathrm{hol}},
    B_{z_2},\pi_!,L_{\mathrm{red}},
    \langle-,-\rangle_{\mathrm{red}},
    \{-,-\}_{\mathrm{red}},
    H_{\mathrm{anom}}).
\]
The package must contain the following data.

1. A projection
   \[
      \pi:\mathbb R_t\times\mathbb R_s\times
      \mathbb C_{z_1}\times\mathbb C_{z_2}
      \longrightarrow \mathbb R_t\times\mathbb C_{z_1},
   \]
   with contraction kernels \(\kappa_{\mathrm{top}}\) and
   \(\kappa_{\mathrm{hol}}\) implementing the pushforward.  These
   kernels must identify which modes are killed and which modes survive.

2. A retained \(z_2\)-coefficient or principal-part system
   \(B_{z_2}\).  A minimal formal choice is
   \[
      \mathfrak h_{\mathrm{red}}
      =
      \mathbb C[[z_1]][[z_2]]/\mathbb C\cdot 1
   \]
   or the corresponding residue/principal-part completion used by the
   manuscript's \(P_0\) construction.  Setting \(z_2=0\) is not allowed
   for the theorem: it collapses the Hamiltonian bracket and destroys the
   bracket data whose zero modes later become the Weyl relation.

3. A brane image
   \[
      L_{\mathrm{red}}=\mathbb R_t\times\{z_1=0\}
   \]
   together with the pushed-forward BV pairing and the reduced
   Hamiltonian bracket.  The reduced pairing must be residue-normalized
   so that the vertex OPE coefficient below agrees with the Weyl
   normalization in `main.tex:6385-6448`.

4. An anomaly homotopy \(H_{\mathrm{anom}}\) comparing the pushed-forward
   BV/QME anomaly with the one-dimensional BRST anomaly.  This is the
   point that keeps the curve construction from becoming a false native
   theorem on \(\mathbb C^2\).

5. A conformal weight convention
   \[
      \operatorname{wt}(Z_1)=0,\qquad
      \operatorname{wt}(Z_2)=1,\qquad
      \operatorname{wt}(c)=0,\qquad
      \operatorname{wt}(b)=1.
   \]
   These weights are part of the Zhu theorem surface; without them the
   zero-mode map is not defined.

6. A finite-\(N\) gauge convention.  The scalar in
   \(\mathfrak{gl}_N\) acts trivially by conjugation, so the effective
   moment-map reduction is \(\mathfrak{sl}_N\) or
   \(\mathfrak{pgl}_N\), matching `main.tex:6426-6438`.  It is harmless
   to write the BRST fields as \(\mathfrak{gl}_N\)-valued provided that
   the scalar ghost is either quotiented out or treated as a decoupled
   acyclic factor.

## Free Dirac Vertex Algebra

After the above reduction, let \(z=z_1\) be the curve coordinate.  Define
\(V_N^{\mathrm{Dir}}\) to be the free vertex algebra generated by
matrix-valued fields
\[
  Z_1{}^i{}_j(z),\quad Z_2{}^i{}_j(z),\quad
  c^i{}_j(z),\quad b^i{}_j(z).
\]
The fields \(Z_1,Z_2\) are even.  The ghost \(c\) is odd of ghost degree
\(+1\), and \(b\) is odd of ghost degree \(-1\).  The singular OPEs are
\[
  Z_1{}^i{}_j(z)\,Z_2{}^k{}_\ell(w)
  \sim
  \frac{\hbar\,\delta^i_\ell\delta^k_j}{z-w},
  \qquad
  b^i{}_j(z)\,c^k{}_\ell(w)
  \sim
  \frac{\delta^i_\ell\delta^k_j}{z-w},
\]
with all other generating singular OPEs regular.  The bracket used below
is the matrix supercommutator; for \(Z_1,Z_2\) it is the ordinary matrix
commutator.

The moment-map current is
\[
  \mu=[Z_1,Z_2],
\]
and the BRST current is
\[
  j_{\mathrm{BRST}}
  =
  \operatorname{Tr}\bigl(c[Z_1,Z_2]\bigr)
  +\frac12\operatorname{Tr}\bigl(b[c,c]\bigr).
\]
Let
\[
  Q_{\mathrm{BRST}}=\operatorname{Res}_{z=0} j_{\mathrm{BRST}}(z)\,dz .
\]
With the above sign convention, the induced differential is
\[
  QZ_a=[c,Z_a]\quad (a=1,2),\qquad
  Qc=\frac12[c,c],
\]
\[
  Qb=[Z_1,Z_2]+[b,c].
\]
The reduced chiral Dirac algebra is
\[
  \mathcal A_{\partial,N}^{\mathrm{Dir\text{-}ch}}
  =
  H^\bullet(V_N^{\mathrm{Dir}},Q_{\mathrm{BRST}}).
\]

## Square-Zero Check

The proof that \(Q_{\mathrm{BRST}}^2=0\) is the finite-dimensional
BRST/Jacobi computation.  It must be included in the manuscript if this
theorem surface is promoted.

First, the moment map is equivariant:
\[
  Q\mu
  =
  Q[Z_1,Z_2]
  =
  [[c,Z_1],Z_2]+[Z_1,[c,Z_2]]
  =
  [c,[Z_1,Z_2]]
  =
  [c,\mu].
\]
Therefore
\[
  Q^2Z_a
  =
  \frac12[[c,c],Z_a]-[c,[c,Z_a]]
  =
  0
\]
by the graded Jacobi identity, and
\[
  Q^2c
  =
  \frac12\bigl([Qc,c]-[c,Qc]\bigr)
  =
  \frac14[[c,c],c]-\frac14[c,[c,c]]
  =
  0.
\]
For \(b\),
\[
  Q^2b
  =
  Q\mu+Q[b,c]
  =
  [c,\mu]+[Qb,c]-[b,Qc].
\]
Substituting \(Qb=\mu+[b,c]\) and \(Qc=\frac12[c,c]\) gives
\[
  Q^2b
  =
  [c,\mu]+[\mu,c]+[[b,c],c]
     -\frac12[b,[c,c]]
  =
  0,
\]
again by equivariance and Jacobi.  In OPE language this is the statement
that the singular part of \(j_{\mathrm{BRST}}(z)j_{\mathrm{BRST}}(w)\)
has no residue after the chosen normal-ordering and anomaly convention.
The anomaly convention is part of
\(\mathcal R_{\mathbb C\times\mathbb R}\).

## Zero Modes and the Dirac Koszul Complex

Let
\[
  \phi_a=Z_{a,0},\qquad \psi=b_0.
\]
After taking gauge invariants and restricting to the Koszul part in
which the gauge ghost zero mode is removed, the differential reduces to
\[
  Q\phi_1=Q\phi_2=0,\qquad
  Q\psi=[\phi_1,\phi_2].
\]
This is the local Dirac brane complex
\[
  R_N=\mathbb C[\phi_1,\phi_2]\otimes \Lambda(\psi)
\]
from `main.tex:118-144`.  Thus the reduced curve BRST algebra is a
chiral lift of the already present Dirac Koszul model, not a replacement
for the native mixed \(E_2\) object.

## Zhu Algebra and Weyl Relation

With weights
\[
  \operatorname{wt}(Z_1)=0,\quad
  \operatorname{wt}(Z_2)=1,\quad
  \operatorname{wt}(c)=0,\quad
  \operatorname{wt}(b)=1,
\]
the Zhu zero mode of a weight \(r\) field \(a\) is
\[
  o(a)=a_{r-1}.
\]
Use the transpose-compatible matrix convention
\[
  X^j{}_i=o(Z_1{}^i{}_j),\qquad
  Y^j{}_i=o(Z_2{}^i{}_j),\qquad
  C^j{}_i=o(c^i{}_j),\qquad
  B^j{}_i=o(b^i{}_j).
\]
The OPE gives the Zhu relations
\[
  [Y^j{}_i,X^\ell{}_k]
  =
  \hbar\,\delta^\ell_i\delta^j_k,
  \qquad
  [B^j{}_i,C^\ell{}_k]_{\mathrm{super}}
  =
  \delta^\ell_i\delta^j_k.
\]
Equivalently, after undoing the transpose convention, this is the Weyl
relation of `main.tex:6385-6392`.  The pre-BRST Zhu algebra is therefore
\[
  A(V_N^{\mathrm{Dir,free}})
  \cong
  W_\hbar(\operatorname{Mat}_N^2)
  \otimes
  \operatorname{Cliff}_{bc}(\mathfrak{gl}_N),
\]
where the second factor is the odd \(bc\) zero-mode algebra.

The Zhu differential is the BRST differential
\[
  QX=[C,X],\qquad
  QY=[C,Y],\qquad
  QC=\frac12[C,C],
\]
\[
  QB=YX-XY+\hbar N I+[B,C].
\]
The shift \(\hbar N I\) is the same normal-ordering/Capelli shift as in
`main.tex:6518-6538`; it is not optional.  Consequently
\[
  H^\bullet(A(V_N^{\mathrm{Dir}}),Q)
  \simeq
  C_{\mathrm{BRST}}^\bullet
  (\mathfrak{sl}_N,W_\hbar(\operatorname{Mat}_N^2),\widehat\mu),
\]
where
\[
  \widehat\mu(A)
  =
  \operatorname{Tr}\bigl(A(YX-XY+\hbar N I)\bigr)
  \qquad(A\in\mathfrak{sl}_N).
\]
In degree zero this is the quantum Hamiltonian reduction
\[
  H^0
  =
  N_{W_\hbar}(W_\hbar\widehat\mu(\mathfrak{sl}_N))
  \big/
  W_\hbar\widehat\mu(\mathfrak{sl}_N),
\]
with relation
\[
  YX-XY+\hbar N I=0
\]
inside the reduced algebra.  This is the Zhu form of the Capelli
renormalized Weyl/Moyal reduction already present in the manuscript.

## Hochschild/HKR/CE-PV Comparison Chain

The honest comparison chain is
\[
  \operatorname{ChirHoch}^\bullet(V_N^{\mathrm{Dir}})
  \xrightarrow{\operatorname{Zhu}^\ast}
  HH^\bullet(A(V_N^{\mathrm{Dir}}))
  \xrightarrow{\operatorname{gr}_{\hbar=0},\,N\to\infty}
  HH^\bullet(A_{\mathrm{cl,st}})
  \xrightarrow{\operatorname{HKR}}
  PV^\bullet(\widehat S(\mathfrak h_{\mathrm{red}}))
  \xrightarrow{\operatorname{CE/PV}}
  C_{\mathrm{CE}}^\bullet(\mathfrak h_{\mathrm{red}}\ltimes P[1]).
\]
Each arrow has hypotheses.

1. The Zhu bridge requires a \(\mathbb Z_{\ge 0}\)-graded vertex algebra
   with conformal vector, finite Laurent OPEs, and the Zhu product
   \[
      a*b=\operatorname{Res}_z
      Y(a,z)b\,\frac{(1+z)^{\operatorname{wt}(a)}}{z}\,dz.
   \]
   The map is the Vol II bridge from chiral Hochschild cochains to
   Hochschild cochains of the zero-mode/Zhu algebra.  It is a bridge map,
   not automatically a quasi-isomorphism.  The stronger quasi-isomorphism
   claims in Vol II require hypotheses such as rationality and
   \(C_2\)-cofiniteness, which are not presently known for this free
   Dirac BRST reduction.

2. The passage to HKR is classical/associated-graded.  It is not a
   finite-\(N\), finite-\(\hbar\) equality.  It requires the
   \(\hbar\)-adic associated graded, degreewise stable \(N\to\infty\)
   connected scalar reduction, and a completed smooth or quasi-free
   algebra for which continuous HKR is valid.

3. The final CE/PV identification requires the same completion and
   admissibility hypotheses as the local \(P_0\) theorem in
   `main.tex:1626-1797` and `theorem-lanes.tex:1000-1136`: constants
   removed, residue/principal-part pairing fixed, and Hamiltonian bracket
   transported through the retained \(z_2\)-coefficient system.

Under these hypotheses the chain says that the reduced curve BRST/Zhu
construction recovers, after stable associated grading, the same
CE/PV central-operation surface as the local reduced Hamiltonian sector.
It does not identify the native \(\mathbb C^2\) holomorphic \(E_2\)
factorization algebra with a curve VOA.

## Attacks and Heals

1. False native curve transfer.
   Attack: a vertex algebra and Zhu algebra cannot be the native object
   on \(\mathbb C^2\).
   Heal: the theorem surface is conditional on
   \(\mathcal R_{\mathbb C\times\mathbb R}\), and every curve statement
   is labelled reduced.

2. Killing the transverse holomorphic mode.
   Attack: setting \(z_2=0\) destroys the Hamiltonian bracket.
   Heal: retain \(B_{z_2}\) as formal coefficients or principal parts.

3. Unproved BRST nilpotence.
   Attack: \(Q_{\mathrm{BRST}}^2=0\) is not a slogan.
   Heal: square-zero follows from moment-map equivariance and graded
   Jacobi, displayed above.

4. Missing quantum shift.
   Attack: the Zhu moment-map relation without \(\hbar N I\) contradicts
   the manuscript's normal-ordering computation.
   Heal: the Zhu differential uses
   \(QB=YX-XY+\hbar N I+[B,C]\).

5. Overstrong Hochschild comparison.
   Attack: chiral Hochschild, Hochschild of Zhu, HKR, and CE/PV are not
   canonically all the same.
   Heal: the comparison is a chain with exact hypotheses and no
   finite-\(N\)/finite-\(\hbar\) quasi-isomorphism claim.
