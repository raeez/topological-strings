Chevalley--Eilenberg habitat.  It is false as the same-rank operation which
keeps fixed-rank one-cycle tensors and deletes fixed-rank multicycle tensors.

For a Koszul-weight bound \(K\) and CE-length bound \(L\), put
\[
  A_{\psi,K}=A_\psi/F^{>K}A_\psi,\qquad
  A_\psi=T(x,y,\psi),\qquad d\psi=xy-yx,

with
\[
  \operatorname{wt}(x)=(1,0),\qquad
  \operatorname{wt}(y)=(0,1),\qquad

\[
  N\geq\max(K,L+2),
\]

\[
  \lambda_{\mathrm{LQT},K,L}
  =
  \Theta_{N,K,L}^{-1}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}},
  \qquad
  P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}=\log^*(\mathrm{id})
  =
  \sum_{m=1}^{L}\frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{m}
  (\mathrm{id}-u\epsilon)^{*m}.

The convolution is computed with the stable block-sum product and the CE

```tex
\begin{thm}[Stable Eulerian finite-window LQT projection]
\label{thm:stable-eulerian-lqt-window}
Let
\[
  A_\psi=T(x,y,\psi),\qquad d\psi=xy-yx,
\]
with
\[
  \operatorname{wt}(x)=(1,0),\qquad
  \operatorname{wt}(y)=(0,1),\qquad
  \operatorname{wt}(\psi)=(1,1).
\]
For a Koszul-weight bound \(K\), let
\[
  A_{\psi,K}=A_\psi/F^{>K}A_\psi
\]
be the quotient by the two-sided dg ideal generated by words of total
Koszul weight \(>K\).  Let \(L\) bound CE length.  In the stable
block-sum Hopf CE window
\(\mathsf H^{\mathrm{st}}_{K,L}(A_\psi)\), with product induced by block
diagonal sum of matrix ranks and coproduct the CE coproduct, put
\[
  J=\mathrm{id}-u\epsilon,\qquad
  P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}
  =
  \log^*(\mathrm{id})
  =
  \sum_{m=1}^{L}\frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{m}J^{*m}.
\]
Then
\[
  P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}^2=P_{1\mathrm{cyc}},\qquad
  d_{\mathrm{CE}}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}
  =
  P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}d_{\mathrm{CE}},
\]
and
\[
  \operatorname{im}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}
  =
  \operatorname{im}\Theta_{N,K,L}.
\]
For
\[
  N\geq\max(K,L+2),
\]
the finite-window LQT map is the chain map
\[
  \lambda_{\mathrm{LQT},K,L}
  =
  \Theta_{N,K,L}^{-1}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}
  \colon
  \mathsf H^{\mathrm{st}}_{K,L}(A_\psi)
  \longrightarrow
  CC_{\mathrm{red},\leq L}(A_{\psi,K})[1].
\]
If one bound \(W\) controls both \(K\leq W\) and \(L\leq W\), this means
\(N\geq W+2\).
\end{thm}

\begin{proof}
The quotient \(A_{\psi,K}\) is a dg quotient because
\(d\psi=xy-yx\) preserves the Koszul bidegree \((1,1)\), while
\(dx=dy=0\).  The CE-length cutoff is separate: the algebra quotient
\(A_{\psi,K}\) alone still leaves reduced cyclic chains of unbounded
length.

For homogeneous \(a_i\in A_{\psi,K}\), define the trace-cycle map by
\[
  \Theta_{N,K,L}(a_0|\cdots|a_r)
  =
  \sum_{i_0,\ldots,i_r}
  (E_{i_0i_1}a_0)\wedge\cdots\wedge(E_{i_ri_0}a_r),
  \qquad r+1\leq L.
\]
The matrix bracket is
\[
  [E_{ij}a,E_{kl}b]
  =
  \delta_{jk}E_{il}ab
  -
  (-1)^{|a||b|}\delta_{li}E_{kj}ba,
\]
and the reduced cyclic relation is
\[
  (a_0|\cdots|a_r)
  \sim
  (-1)^{r+|a_r|(|a_0|+\cdots+|a_{r-1}|)}
  (a_r|a_0|\cdots|a_{r-1}).
\]
With these signs, the CE differential on trace cycles matches the reduced
cyclic differential; in particular
\[
  d_{\mathrm{CE}}\Theta_1(\psi)=\Theta_1(xy-yx),
\]
\[
  \lambda_{\mathrm{LQT},K,L}d_{\mathrm{CE}}\Theta_2(a,b)
  =
  [ab]-(-1)^{|a||b|}[ba],
\]
and
\[
  \lambda_{\mathrm{LQT},K,L}d_{\mathrm{CE}}\Theta_3(a,b,c)
  =
  [ab|c]-[a|bc]
  +
  (-1)^{|c|(|a|+|b|)}[ca|b].
\]

The stable CE window is formed before applying the projection.  Its
product is induced by block diagonal sum: a chain in rank \(N_1\) and a
chain in rank \(N_2\) are placed in disjoint diagonal blocks of rank
\(N_1+N_2\).  Cross-block brackets vanish, so this product is a chain map.
The CE coproduct is the usual cocommutative coalgebra coproduct on CE
chains; the CE differential is a coderivation, hence the coproduct is also
a chain map.  Unit and counit are chain maps.  Therefore each convolution
power \(J^{*m}\) is a chain map, and so is
\(P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}\).

In the stable range the invariant tensors are indexed by permutations of
the CE factors.  A permutation decomposes uniquely into cycles.  A single
cycle is exactly a trace-cycle tensor in the image of \(\Theta_{N,K,L}\);
a permutation with several cycles is the block-sum product of the
corresponding single-cycle tensors.  Thus
\(\mathsf H^{\mathrm{st}}_{K,L}(A_\psi)\) is the symmetric Hopf algebra on
the single-cycle span, truncated to the window \((K,L)\).  The bound
\(N\geq K\) excludes trace identities in total Koszul weight at most \(K\);
the bound \(N\geq L+2\) is the safe CE stable range for length at most
\(L\), matching the Loday--Quillen stabilization inequality
\(i<N-1\) in homological degree \(i\leq L\).  Hence
\(N\geq\max(K,L+2)\) is the degreewise stable range used here.

It remains to compute the Hopf logarithm.  Let
\[
  \Delta^{(m)}_{\mathrm{red}}
  =
  J^{\otimes m}\Delta^{(m)}.
\]
On a product \(z_1\cdots z_n\) of \(n\) positive single-cycle generators,
\(J^{*m}\) sums over ordered decompositions of the \(n\) factors into
\(m\) nonempty blocks.  After multiplication all terms again equal
\(z_1\cdots z_n\), with coefficient \(m!S(n,m)\), where \(S(n,m)\) is a
Stirling number of the second kind.  Hence
\[
  P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}(z_1\cdots z_n)
  =
  \left(
    \sum_{m=1}^{n}(-1)^{m-1}(m-1)!S(n,m)
  \right)z_1\cdots z_n.
\]
The coefficient is the coefficient of \(t^n/n!\) in
\(\log(1+(e^t-1))=t\).  It is \(1\) for \(n=1\) and \(0\) for
\(n\geq2\).  Therefore \(P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}\) fixes the single-cycle span
and kills all decomposable multicycle products.  This proves
\[
  P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}^2=P_{1\mathrm{cyc}},
  \qquad
  \operatorname{im}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}
  =
  \operatorname{im}\Theta_{N,K,L}.
\]
Since \(\Theta_{N,K,L}\) is injective on the displayed stable image, the
formula
\[
  \lambda_{\mathrm{LQT},K,L}
  =
  \Theta_{N,K,L}^{-1}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}
\]
defines a chain map.
\end{proof}
```

## False Same-Rank Projection Fence

This paragraph should be placed immediately after the theorem above, or as
the last paragraph of the K3 LQT item in `theorem-lanes.tex:2079-2105`.

```tex
The idempotent \(P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}\) above is not the raw fixed-rank
operation which keeps one-cycle permutation tensors and deletes all
multicycle tensors in a single exterior CE complex.  If \(P_{\mathrm{del}}\)
denotes that operation, then for homogeneous \(a,b\)
\[
  P_{\mathrm{del}}d_{\mathrm{CE}}
  \bigl(\Theta_1(a)\wedge\Theta_1(b)\bigr)
  =
  \pm\Theta_1(ab-(-1)^{|a||b|}ba),
  \qquad
  d_{\mathrm{CE}}P_{\mathrm{del}}
  \bigl(\Theta_1(a)\wedge\Theta_1(b)\bigr)=0.
\]
Thus \(P_{\mathrm{del}}\) is not a chain map when the graded commutator is
nonzero.  The stable Hopf projection is a cumulant projection: products are
formed by block sum before the Hopf logarithm is applied, and transfer to a
fixed stable rank subtracts diagonal-collision terms rather than deleting
raw multicycles.
```

The computation is first-principles.  With
\[
  \Theta_1(a)=\sum_i E_{ii}a,
\]
one has
\[
\begin{aligned}
  d_{\mathrm{CE}}\bigl(\Theta_1(a)\wedge\Theta_1(b)\bigr)
  &=
  \pm\sum_{i,j}[E_{ii}a,E_{jj}b]  \\
  &=
  \pm\sum_i E_{ii}\bigl(ab-(-1)^{|a||b|}ba\bigr) \\
  &=
  \pm\Theta_1(ab-(-1)^{|a||b|}ba).
\end{aligned}
\]
Applying \(P_{\mathrm{del}}\) after the differential keeps this one-cycle.
Applying \(P_{\mathrm{del}}\) before the differential kills
\(\Theta_1(a)\wedge\Theta_1(b)\) as a multicycle tensor.  The two results
therefore differ whenever the graded commutator is nonzero; \(a=x\),
\(b=y\) already gives the chain-level obstruction
\(\Theta_1(xy-yx)\).
