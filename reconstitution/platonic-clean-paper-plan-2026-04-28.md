## Governing Sentence

The paper's theorem spine is the reduced local algebraic statement:
formal Hamiltonian BF closed fields compute the Poisson-CE/PV center
model of the scalar-reduced constant-stalk Hamiltonian brane sector:
```tex
C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}
  (\mathfrak h\ltimes\mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1])
  \cong
\mathrm{PV}_{\mathrm{red}}(\mathcal O(\mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}})).
```
Its brane-side target is the selected scalar-reduced connected
Hamiltonian PBW sector of the first-class Dirac matrix probe, not the
full Dirac-reduced open BV/factorization algebra. The cotangent module
is the Matlis residue-current coadjoint module. The polynomial one-psi
classes are a PBW special-fibre shadow, not the deformation-level
coadjoint module.

The full unreduced factorization-center morphism, continuous Tate HKR
quasi-isomorphism, Costello brane-defect graph theory, QME cancellation,
all-`N` radial-parts comparison, and large-`N` cross-volume consequences
are conditional theorem lanes or open obligations until proved in their
own categories.

## Critical Diagnosis

The critique does not ask for a smaller paper. It asks for a typed paper.
The fatal risk is layer collapse.

The permanent type distinctions are:

1. Boundary Hamiltonian functions come from shifted-cotangent CE
   coordinates:
   ```tex
   \Phi(c^I)=\theta^I,\qquad \Phi(u_I)=O_I.
   ```
   Never write the boundary trace as the image of the Hamiltonian CE
   coordinate `c^I`.

2. Polynomial descendants and principal parts are not the same
   `\mathfrak h`-module:
   ```tex
   \Psi_{a,b} \not\simeq_{\mathfrak h} \rho_{a,b}.
   ```
   Their common bidegree labels are only PBW-symbol labels unless a
   separate Fourier/Rees theorem changes the action or target.

3. Locally constant factorization on intervals uses compactly supported
   de Rham forms:
   ```tex
   \mathfrak h_I=\Omega^\bullet_c(I)\widehat\otimes\mathfrak h.
   ```
   Degree-zero test functions appear only as the top-degree current
   component `a(t)dt`.

4. Scalar reduction is not harmless. The class `[\bar c]` is the
   classical center-of-mass extension and the quantum Capelli class
   `\hbar N[\bar c]`. It belongs before the derived-center theorem
   package. The effective Hamiltonian brane sector is `PGL_N`/`\mathfrak
   sl_N`, or else the `GL_N` model must carry an explicit decoupled
   scalar exterior factor.

5. The degree-zero Weyl/Moyal algebraic target is separate from the
   Costello brane-defect graph theorem. Reduced Wick coefficients are
   computations; an elliptic BV/QME realization is a named problem.
   The raw star commutator has odd `\hbar` powers; the normalized
   Hamiltonian bracket `\hbar^{-1}[f,g]_*` has even powers and generally
   a nonzero `\hbar^2` coefficient.

6. Cross-volume consequences are not outcomes of this paper. The local
   theorem is a formal-disk normalization until a target volume supplies
   a matched-conventions theorem.

## Attack-Heal Corrections

The six-agent attack phase forces the following dependency changes.
These are not optional style changes; they are proof-strength controls.

1. The symbol `Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}` may be used only after its
   category has been defined. Until continuous Tate HKR, compact-support
   interval shifts, and centrality homotopies are proved, the theorem is
   the reduced local Poisson-CE/PV model, not an unrestricted
   factorization-center equivalence.
2. The scalar quotient and effective-group choice precede the center
   theorem. `\operatorname{Tr}(\psi)` is a genuine `GL_N` Koszul class;
   projecting it out requires either an effective `PGL_N`/`\mathfrak
   sl_N` model or an explicit scalar factor plus projection.
3. The full formal Hamiltonian Lie algebra is not licensed by the
   admissible conilpotent bar-cobar theorem. CE/PV for the full local
   Hamiltonian algebra must be proved directly as a continuous
   generator-level cochain identity unless a new nonconilpotent completed
   bar-cobar theorem is supplied.
4. `\mathcal P` is the C-linear residue annihilator in
   `H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\mathbb C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2`, stable for the
   Hamiltonian coadjoint action. It is not an `R`-submodule, and it is
   not a quotient by `\rho_{0,0}`.
5. The compactly supported interval current model must carry the correct
   orientation shift: on an open interval `H_c^1(I)` is the point-like
   top-degree class, not `H_c^0(I)`.
6. The `P_0`/Schouten degree convention must be separated from the
   degree-zero boundary Poisson bracket. The source coordinates `u_K`
   are CE coordinates with nonzero differential
   `d_{\mathrm{CE}}u_K=C^L_{KJ}u_Lc^J`; they are not closed generators.
7. Rees/Fourier comparison is a limited associated-graded label bridge
   through the Fourier-delta lattice. It is not a same-action
   `\mathfrak h`-module bridge.
8. Quantum degree-zero results split into: unconditional formal
   Weyl/Moyal coefficients; conditional radial-parts comparison; and a
   not-yet-defined stable quantum target over either a
   `\lambda=\hbar N` Rees coefficient ring, a polynomial-in-`N`
   pro-system, or associated graded leading symbols.
9. Weighted Tate constructions provide finite-window/pro-kernel
   regulator control unless a genuine continuous BV pairing and Costello
   locality/QME theorem are proved.
10. First/third diagrams are reduced Wick coefficient diagrams unless
    the analytic Costello propagator, automorphism, counterterm,
    orientation, scalar anomaly, and QME data are displayed.

## Stratified Koszul Layers

Layer 0: operadic Koszul duality.
Finite-dimensional textbook identity:
`C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE}}(\mathfrak l)^! \simeq U(\mathfrak l)`.
No formal disk, no Tate completion, no large `N`.

Layer 1: continuous Tate CE/bar-cobar identity.
The completed CE and enveloping constructions are valid only under the
stated admissible Tate/conilpotent hypotheses.

Layer 2: Tate Quillen equivalence.
This is a categorical enhancement inside the admissible envelope. It
does not prove the Hamiltonian CE/PV theorem for the full perfect
Hamiltonian algebra.

Layer 3: reduced CE/PV Poisson-center model.
This is the local spine. Prove directly on generators:
```tex
d_{\mathrm{CE}}c^K=-\frac12C^K_{IJ}c^Ic^J,\qquad
d_{\mathrm{CE}}u_K=C^L_{KJ}u_Lc^J,
```
and
```tex
d_\pi\theta^K=-\frac12C^K_{IJ}\theta^I\theta^J,\qquad
d_\pi O_K=C^L_{KJ}O_L\theta^J.
```
This is not a consequence of the full formal Hamiltonian bar-cobar
theorem unless the missing nonconilpotent completed theorem is supplied.
It is also not yet an unrestricted factorization-center equivalence.

Layer 4: PBW/Rees special-fibre shadow.
The PBW special fibre gives
`S(\mathfrak h\oplus\mathfrak h^\vee[1])`. The open one-psi
descendants identify only with the polynomial symbol labels.

Layer 5: Matlis/Grothendieck cotangent module.
The continuous cotangent module is
```tex
\mathcal P=\operatorname{Ann}_{\mathrm{res}}(\mathbb C\cdot1)
\subset H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\mathbb C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2.
```
This is a C-linear residue annihilator stable under the Hamiltonian
coadjoint action, not an `R`-submodule. Its action is the coadjoint
principal-part action:
```tex
z_1^p z_2^q\cdot\rho_{a,b}
=-(pb-qa+p-q)\rho_{a-p+1,b-q+1}.
```

Layer 6: brane-line factorization Koszul layer.
For intervals, use `\Omega^\bullet_c(I)` and the source
`u_{a(t)dt\otimes f}`. The point-like class is represented by the
top-degree compactly supported current component `a(t)dt`. Prove
interval CE/PV and factorization compatibility after the orientation
shift and continuous HKR hypotheses are explicit.

Layer 7: stable large-`N` bulk-boundary Koszul horizon.
This remains conjectural and is not used to prove the local theorem.
