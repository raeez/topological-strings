# Agent 118 - Construct X/Xi Closed Exchange

## Stable core

The regular-density part of the closed-exchange branch is now constructed
inside the finite-window non-scalar QME tower.

For each finite Hamiltonian window \(M\), the source complex is
\[
  \mathcal X^{\bullet,\mathrm{reg}}_{w,M}(I)
    \subset \mathcal Q^\bullet_{w,M}(I),
\]
the smallest complete filtered \(d_M\)-stable subcomplex generated by the
scalar-zero regular-density renormalized graph weights
\[
  W^{R,\mathrm{reg},M}_{\Gamma,L}
    (a_\bullet,b_\bullet;f_\bullet,\rho_\bullet)
\]
and their regular-density local counterterms. The zero-internal-edge
generators are the scalar-zero coefficient-current terms
\[
  \iota_I(B_f^w(a)),\qquad
  \iota_I(\Theta_\rho^w(b(t)dt)).
\]

The differential is
\[
  d_M^{X,\mathrm{reg}}=
  d_M|_{\mathcal X^{\bullet,\mathrm{reg}}_{w,M}(I)}.
\]
The transition maps are restrictions of the non-scalar truncations:
\[
  \rho^{X,\mathrm{reg}}_{M,N}
  =\pi_{M,N}|_{\mathcal X^{\bullet,\mathrm{reg}}_{w,M}(I)}.
\]
The cochain map is the inclusion
\[
  \Xi_M^{\mathrm{reg}}\colon
  \mathcal X^{\bullet,\mathrm{reg}}_{w,M}(I)
  \hookrightarrow
  \mathcal Q^\bullet_{w,M}(I).
\]
It satisfies
\[
  \pi_{M,N}\Xi_M^{\mathrm{reg}}
  =
  \Xi_N^{\mathrm{reg}}\rho^{X,\mathrm{reg}}_{M,N},
\]
and is compatible with admissible weight transport.

This construction deliberately does not use a naive finite-dimensional
Hamiltonian Lie truncation. The window is a coefficient-support and graph
locality condition on regular-density local functionals, not a Lie quotient.

## Raw kernel obstruction

The stronger construction
\[
  \Xi_M=\kappa^{\mathrm{graph,reg},M}_{\hbar,w,I}
\]
from the regular-density CE source itself is not proved. Its exact ordinary
cochain-map obstruction is the pair
\[
  \widehat\sigma_{\mathrm{sc},M}
    \kappa^{\mathrm{graph,reg},M}_{\hbar,w,I},
  \qquad
  d_{\mathbf K}\kappa^{\mathrm{graph,reg},M}_{\hbar,w,I}
  =
  d_M\kappa^{\mathrm{graph,reg},M}_{\hbar,w,I}
  -
  \kappa^{\mathrm{graph,reg},M}_{\hbar,w,I}d_{\mathrm{CE}}.
\]
The first term obstructs landing in
\(\ker\widehat\sigma_{\mathrm{sc},M}\). The second obstructs commuting with
differentials. If the raw kernel is required to be a \(P_{0,\hbar}\)
central-operation kernel, the obstruction is strengthened to
\[
  \operatorname{Curv}_{\mathbf K}(\kappa)
  =
  d_{\mathbf K}\kappa+\frac12[\kappa,\kappa]_{\mathbf K}.
\]
