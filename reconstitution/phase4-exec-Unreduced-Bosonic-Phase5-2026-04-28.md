> $\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)$ and compatibility under
> $\mathfrak{gl}(N|N) \hookrightarrow \mathfrak{gl}(N+k|N+k)$. In

> $\mathfrak{gl}_N$ and the mixed $\R^2 \times \C^2$ Hamiltonian

> **(F4) Gauge data.** Ordinary $\mathfrak{gl}_N$ is not

### §1.2 Polyakov/Invariants reading

**What is preserved.** The Costello–Li 2015 / 2016 chain-level

On bosonic $\mathfrak{gl}_N$, the open-loop closure produces

**Invariant under the comparison.** The supertrace $\mathrm{Str}$ on
$\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)$ is a representation-theoretic constant that
does not have a bosonic analogue. The trace $\mathrm{Tr}$ on
$\mathfrak{gl}_N$ produces the non-zero coefficient $N$.

### §1.4 Polyakov scan: does any reduction or summand argument extract a bosonic discharge?

The manuscript notes that "additional reduction or summand argument"
might bridge from $\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)$ to $\mathfrak{gl}_N$. Two
candidates:

(R1) **Reduction.** Project $\mathfrak{gl}(N|N) \to \mathfrak{gl}_N$
via the even-block diagonal embedding. The supertrace
$\mathrm{Str}_{\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)}$ pulls back to
$\mathrm{Tr} - \mathrm{Tr} = 0$ on the even diagonal blocks taken
identically, but to $\mathrm{Tr}$ on a single block — a contradiction.
The supertrace identity $\mathrm{Str}(I) = N - N = 0$ does *not*
descend to a bosonic identity $\mathrm{Tr}(I) = N$ via projection.

(S1) **Summand.** Decompose $\mathfrak{gl}(N|N) = \mathfrak{gl}_N
\oplus \mathfrak{gl}_N \oplus (\mathrm{odd})$. The supertrace
identity is the *signed sum* of the two $\mathrm{Tr}_N$ traces; the
bosonic side is just one. There is no summand decomposition that
turns the bosonic anomaly $\mathrm{Tr}(I) = N$ into the cancelled
super-anomaly $\mathrm{Str}(I) = 0$.

**Both routes (R1) and (S1) fail.** This is consistent with the
manuscript's `rmk:convention-firewall` (`main.tex`:5380–5394) and
`prop:costello-li-2012-fail-local` (F4).

### §1.5 Conclusion on UB.1

UB.1 is **the only inscription-ready bosonic-adjacent discharge**
but is structurally **not a bosonic discharge**. Phase-5 obligations
on this candidate are nil — the inscription is complete, and the
named obstruction (F4) is permanent.

## §2. UB.2 — Wilsonian RG flow (Polchinski 1984; Costello 2011 Ch 4)

### §2.1 The Wilsonian RG mechanism

The Wilsonian renormalization group flow (Polchinski 1984; Costello
2011 Ch 4 §4.4) governs how the effective action $W(P_{\epsilon, L},
I[\epsilon])$ evolves under change of cutoff scale. The Costello
2011 framework, adapted to BV theories, defines the RG flow
operator
$$W(P_{\epsilon, L}, I[\epsilon]) = \hbar \log\!\left(\exp(\hbar \partial_{P_{\epsilon, L}}) \exp(I[\epsilon] / \hbar)\right),$$
and the renormalised interaction is obtained by adding local
counterterms and taking the $\epsilon \to 0$ limit. The RG flow
preserves the connected-graph expansion and locality.

**Polchinski 1984.** The original Wilsonian framework:
$$\partial_\Lambda S_\Lambda^{\mathrm{eff}} = -\frac{1}{2} \mathrm{Tr}\!\left(\partial_\Lambda P_\Lambda \cdot \frac{\delta^2 S_\Lambda^{\mathrm{eff}}}{\delta \phi^2}\right) + (\text{contact terms}),$$
where $S_\Lambda^{\mathrm{eff}}$ is the effective action at cutoff
$\Lambda$ and $P_\Lambda$ is the IR-cutoff propagator.

**The discharge question.** Can a Wilsonian RG flow produce a
local counterterm that cancels the residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$ on the
bosonic source?

### §2.2 Polyakov-lens reading: regulator-class invariance of $[\bar c]$

The (A1)–(A4) admissible regulator class
(`tate-T1-weighted-completion.tex`:596–632) is *defined* to ensure
regulator-class canonicity. `thm:wt-regulator-independence-admissible`
(`tate-T1-weighted-completion.tex`:634–707) records that the
chain-level QME residue is independent of the choice of admissible
weight (Polyakov scheme-independence on the bosonic source).

Concretely: the residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$ on the bosonic source has
chain-level representative
$$\mathrm{Ob}_{\mathrm{sc}}(\gamma_{\mathbf 1}; a, f; b, g) = \hbar N\,\omega(f,g)\,\int_\R a(t)b(t)\gamma_{\mathbf 1}(t)\,dt,$$
(`appendix-unreduced-bv-qme.tex`:118–123). The class
$[\bar c] \in H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A, \C)$ is invariant under
admissible-class regulator change. The coefficient $\hbar N$ is the
canonical Capelli shift (Theorem `thm:quantum-classical-anomaly`),
also invariant.

**Polyakov verdict.** Wilsonian RG flow inside the (A1)–(A4)
admissible class **cannot move** the residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$. The
class is regulator-class invariant by construction. The Wilsonian
flow on $W(P_{\epsilon, L}, I[\epsilon])$ varies the
*representative* but preserves the cohomology class.

### §2.3 Could a non-admissible Wilsonian regulator discharge the residue?

**The (A1)–(A4)-violating attempt.** Consider a regulator outside
the admissible class — e.g., one that violates (A1) (truncation
discipline) or (A4) (admissible filtered cohomology). Could such a
regulator produce a Wilsonian flow that cancels the residue?

**Three structural barriers.**

(2.B-1) **(A1) violation.** A regulator without truncation
discipline does not produce a chain-level QME equation; the
residue is not even well-defined as a chain-level cocycle. The
"discharge" question is meaningless.

(2.B-2) **(A4) violation.** A regulator that detects "tail-only"
classes outside finite quotients could in principle produce a
non-admissible Wilsonian counterterm. But by
`thm:strict-unweighted-no-go` (`tate-T2-nilpotent-truncation.tex`),
the strict unweighted product/direct-sum pair fails (A4) and does
not carry the Costello coefficient kernel. **No Wilsonian flow
exists in the strict unweighted regulator.**

(2.B-3) **Heat-kernel boundary.** Costello 2011 Ch 4's RG flow is
*defined* via the heat-kernel propagator $K_t$. On
$\R^2 \times \widehat{\C^2}_0$ with bosonic $\mathfrak{gl}_N$, the
heat-kernel construction is fixed by the gauge fixing
$Q^{\mathrm{GF}} = d_{\R^2}^* + \bar\partial_{\C^2}^*$. The RG flow
from $\epsilon \to 0$ is canonical inside the (A1)–(A4) admissible
class.

### §2.4 Invariants-lens reading

**Invariant 1 — $\mathrm{Tr}(I) = N$.** The bosonic matrix-factor
trace coefficient is *purely representation-theoretic* and is
preserved by every Wilsonian RG flow (Polchinski 1984; Costello
2011 Ch 4). No flow on the spacetime side can change a
representation-theoretic constant on the matrix side.

**Invariant 2 — $[\bar c] \in H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A, \C)$.** The
Lie-algebraic class on the polynomial Hamiltonian Lie algebra is
also invariant under Wilsonian flow because it lives in
*Lie-algebra cohomology* — a topological invariant of $\bar A$, not
a path-integral object.

**Conclusion.** Both invariants survive Wilsonian RG flow. The
residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$ is the *product* of these two invariants
and is preserved.

### §2.5 Verdict on UB.2

**NEGATIVE.** Wilsonian RG flow does not provide a bosonic discharge
mechanism distinct from the (A5) super-balance.

**Polyakov reading.** The residue is *manifestly Polyakov-invariant*
inside the (A1)–(A4) admissible class. Polyakov scheme-independence
(`thm:wt-regulator-independence-admissible`) records this
explicitly.

**Invariants reading.** The class lives in two structurally
invariant complexes (matrix-factor representation theory and
Lie-algebra cohomology of $\bar A$); Wilsonian flow does not act on
either.

**Phase-5 scope.** None. The Wilsonian RG flow is fully captured by
the existing (A1)–(A4) regulator-class machinery; no Phase-5
extension provides new discharge.

## §3. UB.3 — Geometric symmetry mechanisms (Sp(2), Sl(2, $\C$), Möbius, Hamiltonian rotation)

### §3.1 The geometric symmetries available on $(z_1, z_2)$

The holomorphic symplectic form $\omega = dz_1 \wedge dz_2$ on
$\widehat{\C^2}_0$ is preserved by:

(G1) **Sp(2, $\C$).** Linear symplectic transformations
$z \mapsto Mz$ with $M \in \mathrm{Sp}(2, \C) = \mathrm{SL}(2, \C)$.
This acts on $\C[z_1, z_2]$ by ring automorphisms preserving the
Poisson bracket.

(G2) **Möbius / $\mathrm{SL}(2, \C)$ on each $z_i$.** Independent
fractional-linear transformations on each variable. **Does not
preserve $\omega = dz_1 \wedge dz_2$ as a *global* form** (only up
to a scaling); irrelevant for the bracket structure.

(G3) **Hamiltonian rotation $\C^*$.** The flow generated by the
Hamiltonian $z_1 z_2$ (rotation in the $(z_1, z_2)$-plane).

(G4) **Translation $\C^2$.** Translations $z \mapsto z + v$. Note
the unreduced primitive $\eta(f) = -[f]_0$ is *not*
translation-invariant
(`reconstitution/phase4-exec-unreduced-primitive-2026-04-28.md`
T3-02).

(G5) **Scaling.** $(z_1, z_2) \mapsto (\lambda z_1, \mu z_2)$ for
$\lambda, \mu \in \C^*$ with $\lambda\mu = 1$ (preserves $\omega$).

(G6) **$\mathrm{Sp}(4, \R)$ outer.** No: $\mathrm{Sp}(4, \R)$ does
not act on $\C^2$ holomorphically.

### §3.2 Polyakov-lens reading: does any symmetry kill the constant Taylor coefficient cocycle?

The Lie 2-cocycle $\omega(f, g) = [\{f, g\}]_0$ is the constant
Taylor coefficient of the Poisson bracket. Each candidate symmetry
acts as follows.

**(G1) Sp(2, $\C$) action on $\omega$.** Let
$M = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$ with
$\det M = 1$. Then $M$ preserves $\omega$ pointwise:
$M^* \omega = \omega$. The induced action on $\bar A$ is the
ring automorphism $f \mapsto f \circ M$. The cocycle
$\omega(M^* f, M^* g) = \omega(f, g)$ pointwise (since the bracket
is Sp(2, $\C$)-invariant). Hence the class $[\bar c]$ is fixed by
Sp(2, $\C$). **Sp(2, $\C$) action does *not* kill $[\bar c]$.**

**(G3) Hamiltonian rotation by $H = z_1 z_2$.** The flow generated
by $\{H, -\}$ has $\{z_1 z_2, z_1\} = -z_1$,
$\{z_1 z_2, z_2\} = z_2$ — i.e., the standard scaling action by
$\mathrm{diag}(e^{-t}, e^t)$. Under this, $\omega(z_1, z_2) =
\{z_1, z_2\}|_0 = 1$ is fixed (the bracket is preserved by the
Hamiltonian flow of any scalar Hamiltonian). The class is fixed.
**Hamiltonian rotation does *not* kill $[\bar c]$.**

**(G5) Scaling.** Under $(z_1, z_2) \mapsto (\lambda z_1,
\lambda^{-1} z_2)$, $\omega(z_1, z_2) = \{z_1, z_2\}|_0 = 1$ is
fixed (since $\lambda \cdot \lambda^{-1} = 1$). The class is fixed.

**(G2) Möbius.** Möbius does *not* preserve the symplectic form
globally; the question is ill-posed inside the Hamiltonian BF
framework on $\widehat{\C^2}_0$ (the Poisson bracket is not Möbius-invariant).
This rules out Möbius as a discharge mechanism unless one *changes*
the symplectic structure to a Möbius-invariant one — but no such
non-trivial holomorphic-symplectic structure exists on
$\widehat{\C^2}_0$.

**Conclusion.** None of the geometric symmetries act non-trivially
on the Lie 2-cocycle class $[\bar c]$. The constant Taylor
coefficient $\omega(z_1, z_2) = 1$ is *invariant* under every
symplectic and rescaling symmetry of $(\widehat{\C^2}_0, \omega)$.

### §3.3 Invariants-lens reading: m-adic completion and brane-defect coupling

**m-adic completion.** The formal disk $\widehat{\C^2}_0$ is the
m-adic completion of $\C[z_1, z_2]$ at $\mathfrak m = (z_1, z_2)$.
The geometric symmetries (G1), (G3), (G5) preserve this completion
because they preserve the maximal ideal $\mathfrak m$ (G1: Sp(2)
fixes the origin; G3: the Hamiltonian rotation fixes $\mathfrak m$;
G5: scaling fixes $\mathfrak m$).

**Brane-defect coupling.** The boundary evaluation map
$\partial_{\mathrm{bb}, N}^{\mathrm{full}}: \C[z_1, z_2] \to
\C[\mathfrak{gl}_N^2]^{GL_N}$ commutes with Sp(2, $\C$) action
(both sides act linearly on the matrix factor by the same
representation). Hamiltonian rotation lifts to a one-parameter
family of automorphisms of the brane-defect coupling. Scaling
similarly.

**Geometric-symmetry-equivariant reformulation.** Could the residue
class $\hbar N[\bar c]$ be reinterpreted as a *symmetry-equivariant*
class that vanishes upon equivariant restriction? The candidate is
the equivariant cohomology
$H^*_{\mathrm{eq}}(\bar A, \C; \mathrm{Sp}(2, \C))$. But the
Sp(2, $\C$)-action on $\bar A$ is the standard one, and the cocycle
$\omega$ is Sp(2, $\C$)-invariant. The equivariant class
$[\bar c]_{\mathrm{eq}}$ is non-zero precisely because $[\bar c]$
is non-zero in non-equivariant cohomology and Sp(2, $\C$) is
connected. **No equivariant cancellation.**

### §3.4 Polyakov scan: scaling under $\hbar^2 = \varepsilon_1 \varepsilon_2$ rebasing

The G4-M2 Heisenberg twist
(`reconstitution/phase4-exec-G4-M2-Heisenberg-twist-2026-04-28.md`)
provides the rebasing $\hbar^2 = \varepsilon_1 \varepsilon_2$ via
the equivariant CGW (Costello–Gaiotto–Williams) framework. Under
this rebasing, the residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$ becomes
$\sqrt{\varepsilon_1\varepsilon_2}\,N\,[\bar c]$. **The class is
preserved under rebasing; only the coupling parameter changes
form.** This confirms that scaling-type symmetries do not
discharge the bosonic class.

**Polyakov reading.** The class $[\bar c]$ is fixed by every

**Phase-5 scope.** None. Geometric symmetries do not provide a

## §4. UB.4 — Topological-twist mechanism (CGW arXiv:2007.09497 framework on $\R^2 \times \C^2$)

### §4.1 The CGW topological-twist framework

Costello–Gaiotto–Williams (CGW, arXiv:2007.09497) construct a
twisted M-theory protected sector on $\R^2 \times \C^2$, with
worldsheet vertex algebra $W_{1+\infty}(\varepsilon_1,
\varepsilon_2)$ at level 1, equivariant for the
$T^2 = (\C^*)^2$ rotating $\C^2$. The Heisenberg sub-VOA of
$W_{1+\infty}$ is the spin-1 current $J(z) = J^{(1)}(z)$, with
level $k(\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2)$ a quadratic function.

**G4-M2 anchor.** The G4-M2 milestone
(`reconstitution/phase4-exec-G4-M2-Heisenberg-twist-2026-04-28.md`)
constructs a twist functor $\tau_{\mathfrak h}$ on the Heisenberg
sub-VOA, identifies the level $k$ in $\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2$,
and verifies that the twist limit produces a finite cohomological
class matching $[\bar c]$ at the diagonal self-dual point
$\lambda = 1$, modulo the rebasing $\hbar^2 = \varepsilon_1
\varepsilon_2$.

### §4.2 The bosonic discharge question for UB.4

**The conjecture.** Could the topological-twist on $\R^2 \times \C^2$
produce a *bosonic* anomaly cancellation distinct from the
super-balance?

**The mechanism candidate.** In CGW's protected sector, the chiral
algebra $W_{1+\infty}(\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2)$ has its anomaly
class redistributed across the spin-tower
$[c^{(s)}]_{s \geq 1}$. The Heisenberg level $k$ at spin 1 carries
the central charge contribution $[c^{(1)}]$. **At the diagonal
self-dual point**, $[c^{(1)}]$ matches our $[\bar c]$ modulo
rebasing. Could a *non-diagonal* point in
$(\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2)$-parameter space produce a
cancellation $[c^{(1)}] = 0$?

### §4.3 Polyakov-lens reading

**The CGW level computation.** From G4-M2:
$$k(\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2) = \frac{(\varepsilon_1 + \varepsilon_2)^2}{\varepsilon_1 \varepsilon_2}$$
(quadratic in the $\varepsilon$'s, vanishing at the *anti-diagonal*
self-dual point $\varepsilon_1 = -\varepsilon_2$).

**The anti-diagonal candidate.** At $\varepsilon_1 = -\varepsilon_2$,
$k = 0$ and the Heisenberg current decouples: no central charge,
no anomaly. **Could this be a bosonic discharge?**

**The structural barrier.** The anti-diagonal point
$\varepsilon_1 = -\varepsilon_2$ corresponds (via the rebasing
$\hbar^2 = \varepsilon_1 \varepsilon_2$) to $\hbar^2 < 0$ — i.e.,
imaginary $\hbar$. In the CGW physical interpretation, this is the
*chiral algebra structure*, not the topological string structure
($\hbar$ real). The anti-diagonal limit *cancels* the central
charge but *also* cancels the $\hbar$-deformation parameter; the
resulting theory is the *commutative* limit, not a discharge of the
deformed quantum theory.

**Polyakov scan.** The cancellation at the anti-diagonal is a
*degeneration* of the deformation parameter, not a regulator-class
flow inside the (A1)–(A4) admissible class. Polyakov-class
scheme-independence does *not* extend to deformation-parameter
degeneration.

### §4.4 Invariants-lens reading

**Invariant under twist.** The CGW twist functor $\tau_{\mathfrak h}$
preserves the spin-tower decomposition; the central charge
$[c^{(1)}]$ at spin 1 is the dominant contribution. At the
anti-diagonal, this contribution vanishes, but so does the
underlying quantum structure (commutative limit).

**Invariant: representation theory.** The bosonic
$\mathfrak{gl}_N$ structure on the *open algebra* is unchanged by
the CGW twist (which acts on the closed-string side). The
bosonic-side residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$ persists at any non-degenerate
point in $(\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2)$-parameter space.

### §4.5 Verdict on UB.4

**PARTIAL.** The CGW topological-twist framework *redistributes* the
anomaly class through the spin-tower (G4-M2 PARTIAL) but does
**not** cancel the bosonic-side residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$. The only
candidate cancellation point (anti-diagonal $\varepsilon_1 = -\varepsilon_2$)
degenerates the underlying quantum structure, producing a
commutative limit rather than a discharge of the deformed theory.

**Phase-5 scope.** UB.4 is a *redistribution* mechanism, not a
*cancellation* mechanism. It moves the bosonic residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$
into the spin-tower (e.g., higher $W_{1+\infty}$ generators) but
does not eliminate it. Phase-5 milestones G4-M3 (W$_3$ twist),
G4-T22 (Virasoro twist) are the natural follow-ups; both are
*frontier*, not *firewall*.

**Note.** UB.4's PARTIAL status is the strongest viable candidate
in the survey, alongside UB.5. It is **not** a discharge of the
inscribed Theorem G; it is a different theorem (CGW protected
sector with redistributed anomaly).

## §5. UB.5 — Boundary-state mechanism (Lagrangian boundary at infinity on $\R^2 \times \C^2$)

### §5.1 The boundary-state mechanism

The candidate Phase-5 mechanism is to add a *bosonic boundary state*
at infinity on $\R^2 \times \C^2$, producing a counter-term that
cancels the residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$. This is suggested by the
manuscript's `rmk:t4-four-missing` (`tate-T4-bv-vanishing.tex`:155–192):

> **(I) Non-compact / Omega-background BV-cohomology vanishing.** A
> version of `stmt:costello-li-2012-restated` on $\R^2 \times \C^2$,
> either via Costello's Omega-background (Costello, twisted M-theory
> arXiv:1610.04144) or via a compactification with **Lagrangian
> boundary at infinity**.

**The mechanism.** Add a Lagrangian boundary state
$|L\rangle \in \mathcal H_{\mathrm{closed}}$ at the spatial infinity
$|z_1|^2 + |z_2|^2 \to \infty$. The boundary-state path integral
contributes a counter-term:
$$\langle 0 | e^{-\int I_0} | L \rangle = \langle 0 | e^{-\int I_0} | 0 \rangle + \delta_L\!\left(\int I_0\right),$$
where $\delta_L$ encodes the boundary contribution. **Could
$\delta_L$ produce a counter-term cancelling $\hbar N[\bar c]$?**

### §5.2 Polyakov-lens reading

**Where does the bosonic counter-term come from?** A Lagrangian
boundary at infinity introduces *boundary modes* that contribute to
the path integral via the Lagrangian-state expansion. On bosonic
$\mathfrak{gl}_N$, the boundary modes are fields valued in the
matrix algebra; the boundary-state path integral generically
contributes a *constant* shift to the effective action.

**The shift candidate.** Could the boundary state produce a counter-
term of the form $C_{\rm bdy} = \hbar N \cdot \chi(L)$, where
$\chi(L)$ is a topological invariant of $L$, with the property
$(Q + \{I_0, -\}) C_{\rm bdy} = -\hbar N[\bar c]$?

**The structural barrier (Costello locality).** Costello-RG locality
(stmt:costello-bv-package, `main.tex`:5136–5152) requires the
counter-term to be a *local* functional in the BV complex. A
boundary state at *infinity* is non-local in the spatial direction
— it depends on the global geometry of the boundary, not on local
observables. **Boundary-state counter-terms are non-local in the
Costello sense.**

**Trade-off.** To preserve Costello-RG locality, one must either:

(5.A) **Localise the boundary contribution.** Replace the boundary
at infinity by a *local* Lagrangian fragment supported in a
bounded region — but this changes the theory (no longer
$\R^2 \times \C^2$ with brane $\R \times p$).

(5.B) **Allow non-local counter-terms.** Step outside Costello-RG
locality. This violates (A1)–(A4) of the admissible regulator class
and is therefore outside the manuscript's framework.

### §5.3 Invariants-lens reading

**Invariant under (5.A).** Localising the boundary fragment changes
the underlying spacetime to $\R^2 \times \C^2 \cup L$ where $L$ is
a compact Lagrangian piece. The residue class on the modified
spacetime is *different* from $[\bar c]$ on the original.

**Invariant under (5.B).** The (A4) admissible filtered cohomology
is violated; the residue class is no longer well-defined on finite
quotients.

**Conclusion.** Both (5.A) and (5.B) are *changes of theorem*, not
discharges of the inscribed Theorem G.

### §5.4 The Costello–Li 2012 / 2015 / 2016 anti-attack

**Costello–Li 2015 boundary structure.** On flat $\C^d$,
Costello–Li 2015 work with brane probes on Lagrangian submanifolds
$L \subset \C^d$. The brane is *compact* (or at least
finite-extent in the holomorphic direction). The anomaly cancellation
mechanism (Costello–Li 2016 Theorem A) relies on the supertrace
$\mathrm{Str}_{\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)}(I) = 0$, *not* on the boundary
geometry of $L$. **Costello–Li boundary states do not provide a
bosonic discharge.**

**Polyakov anti-attack.** In Polyakov's framework, the path-integral
boundary conditions are part of the regulator data. Different
boundary conditions correspond to different regulator schemes; if
the residue is regulator-class invariant (under (A1)–(A4)), then
different boundary conditions cannot change it. **The boundary
state at infinity is just one regulator scheme; it cannot move
$[\bar c]$ inside the admissible class.**

### §5.5 Verdict on UB.5

**POSSIBLE but highly conditional.** A boundary-state mechanism
could in principle produce a bosonic counter-term, but only at the
cost of either (5.A) changing the spacetime or (5.B) violating
Costello-RG locality. Neither is a discharge of the inscribed
Theorem G inside the manuscript's framework.

**Polyakov reading.** Boundary conditions are regulator-class data;
admissible-class regulators are insensitive to boundary-state
choice (`thm:wt-regulator-independence-admissible`).

**Invariants reading.** The class $[\bar c]$ is invariant under
admissible regulator change; non-admissible (5.A or 5.B) regulators
do change the class but *also* change the theory.

**Phase-5 scope.** UB.5 requires *fresh boundary apparatus* — a
non-trivial extension of the Costello-RG framework to handle
non-local boundary-state contributions. The Costello twisted
M-theory (arXiv:1610.04144) and the Omega-background framework
provide candidate apparatus, but neither has been verified to
produce a bosonic anomaly cancellation on
$\R^2 \times \widehat{\C^2}_0$.

**Together with UB.4.** UB.5 is structurally close to UB.4 (CGW
twist) — both involve modifying the boundary / topological structure
at infinity. The smallest viable Phase-5 mechanism (§8) combines
elements of both.

## §6. UB.6 — Cohomological-shift / parity-preserving degree shift

### §6.1 The cohomological-shift mechanism

The candidate Phase-5 mechanism is to absorb the residue
$\hbar N[\bar c]$ via a degree shift in the BV complex. Concretely:
- Replace the BV complex $(\mathcal A^\bullet, Q + \{I_0, -\})$ by
  a *shifted* version $(\mathcal A^\bullet[k], Q + \{I_0, -\})$
  for some integer shift $k$.
- Or introduce a parity-preserving degree generator $\zeta$ of
  parity 0 and BV degree $\pm 1$ such that $\delta \zeta = \bar c$
  (shifted-cocycle structure).

### §6.2 The Massey-product re-emergence

This is the same structural mechanism as the A$_\infty$-deformation
prescription (P-A$_\infty$) in the unreduced primitive analysis
(`reconstitution/phase4-exec-unreduced-primitive-2026-04-28.md`
T4-01). The structural argument applies *verbatim* on the reduced
side $\bar A$ as well.

**The Massey product.** Suppose $\zeta$ is a parity-0 generator of
BV degree $\pm 1$ with $\delta \zeta = \bar c$. Then the
A$_\infty$-coherence at arity 3 forces a Massey product
$m_3(\bar c, \bar c, \bar c)$, computable from the cup product
$[\bar c]^{\cup 2} \in H^4_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A, \C)$. From the
unreduced analysis:

> The A$_\infty$ Massey product $m_3(\bar c, \bar c, \bar c)$ equals
> $[\bar c]^{\cup 2} \cdot \bar c$ modulo coboundaries; this is
> non-zero in $H^6_{\mathrm{CE}}(\bar A, \C)$. The residue at order
> $\hbar^3 N^3$ in the A$_\infty$-deformed QME is exactly this
> Massey product, **non-zero**.

The same applies to UB.6: the parity-preserving degree shift
postpones the residue from order $\hbar^1 N^1$ to order
$\hbar^3 N^3$ via the cup product $[\bar c]^{\cup 2}$, but does not
cancel it.

### §6.3 Polyakov-lens reading

**Polyakov scaling.** A degree shift in the BV complex changes the
*grading* of the obstruction class but does not change its
*cohomology class*. Polyakov's question "what is preserved under
this transformation?" answers: *the cohomology class is preserved*.

**Concrete check.** Suppose $\zeta$ has BV degree $-1$. Then
$\hbar N \zeta \cdot \bar c$ has BV degree $0 + (-1) + 1 = 0$ (wrong;
needs to match the degree-1 obstruction representative), or
$+1$ if $\zeta$ has BV degree 0. Either way, the
*coefficient* $\hbar N$ is unchanged. The shift moves the residue
to a different degree slice but preserves the coefficient.

### §6.4 Invariants-lens reading

**Invariant under shift.** The class $[\bar c]$ is invariant under
degree shifts because it is a Lie-algebra cohomology class on
$\bar A$ — independent of the BV degree assignment. Shifting the
BV grading does not move the underlying cohomology class.

**Invariant under cup product.** The cup product
$[\bar c]^{\cup 2}$ in $H^4_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A, \C)$ is *also*
non-zero, by the structure of the $\bar A$-cohomology ring. The
Massey product re-emerges as the higher-order obstruction.

**Conclusion.** The cohomological-shift mechanism does not discharge
the residue; it merely postpones it to higher loop order via the
cup product structure.

### §6.5 Verdict on UB.6

**NEGATIVE.** Cohomological shifts in the BV complex do not
discharge the residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$. The same Massey-product
obstruction that closes the A$_\infty$-deformed unreduced primitive
route closes the UB.6 candidate.

**Polyakov reading.** Cohomology classes are invariant under
degree shifts; the shift preserves the residue coefficient.

**Invariants reading.** The cup product $[\bar c]^{\cup 2}$ is
non-zero in $H^4_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A, \C)$; the Massey product
re-introduces the obstruction at higher order.

**Phase-5 scope.** None. The structural barrier is identical to the
A$_\infty$-deformation analysis already closed in the unreduced
primitive route.

## §7. UB.7 — Cross-volume BCOV-A5 firewall lift at chain layer for the bosonic side

### §7.1 The BCOV-A5 firewall structure

The BCOV-A5 comparison
(`reconstitution/phase4-exec-BCOV-A5-comparison-2026-04-28.md`)
records that the cross-volume firewall is structurally permanent
at the chain-level / vertex-class layer for three reasons:

(F-1) **Spacetime mismatch.** BCOV is on a CY$_3$ (or flat $\C^3$);
   we are on $\R^2 \times \widehat{\C^2}_0$. The dimensions and
   structure groups do not match.

(F-2) **Form-structure mismatch.** BCOV uses the Calabi–Yau
   holomorphic volume form $\Omega_X$; we use a holomorphic
   symplectic form $\omega = dz_1 \wedge dz_2$. These are not the
   same datum.

(F-3) **Field-content mismatch.** BCOV fields are polyvector fields
   with the Schouten–Nijenhuis bracket; ours are Hamiltonian BF
   fields with the polynomial Hamiltonian bracket.

The Costello–Li 2015 partial firewall lift covers $d = 3$ flat
case with $\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)$ open algebra. **Our $d = 2$
surface model is not within their theorem.** Worse: the bosonic
$\mathfrak{gl}_N$ open algebra is outside the Costello–Li framework
(F4 in `prop:costello-li-2012-fail-local`).

### §7.2 Could the firewall lift on the bosonic side specifically?

**The candidate Phase-5 mechanism.** A *new* matched-conventions
theorem comparing BCOV BV theory on $\C^2$ (or $\R^2 \times \C^2$)
with bosonic open algebra to our Hamiltonian BF on
$\R^2 \times \widehat{\C^2}_0$. This would discharge the bosonic
residue if BCOV on $\C^2$ provides a bosonic anomaly-cancellation
mechanism.

**Why it fails — the BCOV side has no bosonic discharge.** The BCOV
1994 holomorphic anomaly equation (Eq. 6.11) has the
*Euler-characteristic correction* $\chi(X)/24 - 1$ on the closed
side, which is **non-zero** for compact CY$_3$. On the open side,
Costello–Li 2015 / 2016 cancel the U(1) center anomaly *only via
the supertrace*. **There is no BCOV statement that cancels a
bosonic U(1) center anomaly.**

**The structural reason.** BCOV's closed-string anomaly mechanism
relies on the Calabi–Yau condition $c_1(X) = 0$ to ensure
$\partial_\Omega$ is exact on polyvectors. The open-string
extension (Costello–Li 2015 / 2016) requires $\mathrm{Str}(I) = 0$
on the open algebra. **Both mechanisms fail on bosonic
$\mathfrak{gl}_N$**: the closed-string mechanism cancels the
*closed-string* anomaly (Euler-characteristic correction), not the
*open-string* U(1) center; the open-string mechanism requires
super-balance.

### §7.3 Polyakov-lens reading

**Polyakov: regulator-class invariance.** Even if a matched-
conventions theorem existed, the residue $\hbar N[\bar c]$ would
still be the *coupling coefficient* on the open-loop closure.
Polyakov scheme-independence on the bosonic side is invariant under
admissible regulator change but *cannot* eliminate the coefficient
$\mathrm{Tr}(I) = N$.

**Polyakov: anomaly cohomology.** The U(1) center anomaly on
bosonic $\mathfrak{gl}_N$ is a genuine cohomology class (not gauged
away by any local counterterm inside the (A1)–(A4) class). On the
BCOV side, this would correspond to a non-zero Pontryagin-class-type
anomaly that is *not* cancelled by the Calabi–Yau condition. **No
analogue of the Calabi–Yau cancellation works for bosonic
$\mathfrak{gl}_N$.**

### §7.4 Invariants-lens reading

**Invariant 1: $\mathrm{Tr}(I) = N$.** Preserved under any matched-
conventions functor between BCOV and our local model. Cannot be
gauged away on the bosonic side.

**Invariant 2: $[\bar c]$.** Preserved as a Lie-algebra cohomology
class on $\bar A$. The BCOV vertex cocycle (the $\bar\partial$-coboundary
class) is structurally distinct, but the comparison preserves the
non-vanishing of either class on the respective bosonic side.

**Conclusion.** No cross-volume mechanism on the bosonic side
discharges the residue. The firewall is *firewall-permanent* at the
bosonic level.

### §7.5 Verdict on UB.7

**FIREWALL-PERMANENT.** The BCOV-A5 firewall does not lift at the
chain layer for the bosonic side. The Costello–Li 2015 / 2016
framework provides the only currently inscribed partial lift
($d = 3$ flat with $\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)$); bosonic $\mathfrak{gl}_N$

our side.

**Phase-5 scope.** The named obstruction is the *absence of a
