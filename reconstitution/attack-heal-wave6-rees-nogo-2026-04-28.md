## Claim Attacked

The new Fourier--Rees bridge might give an honest same-action
\(\mathfrak h\)-module interpolation between the polynomial
one-\(\psi\) descendants \(\Psi_{a,b}\) and the Matlis principal parts
\(\rho_{a,b}\).  The strongest possible version would identify the
Rees Weyl Fourier transform
\[
  \mathcal O_\hbar=\mathbb C[z_1,z_2][[\hbar]]
  \simeq \mathcal D_\hbar/(\partial_1,\partial_2)
\]
with the full Cech local-cohomology lattice
\[
  H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\mathbb C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2[[\hbar]]
\]
and then read this as a flat bridge to \(\mathcal P\).

## Failure Mode Found

The full-lattice assertion is false over \(\mathbb C[[\hbar]]\).  The
Fourier twist is the abstract Rees delta module
\[
  \Delta^{\mathsf F}_\hbar=\mathcal D_\hbar/(z_1,z_2),
\]
generated by \(e_0\).  Its Cech realization sends
\[
  \partial_1^a\partial_2^b e_0
  \longmapsto
  \partial_1^a\partial_2^b\rho_{0,0}
  =
  (-1)^{a+b}\hbar^{a+b}a!b!\rho_{a,b}.
\]
Thus it lands in the Rees sublattice
\[
  \bigoplus_{a,b\ge0}
  \mathbb C[[\hbar]]\,\hbar^{a+b}\rho_{a,b}
  \subset
  H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\mathbb C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2[[\hbar]],
\]
up to the unit factors \((-1)^{a+b}a!b!\).  It becomes the full
principal-part lattice only after tensoring with \(\mathbb C((\hbar))\).

## Heal Inscribed

Theorem `thm:app-matlis-rees-fourier-bridge` now separates:

- the abstract Fourier delta Rees module
  \(\Delta^{\mathsf F}_\hbar=\mathcal D_\hbar/(z_1,z_2)\);
- the standard Cech lattice
  \(\Delta^{\mathrm{std}}_\hbar=H^2_{\mathfrak m}(R)dz_1dz_2[[\hbar]]\);
- the embedding of the first into the second by the
  \(\hbar^{a+b}a!b!\) Rees factor;
- the generic-fibre label bridge after \(\hbar\) is inverted.

The theorem also distinguishes the Rees-normalized Hamiltonian operators:
\[
  \hbar X_{z_1}=\partial_2,\qquad
  \hbar X_{z_2}=-\partial_1.
\]
Fourier sends these to multiplication by \(-z_2\) and \(z_1\), which is
locally nilpotent on the delta Rees module.  The Matlis same-action
operators remain
\[
  \hbar X_{z_1}\rho_{a,b}=-(b+1)\hbar\rho_{a,b+1},\qquad
  \hbar X_{z_2}\rho_{a,b}=(a+1)\hbar\rho_{a+1,b},
\]
which are not locally nilpotent on any nonzero element after
\(\hbar\) is inverted.

## No-Go Status

Same-action bridge: ruled out on the generic fibre by local
nilpotence.  Multiplication by the invertible scalar \(\hbar\) does not
change local nilpotence, so the obstruction applies to both normalized
operators \(\hbar X_{z_i}\) and unnormalized Hamiltonian vector fields
\(X_{z_i}\).

Fourier--Rees bridge: valid only as a polarization-changing Rees Weyl
module and associated-graded label comparison.  It does not weaken the
Matlis residue action, the no-polynomial theorem, or the quantum
descendant obstruction.

Quantum descendant obstruction: unchanged.  The exact script still
finds the displayed obstruction
\[
  \operatorname{ad}^{\vee}_{z_1^4,\hbar}\delta_{1,1}
  =-24\hbar^2\delta_{0,4}+\cdots,
\]
while the PBW one-\(\psi\) action sends
\[
  \widetilde\Psi_{1,1}\mapsto4\widetilde\Psi_{4,0}.
\]
