# Attack-heal wave6: strict unweighted Tate endpoint

Status: converged for the natural strict endpoint claim; remaining
mixed brane-defect QME question unchanged.

## Claim attacked

The wave5 theorem proves independence of the admissible finite-window
weight.  The stronger claim would identify this regulated weighted
product theory with the original strict product/direct-sum Tate pair
\[
  \mathfrak h_{\Pi}=\prod_{d\geq1}H_d,\qquad
  D_{\oplus}=\bigoplus_{d\geq1}H_d^\vee .
\]

## Result

The stronger strict unweighted claim is false under the Costello
coefficient-kernel hypotheses.  The new theorem
`\ref{thm:strict-unweighted-no-go}` proves:

- no tensor \(K\in\mathfrak h_{\Pi}\widehat\otimes D_{\oplus}\), or in
  the reversed tensor product, projects to the finite Casimir
  \(C^{w_0}_{\mathfrak h}\) for every finite Tate window;
- therefore the strict product/direct-sum pair cannot be the endpoint
  of a finite-window Costello regulator theory whose propagator is
  \(P_{\epsilon,L}^{\mathrm{base}}\widehat\otimes K\);
- the inclusion \(D_{\oplus}\hookrightarrow
  D_{\Pi,w}=\prod_d w(d)^{-1}H_d^\vee\) is only an admissible filtered
  weak equivalence, not an ordinary quasi-isomorphism, because
  \(H^0(D_{\Pi,w}/D_{\oplus})\neq0\).

The proof is the support calculation.  A tensor with a strict direct-sum
factor has finite support in that factor.  A compatible family of
finite-window Casimirs has nonzero support in every \(H_d^\vee\).
These conditions are incompatible.

## Hidden hypotheses audited and healed

- Exactness of inverse limits is now stated only for degreewise
  surjective Mittag-Leffler towers in the admissible envelope of
  `\ref{stmt:tate-model-envelope}`.  No raw inverse-limit exactness is
  used.
- Weight rescaling is now bracket/counterterm transport.  It is not
  described as a symplectic rescaling preserving the unweighted
  Hamiltonian bracket.
- The obstruction class in the weight-independence theorem is now
  explicitly in admissible filtered cohomology.  Tail-sensitive
  ordinary cohomology is outside the theorem.
- The Hadamard/Mittag-Leffler reduction no longer claims finiteness of
  all graph topologies at fixed \(\hbar\)-order.  It asserts only the
  graphwise finite-window bound needed for the theorem.
