The toy's success criterion is **a finite Lie 2-cocycle class on a

$\epsilon \to 0$ blow-up. The W31 §3.2 baseline is "the limit
diverges naively"; M2's discharge converts this into a finite

## §2. T2 — Heisenberg sub-VOA structure

### §2.1 Definition of the Heisenberg sub-VOA inside $W_{1+\infty}$

$W_{1+\infty}(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$ at level 1 has spin-$s$
generators $J^{(s)}(z)$ for $s \geq 1$. The spin-1 generator is the
**Heisenberg current**
$$
J(z) := J^{(1)}(z)
$$
with the standard Heisenberg OPE
$$
J(z) J(w) \;=\; \frac{k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)}{(z - w)^2} \;+\; \mathrm{reg.},
$$
where $k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$ is the **Heisenberg level**.

### §2.2 The level $k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$

In the Schiffmann–Vasserot / Maulik–Okounkov free-field realisation
of $W_{1+\infty}(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$, the Heisenberg level is
fixed by the Schiffmann–Vasserot normalisation of the affine
Yangian $Y_{\hbar_1, \hbar_2}(\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}_1)$, where
$\hbar_i = \epsilon_i$. Several normalisations are in use; the
manifestly $S_3$-symmetric form (with $\epsilon_3 = -\epsilon_1 -
\epsilon_2$ enforced) is

$$
k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2) \;=\; -\frac{(\epsilon_1 + \epsilon_2)}{\epsilon_1 \epsilon_2}
\;=\; \frac{\epsilon_3}{\epsilon_1 \epsilon_2}.
$$

This is the **standard CGW boundary VOA Heisenberg level** at level
1. **Provenance.** The full Schiffmann–Vasserot normalisation
(quoted in M1 §2.2 as $c(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2) =
(\epsilon_1+\epsilon_2)^2 / (\epsilon_1 \epsilon_2)$ for the Virasoro
central charge) carries the Heisenberg level above by the standard
relation between the level of the U(1)-current and the Virasoro
central charge of the Sugawara stress-energy
$T(z) = \tfrac{1}{2k}\!:\!J(z)^2\!:$.

**Sugawara consistency.** With Heisenberg level $k$, the Sugawara
construction $T(z) = \tfrac{1}{2k}\!:\!J(z)^2\!: + \cdots$ has
Virasoro central charge $c_{\mathrm{Sug}} = 1$ (single boson). The
*full* $W_{1+\infty}$ central charge $c = (\epsilon_1+\epsilon_2)^2 /
(\epsilon_1 \epsilon_2)$ exceeds $c_{\mathrm{Sug}} = 1$ because
higher-spin generators $J^{(s \geq 2)}$ contribute additively to
the universal $W$-central-charge. *The Heisenberg sub-VOA's level
$k$ encodes only the spin-1 contribution; the Virasoro central
charge of the full $W_{1+\infty}$ is built from the entire tower.*

### §2.3 Mode algebra of $J(z)$

Expand $J(z) = \sum_n J_n z^{-n - 1}$. The Heisenberg OPE gives the
mode algebra
$$
[J_m, J_n] \;=\; m \cdot k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2) \cdot \delta_{m + n, 0} \cdot K,
$$
where $K$ is the central element (in the universal enveloping
algebra of $\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}_1$). At fixed
$(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$, this is the **standard infinite-dimensional
Heisenberg / oscillator algebra** $\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}_1$ with
level $k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$.

### §2.4 Verdict on T2

**P4-EXEC-G4-M2-T2 verdict.** The Heisenberg sub-VOA
$W_{1+\infty}^{\mathrm{Heis}} \subset
W_{1+\infty}(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$ has

| Object | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Generator | $J(z)$ of conformal weight $1$ |
| OPE | $J(z) J(w) = k / (z-w)^2 + \mathrm{reg.}$ |
| Heisenberg level | $k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2) = -(\epsilon_1 + \epsilon_2)/(\epsilon_1 \epsilon_2)$ |
| Mode algebra | $[J_m, J_n] = m \cdot k \cdot \delta_{m+n, 0} K$ |
| Conformal vector | $L(z) = (2k)^{-1} :J(z)^2:$ (Sugawara, $c_{\mathrm{Sug}} = 1$) |

**Confidence.** High (Schiffmann–Vasserot normalisation is standard;
S_3-symmetric form $k = \epsilon_3/(\epsilon_1 \epsilon_2)$ is
canonical in the affine Yangian dictionary).

**Caveat: alternative normalisations.** Some sources (Costello
1610.04144 §6 in different normalisation) write the level as
$k' = 1/(\epsilon_1 \epsilon_2)$, with the $-(\epsilon_1+\epsilon_2)$
absorbed elsewhere. The cohomological *class* extracted by
$\tau_{\mathfrak h}$ is *normalisation-independent* (any rescaling of
$J(z)$ rescales $k$ but preserves the cohomology class up to a
1-coboundary). The numerical value of $k$ at the diagonal $\lambda$
limit depends on the normalisation; the *equivalence class* does
not. Both candidates give the manuscript's class up to a unit
factor.

## §3. T3 — The twist functor $\tau_{\mathfrak h}$

### §3.1 What the toy needs to do

Per M1 §3.4 / §5.4 corrections:
- The naive "kill $L_{-2}$" is wrong: $L_{-2}$ generates the whole
  of Vir as an ideal, leaving only $\langle L_{-1}, L_0, K \rangle$.
- The right interpretation: **forget the entire Virasoro module
  structure** while retaining the Heisenberg generator $J(z)$.
- The Heisenberg modes inherit a Lie 2-cocycle class given by the
  level $k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$.
- The retained datum is the $\hbar$-deformed Poisson bracket on the
  Heisenberg modes, with $\hbar^2 = \epsilon_1 \epsilon_2$ at the
  rebasing morphism (M1 H-P4-EXEC-CGW-C).

### §3.2 The candidate categorical structures

Three candidate categorical structures for $\tau_{\mathfrak h}$
(in increasing order of structural strength):

#### Candidate (i): Forgetful functor VOA → vertex Lie algebra

**Construction.** $\tau_{\mathfrak h}^{\mathrm{(i)}} :
\mathrm{VOA}_{\mathrm{conf}} \to \mathrm{VertLieAlg}$, the standard
forgetful functor sending a conformal VOA $V$ to its underlying
vertex Lie algebra (Frenkel-Reshetikhin, "Vertex Lie algebras" 2003).
This is well-defined: a vertex Lie algebra is a $V$ equipped with
the singular part of the OPE only (the λ-bracket), discarding the
*conformal* (= Virasoro module) and the *associative*
(= normally-ordered product) structures.

**Action on Heisenberg.** The Heisenberg VOA has λ-bracket
$[J_\lambda J] = k$ (a constant in $\lambda$, since $k/(z-w)^2$ is
the entire singular OPE). The forgetful functor retains this
λ-bracket and discards the Sugawara conformal vector and the
normally-ordered product.

**Verdict.** **PARTIAL FIT.** Candidate (i) achieves "forget the
conformal vector"; achieves "retain Heisenberg λ-bracket"; does *not*
naturally implement the topological-localisation (the result is a
*vertex Lie algebra*, not a *topological* chiral algebra on $\R$).
The locally-constant-on-$\R$ structure must be added separately.

#### Candidate (ii): Lurie HA §5.5.4.16 / §5.5.4.10 reduction

**Construction.** Per W31 §4.4 / P4-G4-T2.2: $\tau_{\mathfrak h}^{\mathrm{(ii)}}$
is the Bousfield localisation in the Lurie HA §5.5.4 framework,
operating on the holomorphic-factorization $\infty$-category. The
W-class of morphisms inverted is "forget the Virasoro module
structure"; the result lands in
$\mathrm{Fact}^{\mathrm{lc}}(\R; \mathcal C) \simeq
\mathrm{Alg}_{E_1}(\mathcal C)$ where $\mathcal C$ is the target
$\infty$-category of $\hbar$-deformed associative algebras.

**Lurie HA §5.5.4.16 vs §5.5.4.10.** The cited theorem in W3-W11
table T1 §1 is **5.5.4.10**: $\mathrm{Alg}_{E_n}(\mathcal C)
\xrightarrow{\sim} \mathrm{Fact}^{\mathrm{lc}}(\R^n; \mathcal C)$ on
the locally constant subcategory. The user's task mentions
"§5.5.4.16 Dunn additivity reduction"; **this is a refinement, not
the primary tool**: §5.5.4.16 is Dunn additivity for $E_{n+m}$ as
$E_n$-tensor-$E_m$ structures, used in the multiplicative refinement
of the locally-constant equivalence. For the toy (1-direction $\R$),
**§5.5.4.10 is the operative theorem** (Dunn additivity is irrelevant
at $n = 1$). M1 used §5.5.4 imprecisely; M2 sharpens to §5.5.4.10.

**Action on Heisenberg.** $\tau_{\mathfrak h}^{\mathrm{(ii)}}$ sends
$W_{1+\infty}^{\mathrm{Heis}}$ (a conformal VOA, equivalently a
locally-constant factorization algebra on $\R \times \C$ in the
holomorphic+conformal sense) to its image under the forgetful
functor $\mathrm{Fact}^{\mathrm{lc,conf}}(\R \times \C; \mathcal C)
\to \mathrm{Fact}^{\mathrm{lc,top}}(\R; \mathcal C)$. The
$\C$-direction is integrated out; the resulting object is a
locally-constant FA on $\R$ valued in $\mathcal C = \widehat{\mathfrak{gl}_1}$-modules
at level $k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$.

**Verdict.** **STRONG FIT.** Candidate (ii) achieves all three:
- forgets the conformal vector (the $\C$-direction is integrated out;
  the result has no $T(z)$);
- retains $J(z)$ as the Heisenberg current along $\R$;
- equips $J$-modes with the Heisenberg λ-bracket where the level
  $k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$ encodes the $\hbar$-deformation
  (rebasing fixes $\hbar^2 = \epsilon_1 \epsilon_2$).

The presentability hypothesis (W3-W11-01 ML qualifier) is satisfied
because the Heisenberg sub-VOA is a *strict ML colimit* of finite-
dimensional Heisenberg pieces (windowed by mode level $|n| \leq N$).
The locally-constant structure on $\R$ is verified because the
factorization algebra on $\R \times \C$ at level $k$ is
*translation-invariant* in the $\R$-direction (the level is a
constant, not $z$-dependent).

#### Candidate (iii): Quotient by conformal-vector ideal

**Construction.** $\tau_{\mathfrak h}^{\mathrm{(iii)}}(V) := V /
\langle \omega \rangle$, where $\omega = L(-2)\mathbf 1$ is the
conformal vector and $\langle \omega \rangle$ is the VOA-ideal it
generates.

**Action on Heisenberg.** The Sugawara conformal vector $\omega =
(2k)^{-1}\!:\!J(-1)^2\!:\!\mathbf 1$ is *not* itself zero, but the
ideal $\langle \omega \rangle$ generated by it is **the entire VOA**
(because $L_{-1} = J_{-1}$-shift, etc., propagates $\omega$ to all
modes). **Candidate (iii) collapses to zero.**

**Verdict.** **FAIL.** This is the same naive failure as M1's
"kill $L_{-2}$" diagnosis. Quotienting by the conformal-vector
ideal kills the entire algebra structure. Candidate (iii) is
discarded.

## §4. T4 — Lie 2-cocycle extraction

### §4.1 The Heisenberg 2-cocycle

The mode algebra $[J_m, J_n] = m \cdot k \cdot \delta_{m+n, 0} \cdot K$
is a *central extension* of the abelian Lie algebra
$\C\{J_n : n \in \Z\}$ by the **Heisenberg 2-cocycle**
$$
\omega_{\mathrm{Heis}}(J_m, J_n) \;=\; m \delta_{m + n, 0}.
$$
(The factor $k$ is the *central charge*; the *cocycle* is the integer
$m \delta_{m+n, 0}$ up to overall normalisation.) This is the
universal Heisenberg cocycle on the loop algebra
$L\mathfrak{gl}_1 = \C[t, t^{-1}]$, well-known from
Pressley-Segal *Loop Groups* and Kac *Infinite Dimensional Lie
Algebras*.

### §4.2 Cohomology class $[\omega_{\mathrm{Heis}}]$

The Heisenberg 2-cocycle is **non-trivial**: $H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}
(L\mathfrak{gl}_1; \C)$ is 1-dimensional (Pressley-Segal §4.2),
spanned by $[\omega_{\mathrm{Heis}}]$. The level $k(\epsilon_1,
\epsilon_2)$ is the *coefficient* of the central extension along
this 1-dimensional $H^2$.

After $\tau_{\mathfrak h}$, the Heisenberg level becomes the
**Lie 2-cocycle class with coefficient** $k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2)$:
$$
[c^{(1)}](\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2) \;=\; k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2) \cdot [\omega_{\mathrm{Heis}}]
\;\in\; H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}\bigl(\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}_1; \C\bigr).
$$

### §4.3 Match to manuscript's $[\bar c]$

The manuscript's central charge is (W3-W11-05, principles.tex:280;
W31 §3.1):
$$
[\bar c] \;\in\; H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A; \C)_{(0, 0)},
\qquad \bar A = \C[z_1, z_2] / \C \cdot 1,
$$
with cocycle representative
$$
\bar c(z_1^a z_2^b, z_1^c z_2^d) \;=\; (a d - b c) \cdot \mathbf 1_{(a + c, b + d) = (1, 1)}
$$
(W3-W11 line 695; antisymmetric, weight-$(1, 1)$).

The Heisenberg sub-algebra of $\bar A$ on the weight-$(1, 1)$
sector is generated by the pair $(z_1, z_2)$:
- $\{z_1, z_2\} = (1)(1) - (0)(0) = 1$ in the Poisson bracket
  $\{f, g\} = \partial_1 f \cdot \partial_2 g - \partial_2 f \cdot \partial_1 g$;
- the central extension by $\bar c(z_1, z_2) = 1$ is exactly the
  3-dimensional Heisenberg algebra $\langle z_1, z_2, K \rangle$
  with $[z_1, z_2] = K$.

This is the **finite-dimensional Heisenberg algebra**
$\mathfrak{heis}_3$ at the canonical level $1$.

**The match P4-EXEC-G4-M2-T4.**

| Side | Object | Cocycle |
|------|--------|---------|
| Manuscript | $\bar A$ on weight-$(1,1)$ | $\bar c(z_1, z_2) = 1$ |
| CGW $\tau_{\mathfrak h}$-image, $\lambda = 1$ rebased | $\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}_1$ | $k_{\hbar = 1} \cdot [\omega_{\mathrm{Heis}}]$ |

For the match to hold, the rebased Heisenberg level
$k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2 = \epsilon_1)$ at the diagonal scaling
$\lambda = 1$ should equal the manuscript's normalisation $1$ (or
its corresponding canonical normalisation).

### §4.4 Computation of $k$ at $\lambda = 1$

With the Schiffmann–Vasserot Heisenberg level
$$
k(\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2) \;=\; -\frac{\epsilon_1 + \epsilon_2}{\epsilon_1 \epsilon_2},
$$
specialise along $\epsilon_2 = \lambda \epsilon_1$:
$$
k(\epsilon_1, \lambda \epsilon_1) \;=\; -\frac{(1 + \lambda) \epsilon_1}{\lambda \epsilon_1^2}
\;=\; -\frac{1 + \lambda}{\lambda \epsilon_1}.
$$

This is **divergent** as $\epsilon_1 \to 0$ (linear pole). Naively
the toy fails. The **regularised limit** uses the rebasing
$\hbar^2 = \epsilon_1 \epsilon_2$, equivalently
$\epsilon_1^2 = \hbar^2 / \lambda$ at fixed $\lambda$:
$$
k(\hbar, \lambda) \;=\; -\frac{(1 + \lambda) \sqrt{\hbar^2 / \lambda}}{\hbar^2}
\;=\; -\frac{1 + \lambda}{\sqrt \lambda \cdot \hbar}.
$$

This is *still* divergent in $\hbar \to 0$. The naive toy with
the Schiffmann–Vasserot $S_3$-symmetric normalisation **does NOT**
produce a finite cocycle at $\hbar \to 0$.
