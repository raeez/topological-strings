## Claim Attacked

Claim: the manuscript has theorem-grade primary support for the
Loday--Quillen--Tsygan comparison

```text
Prim H_*^CE(gl_infty(A); C) = HC_{*-1}(A)
```

and for the connected Hopf-algebra formulation

```text
H_*^CE(gl_infty(A); C) = S(HC_{*-1}(A)).
```

## Finding

The source core is valid with a precise boundary.

Loday--Quillen 1984, Theorem 6.2, p. 584, proved on p. 586, gives the exact
homological shift

```text
Prim H_n(gl(A)) = HC_{n-1}(A)
```

for a unital associative algebra over a characteristic-zero field. This directly
supports `main.tex:1764-1778` after specializing the field to `C`.

The Hopf-algebra form in `main.tex:1767-1772` is not the literal theorem
wording, but follows from the connected graded Hopf algebra structure recorded
in LQ84 and the characteristic-zero Milnor--Moore/free-commutative Hopf
algebra consequence for graded-commutative primitives.

LQ84 Theorem 6.9, pp. 587--588, gives a classical homological-degree stable
range for `H_i(gl_n(A))`. It does not prove the manuscript's finite-window
word-degree map in `theorem-lanes.tex:2079-2088`.

Tsygan 1983 is a primary independent precursor: Theorem 1 and Corollary 1 on
p. 198 identify stable matrix Lie algebra homology coalgebras with the
coalgebra generated by cyclic-invariant functors. It is not the clean source
for the manuscript's exact `HC_{*-1}` notation; LQ84 is.

## Non-Support Boundary

The LQT sources do not prove:

- the finite-window map
  `lambda_{LQT,W}: Prim C_*^CE(gl_N(A_{psi,W})) -> CC_red(A_{psi,W})[1]`;
- dg extension for `A_psi = T(x,y,psi), d psi = xy-yx`;
- connected single-trace/scalar quotient compatibility;
- the Dirac/Koszul Hamiltonian trace theorem;
- the `Psi/rho` bridge, open `A_infinity` transfer, cyclic BV pairing, or
  cone/Roos obstruction comparison;
- Costello graph/QME or brane-defect local field theory results.

These remain internal theorem obligations.
