## §0. Method

The Beilinson lens reads the rigidity proof as a sheaf computation
on a toric base. The principal-part module $\mathcal{P}$ is a
$T^2$-equivariant skyscraper (graded) on the formal disk $\Spf
R$; the rigidity is forced by toric isotypic uniqueness plus
one-parameter propagation. The Boundary lens looks for: degenerate
strata where the rigidity argument touches zero (the socle line
$\C \rho_{0,0}$, the constant Hamiltonian, the row $b = -1$, the
indices $a-p+1 = 0$ or $b-q+1 = 0$); behaviour at the boundary
between the formal symplectic group and the volume-preserving
group SDiff; and the failure of column-Verma covariance under
quadratic Symp$_{\mathrm{form}}$ from W26.

**Master goal.** Decide what hypotheses D₂ and D₃ actually need
and where, and identify each silent strengthening or load-bearing
choice.

## §1. T1 — Verbatim D₂ statement and hypothesis package

D₂ is the **rigidity** half of M-28. In W2-08 it is named
"Residue-pairing rigidity, T²-Cartan form" and formally split off from
the unified `thm:principal-part-coadjoint-uniqueness`. Currently the
manuscript carries it as the existing `thm:canonical-residue-pairing`
at `main.tex` lines 3838-3906 and as
`thm:app-matlis-residue-rigidity` at `appendix-matlis-principal-parts.tex`

### §1.1 Verbatim statement (`main.tex`:3838-3845)

> **Theorem (Residue pairing, normalized up to scalar).** Up to a
> nonzero scalar, the residue pairing
> $$\langle\,\cdot\,,\,\cdot\,\rangle:
>   \mc P\times(\C[[z_1,z_2]]/\C\cdot1)\longrightarrow\C,\qquad
>   \langle\rho_{a,b},[z_1^mz_2^n]\rangle=\delta_{a,m}\delta_{b,n},$$
> is the unique non-degenerate continuous $\mathfrak h$-equivariant
> pairing graded of total degree zero with the property that every
> element of $\mc P=\operatorname{Ann}(\C\cdot1)$ annihilates the
> constant line.

### §1.2 Hypothesis package (extracted from proof at lines 3848-3906)

H1. **Source datum.** $R = \C[[z_1, z_2]]$, $\mathfrak m = (z_1, z_2)$,
   $\mathfrak h = R / \C \cdot 1$ with bracket the Poisson bracket
   modulo constants.
H2. **Volume datum.** $\Omega = dz_1 \wedge dz_2$, fixed once and for
   all (used in `prop:principal-part-coadjoint`:3795 and in the
   integration-by-parts identity at line 3853-3856; the form is
   embedded in $\rho_{a,b} = z_1^{-a-1} z_2^{-b-1} dz_1 dz_2$).
H3. **Bigrading.** $\mathcal{P}$ is the bigraded sum of one-dimensional
   weight spaces $\C \rho_{a,b}$ for $(a, b) \in \Z_{\geq 0}^2$,
   $a + b > 0$; the "polynomial side"
   $\C[[z_1, z_2]] / \C \cdot 1$ is bigraded by total Taylor degree.
H4. **Pairing $\beta$ candidate.** Continuous; bilinear; total-degree
   zero ($\beta_{a,b;m,n} = 0$ unless $a + b = m + n$); $\mathfrak
   h$-equivariant for the coadjoint action; non-degenerate;
   annihilates $\C \cdot 1$ on the polynomial slot.
H5. **Three-element rigidity probe.** The proof uses only:
   $z_1 z_2 \in \mathfrak h$ (T²-Cartan), $z_1, z_2 \in \mathfrak h$
   (the two linear unipotents).
H6. **Constant-Hamiltonian removal.** $\rho_{0,0} \notin \mathcal{P}$;
   equivalently $\beta$ kills the scalar functional.

### §1.3 What is genuinely required (and what is not)

The **truly required hypotheses** are H1, H2, H3, H4, H5; H6 is
already encoded in the definition of $\mathcal{P}$. Notably absent
from what the proof actually invokes:

- **Symp$_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$-equivariance** is *not* used in
  the proof. The proof needs only T²-Cartan equivariance (single
  element $z_1 z_2$ acting via diagonal weight) plus equivariance
  for the two linear elements.
- **Full $\mathfrak h$-equivariance** is over-stated as a hypothesis:
  what the proof uses is "T²-Cartan plus two linear elements"
  equivariance, which is the toric Borel of GL$_2$ inside Symp
  intersected with the linear locus (W26's 3-dim solvable Borel
  $\langle z_1, z_2, z_1 z_2 \rangle$). See §5.1 below for the
  W26 cross-link.
- **Schur on irreducible $\mathfrak h$-modules** is not used (M-06,
  W2-01, W2-02 already removed this language).

## §2. T2 — D₂ proof internals; simplest case; dim_C = 2 specifics

### §2.1 Hand-verification at the simplest non-trivial case

Take $(a, b; m, n) = (1, 0; 0, 1)$, so $a + b = 1 = m + n$. Total
degree zero passes. Now apply the diagonal weight constraint.
Hamiltonian $z_1 z_2$ acts on $\rho_{a, b}$ by $a - b$, on
$z_1^m z_2^n$ by $n - m$. Equivariance:
$\beta(\{z_1 z_2, \rho_{1,0}\}, z_2)
   = -\beta(\rho_{1,0}, \{z_1 z_2, z_2\})$, i.e.
$1 \cdot \beta_{1,0;0,1} = -1 \cdot \beta_{1,0;0,1}$, hence
$\beta_{1,0;0,1} = 0$.

So at the off-diagonal $(1,0;0,1)$ the pairing is forced to zero by
the *single* diagonal Cartan element $z_1 z_2$. **No further data
needed at this off-diagonal cell.**

For the diagonal cell $(1, 0; 1, 0)$, both T² constraints are
automatic ($a-b = m-n = 1$, $a+b = m+n = 1$); the rigidity argument
must invoke a *linear* element. Use $z_1$ on $\rho_{1, 0}$:
$z_1 \cdot \rho_{1, 0} = -1 \cdot \rho_{1, 1}$; $\{z_1, z_1^1 z_2^0\} = 0$
so the equivariance constraint at $(1, 0; 1, 0)$ via $z_1$ reads
$-1 \cdot \beta_{1, 1; 1, 0} = 0$. This kills $\beta_{1, 1; 1, 0}$
(an off-diagonal cell). For propagating $c_{a, b}$ along the column,
use $\{z_1, z_1^m z_2^{n-1}\} = (n - 1) z_1^m z_2^{n-2}$ at
$(m, n) = (1, 1)$, $(a, b+1) = (1, 1)$, source cell $(a, b) = (1, 0)$:
the equivariance reads $-(b+1) \beta_{a, b+1; m, n}
   = -n \beta_{a, b; m, n-1}$ i.e. $-1 \beta_{1, 1; 1, 1}
   = -1 \beta_{1, 0; 1, 0}$, hence $c_{1, 1} = c_{1, 0}$.

So already at total degree 1 + 1 = 2 the linear element $z_1$
propagates $c_{1, 0}$ to $c_{1, 1}$. Symmetrically $z_2$ propagates
to $c_{2, 0}$. These two propagations cover the entire $a + b > 0$
lattice via a connected walk, forcing all $c_{a, b}$ to a single
scalar $c$.

**Concrete-case verdict.** D₂'s proof at the smallest case is
verified. The rigidity goes through exactly as stated.

### §2.2 Why dim_C = 2 specifically

The proof uses three ingredients tied to dim_C = 2:

(D2-S1) **The Cartan torus has rank 2.** With $T^2 \subset \mathrm{GL}_2$
   acting with character $(a, b)$ on $\rho_{a, b}$ and $(m, n)$ on
   $z_1^m z_2^n$, total-degree-zero equivariance gives $a + b = m + n$
   (one constraint). The diagonal Cartan $z_1 z_2$ gives $a - b = m - n$
   (one more constraint). Together: $(a, b) = (m, n)$. **In
   $\dim_{\C} = n$, the Cartan torus is $T^n$ and the graded weight
   spaces of $\rho_{\vec a, \vec b}$ are still 1-dimensional, so the
   same argument extends — but only after one identifies the right
   $n$ (or $n+1$?) commuting elements playing the role of $z_1 z_2$.**
   The naive lift uses the $n$ rank-1 quadratics
   $z_i z_{n+i}$ (in symplectic $\C^{2n}$, or
   $z_{2k-1} z_{2k}$); whether these alone suffice is a non-trivial
   question. **Severity 2 silent strengthening if elevated to
   dim > 2 without justification.**

(D2-S2) **The volume form equals the symplectic form.** In
   $\dim_{\C} = 2$, $\Omega = dz_1 \wedge dz_2 = \omega$. So
   Hamiltonian vector fields preserve $\Omega$ automatically. The
   integration-by-parts identity
   $\langle \{f, \rho\}, g \rangle = -\langle \rho, \{f, g\} \rangle$
   used in the existence half of D₂ (line 3853-3856) requires only
   $\mathcal L_{X_f} \Omega = 0$, which is automatic in dim 2.
   **In dim_C = 2n with $n \geq 2$, $\Omega = \omega^n / n!$ and
   $\mathcal L_{X_f} \Omega = 0$ remains automatic** (Hamiltonian
   vector fields are volume-preserving in any symplectic dim), so
   this is not actually a dim-2 obstruction. The dim-2 specificity
   here is *cleanliness*, not *correctness*.

(D2-S3) **The bigraded lattice walk is connected.** The
   $\mathfrak m^N$-adic filtration and the Cartan weight
   $(a, b)$-grading together cover $\Z_{\geq 0}^2 \setminus \{(0,0)\}$
   by the linear walks generated by $z_1$ ($b \mapsto b+1$) and $z_2$
   ($a \mapsto a+1$). In dim 2 these two walks visit every nonzero
   weight cell. **In dim 2n with $n \geq 2$, the analogous walks use
   $z_i$ generators and connect $(\vec a, \vec b) \to (\vec a + \vec
   e_i, \vec b)$; but the analogue of the Cartan eigenvalue might be
   only one quadratic combination $\sum_i z_{2i-1} z_{2i}$, rather
   than $n$ distinct ones, leaving non-trivial bigrading orbits.**
   Severity-2 silent strengthening: dim-2 has full toric coverage; in
   higher dim some toric weights collide.

**Verdict on dim-2 specificity.** D2-S1 (Cartan rank 2 fully
distinguishes weights) is the *load-bearing* dim-2 ingredient. D2-S2
is automatic in any symplectic dim. D2-S3 is dim-dependent. Net: D₂
generalises to higher even dim *only* with a proof of toric weight
distinguishability and walk-connectivity, both of which require an
explicit Hartshorne-style argument that the manuscript correctly
omits as Phase-4 (W2-08 residual 1, M-28 residual).

equivariance under the *full* infinite-dimensional Lie algebra
$\mathfrak h = \C[[z_1, z_2]] / \C$.
**Broken step.** What the proof actually uses is equivariance under
the 3-element subset $\{z_1 z_2, z_1, z_2\}$, i.e. the 3-dim
solvable Borel $\mathfrak{b} = \langle z_1, z_2, z_1 z_2 \rangle$
of W26 (W26-01: $[H, X] = X$, $[H, Y] = -Y$, $[X, Y] = 0

because once T²-Cartan plus linears determine $\beta$ up to a single
scalar, the higher Hamiltonians automatically respect $\beta$ by
the existence half (integration by parts under $\Omega = dz_1 dz_2$).
But the *minimum* hypothesis required for uniqueness is
$\mathfrak{b}$-equivariance.
**Sharpening proposal.** Two options:
(option A, clean) Replace "$\mathfrak h$-equivariant" by
"$\mathfrak{b}$-equivariant" where $\mathfrak{b}$ is the 3-dim
solvable Borel. This makes the rigidity statement *strictly
sharper* and matches W26's column-Verma classification exactly:
both D₂ uniqueness and the column-Verma decomposition live in
the GL$_2 \times T^2$ functorial subcategory.
(option B, status-quo with footnote) Keep "$\mathfrak h$-equivariant"
but add a footnote: "the proof uses only equivariance for $z_1 z_2$,
$z_1$, $z_2$, i.e. for the 3-dim solvable Borel
$\mathfrak{b} = \langle z_1, z_2, z_1 z_2 \rangle \subset \mathfrak h$."
**Adjudication.** Valid silent strengthening. The current statement
is *not wrong*, but a sharper statement is available.
**Deciding evidence.** §2.1 proof structure; W26-01 Borel structure
constants.

## §3. T3 — Verbatim D₃ statement and hypothesis package

D₃ is the **no-go**. Currently the manuscript has it in two layers:
the categorical version `thm:no-polynomial-realization-categorical`
at `main.tex`:4148, and its appendix counterpart
`thm:app-matlis-no-polynomial-realization` at
`appendix-matlis-principal-parts.tex`:208-216.

Wave-2 W2 interpretation of D₃ is "PBW symbol covariance" interpreted as the
*directional* statement: the symbol/PBW polynomial side has its own
Hamiltonian action (raising), the principal-part side has the
coadjoint action (lowering), and these cannot be intertwined by any
relabelling on the polynomial side that keeps locally nilpotent
linear Hamiltonians.

### §3.1 Verbatim statement (`main.tex`:4148-4172)

> **Theorem (Categorical no-go: principal parts have no polynomial
> realisation).** Let $\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$ denote the abelian
> category of $\Z$-graded bimodules $M = \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} M_n$
> over the polynomial Hamiltonian Lie algebra $\bar A = \C[z_1, z_2] / \C \cdot 1$
> on which (1) every element acts as a Hamiltonian vector field;
> (2) every linear Hamiltonian acts locally nilpotently. Then
> $\mc P \notin \operatorname{Ob}(\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}})$. More
> precisely, every nonzero submodule $M \subset \mc P$ in the larger
> category of all graded Hamiltonian Lie modules contains a generator
> $\rho_{a, b}$ on which both $z_1$ and $z_2$ act
> non-locally-nilpotently, hence $M$ violates condition (2).
> Consequently $\Hom_{\mathrm{Lie\text{-}Mod}_{\bar A}}(\mc P, M) = 0$
> for every $M \in \operatorname{Ob}(\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}})$, and
> the inclusion $\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}} \hookrightarrow
> \mathrm{Lie\text{-}Mod}_{\bar A}$ has no left adjoint sending
> $\mc P$ into $\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$.

### §3.2 Hypothesis package

H1'. **Source datum.** $\bar A = \C[z_1, z_2] / \C \cdot 1$, the
   polynomial (not formal) Hamiltonian Lie algebra.
H2'. **Symbol/PBW shadow datum.** "$\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$"
   = abelian category of $\Z$-graded bimodules of $\bar A$ with
   (a) elements act as Hamiltonian vector fields, (b) linear
   Hamiltonians act locally nilpotently.
H3'. **Target.** $\mathcal{P}$ as defined by D₁'s Matlis identification
   (so D₁ gates D₃, per W4-03).
H4'. **No-go conclusion.** $\Hom_{\mathrm{Lie\text{-}Mod}}(\mc P, M)
   = 0$ for every $M \in \mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$.

## §4. T4 — D₃ on (z_1, z_2) ↦ (z_1, z_2 + ε z_1²): hand-verification

### §4.1 The substitution

Take $\varphi: (z_1, z_2) \mapsto (z_1, z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2)$ for
$\varepsilon \in \C$ (W26-08; per W26 §4.1, this is the simplest
non-linear formal symplectomorphism — verified by
$\varphi^* (dz_1 \wedge dz_2) = dz_1 \wedge (dz_2 + 2 z_1 \varepsilon
dz_1) = dz_1 \wedge dz_2$).

### §4.2 Effect on the polynomial side $\bar A$

Pull-back of generators:
$\varphi^* z_1 = z_1$, $\varphi^* z_2 = z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2$.

So:
$\varphi^* (z_1) = z_1$ (preserved);
$\varphi^* (z_2) = z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2$ (shifted by a quadratic).

**Locality of nilpotence.** On any polynomial $f \in \bar A$, the
linear Hamiltonian $z_1$ acts via $X_{z_1} = \partial_{z_2}$, which is
*locally nilpotent* on every polynomial: for any $f$ of degree
$\leq d$ in $z_2$, $z_1^{d+1} \cdot f = \partial_{z_2}^{d+1} f = 0$.
Now $\varphi^* z_1 = z_1$ keeps the same Hamiltonian vector field
$X_{z_1} = \partial_{z_2}$ (since the substitution leaves $z_1$
invariant), so local nilpotence is preserved for $z_1$.

But $\varphi^* z_2 = z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2$, and the corresponding
Hamiltonian vector field is $X_{z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2}
   = X_{z_2} + \varepsilon X_{z_1^2}
   = -\partial_{z_1} + \varepsilon (2 z_1 z_2 \partial_{z_2}
      - z_1^2 \partial_{z_1}) \cdot (\text{wait})$.

Recompute: $X_f = (\partial_{z_1} f) \partial_{z_2}
   - (\partial_{z_2} f) \partial_{z_1}$. So
$X_{z_2} = (\partial_{z_1} z_2) \partial_{z_2}
   - (\partial_{z_2} z_2) \partial_{z_1} = 0 - \partial_{z_1}
   = -\partial_{z_1}$, OK.
$X_{z_1^2} = (\partial_{z_1} z_1^2) \partial_{z_2}
   - (\partial_{z_2} z_1^2) \partial_{z_1}
   = 2 z_1 \partial_{z_2} - 0
   = 2 z_1 \partial_{z_2}$.

So $X_{z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2} = -\partial_{z_1} + 2 \varepsilon z_1 \partial_{z_2}$.

This is **still locally nilpotent on polynomials** in the strong sense:
- $\partial_{z_1}$ is locally nilpotent on any polynomial in $z_1$;
- $z_1 \partial_{z_2}$ is locally nilpotent on any polynomial in $z_2$;
- their sum acts on $\C[z_1, z_2]$ and a polynomial of total degree
  $d$ is killed by some power of $X_{z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2}$
  (each application either reduces $z_1$-degree by 1 or moves a
  $z_2$-power into a $z_1 \cdot z_2^{n-1}$ term, but the total degree
  is preserved by *both* terms separately — actually:
  $-\partial_{z_1}$ reduces $z_1$-degree by 1, and
  $2 \varepsilon z_1 \partial_{z_2}$ increases $z_1$-degree by 1
  while reducing $z_2$-degree by 1. Net: total degree is preserved
  by $2 \varepsilon z_1 \partial_{z_2}$ but reduced by 1 by
  $-\partial_{z_1}$. After enough iterations, total degree is
  reduced sufficiently that $\partial_{z_1}^N$ kills.)

Wait — more carefully: a polynomial $f$ of total degree $\leq d$
has at most $d$ powers of $z_1 z_2$-monomials. Each iteration of
$X_{z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2}$ sends $f$ to a polynomial of total
degree at most $d - 1$ if the $-\partial_{z_1}$ term wins, else
unchanged total degree. **But.** $2 \varepsilon z_1 \partial_{z_2}$
applied to $f$ gives a polynomial of the *same* total degree, so
strict total-degree reduction is not guaranteed at each step.
However, $z_2$-degree is reduced by 1 by $2 \varepsilon z_1 \partial_{z_2}$
and unchanged by $-\partial_{z_1}$; so iterating $z_2$-degree is
non-increasing and *strictly* decreasing whenever the second term
fires. After at most $d$ firings of the second term, the $z_2$-degree
reaches 0; then the operator reduces to $-\partial_{z_1}$ which is
locally nilpotent in $z_1$. Net: $X_{z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2}$ is
**locally nilpotent on $\bar A$**.

Verdict on the polynomial side: $\varphi^* \bar A = \bar A$, both
linear Hamiltonians remain locally nilpotent, and the category
$\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$ is preserved by $\varphi^*$.

### §4.3 Effect on the principal-part side $\mathcal{P}$

By W26-08: $\varphi^* (v_{0, -1}) = (z_2 + \varepsilon z_1^2)^{-1}$
expanded as the formal series
$$
\varphi^* (v_{0, -1}) = \sum_{k \geq 0} (-\varepsilon)^k v_{2k, -1-k}
   = \rho_{0,0} - \varepsilon \rho_{2,1} + \varepsilon^2 \rho_{4,2}
      - \varepsilon^3 \rho_{6,3} + \cdots
$$
with the convention identifying $v_{a, b} = z_1^a z_2^b$ for
$a \geq 0, b \leq -1$ with $\rho_{a, -b-1}$ after multiplying by
$dz_1 dz_2$. (Re-indexing W26's $v_{a,b} \leftrightarrow$
$\rho_{a', b'}$ with $a' = a, b' = -b - 1$: W26 uses
$v_{a,b} = z_1^a z_2^b$ in the bi-infinite parent; for $a \geq 0,
b \leq -1$ this corresponds to $\rho_{a, -b-1}$ after multiplication
by $dz_1 dz_2$ and translation.)

Critically: **this is an infinite formal series spanning columns
$\{M_0, M_2, M_4, \ldots\}$ of W26.** It is in the Tate completion
$\widehat{\mathcal{P}}$ (formal infinite sums) but **not in the
discrete sum $\mathcal{P} = \bigoplus_{a, b} \C \rho_{a, b}$**.

So $\varphi^*$ sends $\mathcal{P}$ to a *strict superset* — the Tate
completion $\widehat{\mathcal{P}}$ — under quadratic
symplectomorphisms.

### §4.4 The non-trivial "covariance" computation

Now check D₃ under $\varphi^*$.

**Polynomial side claim** (covariance of $\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$):
$\varphi^* \mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}} \subseteq \mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$,
verified in §4.2.

**Principal-part side claim** (covariance of $\mathcal{P}$):
$\varphi^* \mathcal{P} \subseteq \mathcal{P}$, **FAILS**. The image is
in $\widehat{\mathcal{P}}$ only. Equivalently, $\mathcal{P}$ is not
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-stable as a discrete graded vector
space.

**No-go covariance claim** (D₃ under $\varphi^*$):
The statement "$\Hom_{\bar A}(\mathcal{P}, M) = 0$ for $M \in
\mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$" passes to "$\Hom_{\bar A}(\varphi^*
\mathcal{P}, \varphi^* M) = 0$ for $M \in \mathsf P_{\mathrm{pol}}$"
by functoriality of Hom; but $\varphi^* \mathcal{P}$ is no longer
$\mathcal{P}$ — it is in $\widehat{\mathcal{P}}$.

So **D₃ is covariant under $\varphi^*$ only after passing to the
formal completion of the principal-part module**: the no-go
conclusion is preserved (the local-nilpotence obstruction does
not depend on whether one works in $\mathcal{P}$ or
$\widehat{\mathcal{P}}$, since iterated $z_1^N$ on any nonzero
$\rho_{a, b}$ gives nonzero $\rho_{a, b+N}$). But the **module
$\mathcal{P}$ itself is not preserved**, so the "no left adjoint"
clause needs reformulation.

## §5. T5 — Interaction with W26's quadratic-symplectomorphism breakage

### §5.1 W26's column-Verma vs W33's D₂

W26 establishes: $C^{+-} = \bigoplus_{a \geq 0} M_a$ as a column-Verma

This is not coincidence. The 3-dim solvable Borel $\mathfrak{b}$
is the **unique** sub-Lie-algebra of $\mathfrak h$ in dim 2 with:
- a rank-1 Cartan ($H = z_1 z_2$);
- two abelian unipotent generators ($X = z_2$, $Y = z_1$, with
  $[X, Y] = 0 \pmod{\C}$, the $a + b$ "ax + b" structure);
- the $T^2$-toric weight structure that distinguishes

**Implication for D₂.** D₂'s rigidity is Symp$_{\mathrm{form}}$-covariant
*only* in the same scope as W26's column-Verma: linear GL$_2$ + diagonal

*infinite-dimensional family of column-mixed pairings*: under $\varphi$,
the rigid pairing $\langle \rho_{a, b}, z_1^m z_2^n \rangle =
c \delta_{a, m} \delta_{b, n}$ becomes
$\varphi^* \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle = c \cdot$ (composition with
formal-power-series basis change), which mixes $(a, b)$-cells via the
$\sum_k (-\varepsilon)^k$ series.

The question: does $\varphi^* \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$ pass D₂'s
rigidity hypotheses (continuous, total-degree-zero, $\mathfrak h$-equivariant)?
- Continuous: YES (automatic for formal completion).
- Total-degree-zero: YES (preserved by formal symplectomorphism).
- $\mathfrak h$-equivariant: only after re-bracketing $\mathfrak h$
  via $\varphi^*$, which is a Lie-algebra automorphism of $\mathfrak h$.
- *In the Tate-completed module $\widehat{\mathcal P}$*: yes, the
  pulled-back pairing is still a candidate for D₂'s uniqueness, *if*
  we replace the discrete $\mathcal P$ with $\widehat{\mathcal P}$.

So the *natural* statement of D₂ in the
Symp$_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariant setting requires Tate completion
on both sides, exactly as W26 demands for column-Verma.
