## Status

The all-bidegree radial/Weyl identity was not proved in this pass.  The
structural part that does close is the ordinary necklace-cycle mechanism:
over \(\mathbb Q\), the cycle space of the two-colour necklace graph is
generated by adjacent-swap square cells after passing to cyclic words.
Thus the missing quantum correction can be expressed through a cellular
decorated Hodge problem, not only through a pivot-gauge matrix solve.

The remaining unproved statement is the decorated PBW Stokes identity
\[
  \lambda(E^+_{a,b})=\phi_\lambda(T_{a,b})
\]
for every decorated dual row.  No finite computation produced a violating
row.  If the identity fails, the obstruction is exactly a left-cokernel
functional in the cellular decorated complex, equivalently a signed row in
\(\ker B_{a,b}^*\).

## 1. Object

Fix \(a,b\geq1\).  Let
\[
  \mathsf W_{a,b}=\mathbb Q\langle X,Y\rangle_{a-1,b-1},
  \qquad
  \mathsf V_{a,b}=\mathsf{Cyc}_{a,b}.
\]
The classical incidence map is
\[
  \partial W=[WYX]-[WXY].
\]
Let \(\mathsf D^+_{a,b}\) be the positive free indexed normal-diagram
target and
\[
  C^+_{a,b}(W)=
  \pi_+\mathcal N_{\mathrm{free}}\operatorname{Tr}(WM),
  \qquad
  M=YX-XY+\hbar NI.
\]
Put
\[
  B_{a,b}(W)=(\partial W,C^+_{a,b}(W)).
\]
The target is
\[
  \eta_{a,b}=(T_{a,b},E^+_{a,b}),
\]
where
\[
  T_{a,b}=
  \sum_{j=0}^{b}[Y^jX^aY^{b-j}]
  -{b+1\over\binom{a+b}{a}}
  \sum_{U\in\operatorname{Sh}(a,b)}[U]
\]
and \(E^+_{a,b}=\pi_+\mathcal N_{\mathrm{free}}(E_{a,b,N})\).
The all-bidegree identity in bidegree \((a,b)\) is
\[
  \eta_{a,b}\in\operatorname{im}B_{a,b}.
\]

After a classical lift \(\partial c^{\mathrm{cl}}=T_{a,b}\), the same
condition is
\[
  R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b}:=
  E^+_{a,b}-C^+_{a,b}(c^{\mathrm{cl}})
  \in
  \operatorname{im}\left(C^+_{a,b}|_{\ker\partial}\right).
\]

## 2. Structural Cycle Mechanism

Define the based shuffle graph \(\widetilde G_{a,b}\).  Its vertices are
based words with \(a\) letters \(X\) and \(b\) letters \(Y\).  Its edges
are adjacent swaps \(uXYv\to uYXv\).  Add a square cell for every pair of
commuting adjacent swaps:
\[
  uXYvXYw,\quad
  uYXvXYw,\quad
  uYXvYXw,\quad
  uXYvYXw .
\]
Let \(C^{\square}_{\bullet}(a,b)\) be the rational cellular chain complex
obtained after quotienting by cyclic rotation.

**Lemma.**  The map
\[
  \partial_2:C^{\square}_2(a,b)\to C^{\square}_1(a,b)
\]
surjects onto \(\ker\partial\subset\mathsf W_{a,b}\).

**Proof.**  In the based model, a binary word is the same as an order
ideal in the rectangle poset \([a]\times[b]\), via its inversion set.
Adjacent swaps are toggles of a single extremal box.  The cubical complex
of the distributive lattice \(J([a]\times[b])\), with cubes generated by
commuting toggles, is contractible; in particular its one-cycles are
generated by square boundaries.  Cyclic rotation is a finite cellular
action.  Over \(\mathbb Q\), transfer identifies rational homology of the
quotient with the invariant rational homology upstairs.  Hence
\[
  H_1(C^{\square}_{\bullet}(a,b);\mathbb Q)=0,
\]
so every necklace cycle lies in the image of \(\partial_2\).

Thus every correction \(K\in\ker\partial\) may be represented as
\[
  K=\partial_2 H
\]
for a rational square-cell chain \(H\).  Define the cellular decorated map
\[
  D^{\square}_{a,b}:=
  C^+_{a,b}\partial_2:
  C^{\square}_2(a,b)\longrightarrow\mathsf D^+_{a,b}.
\]
The desired quantum correction is equivalently
\[
  D^{\square}_{a,b}H_{a,b}=R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b}.
\]

This is the structural Hodge/PBW mechanism.  It turns the pivot-gauge
kernel correction into a square-cell transgression.  The edge strip is the
one-dimensional degeneration of this mechanism; the \(K_{4,4}\) correction
is the first nontrivial square-cell transgression.

## 3. Hodge Form

Give \(C^{\square}_2(a,b)\) and \(\mathsf D^+_{a,b}\) the basis
inner products.  Let
\[
  \Delta^{\square}_{a,b}
  =
  D^{\square}_{a,b}(D^{\square}_{a,b})^*,
  \qquad
  \mathsf H^{\square}_{a,b}
  =
  \ker(D^{\square}_{a,b})^*.
\]
Let \(\Pi^{\square}_{a,b}\) be projection to
\(\mathsf H^{\square}_{a,b}\).  Then
\[
  R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b}\in\operatorname{im}D^{\square}_{a,b}
  \Longleftrightarrow
  \Pi^{\square}_{a,b}R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b}=0.
\]
When this holds, a canonical square-cell correction is
\[
  H_{a,b}
  =
  (D^{\square}_{a,b})^*
  (\Delta^{\square}_{a,b})^{-1}R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b},
  \qquad
  K_{a,b}=\partial_2H_{a,b},
\]
with the inverse on \(\operatorname{im}D^{\square}_{a,b}\).

This is an exact finite-dimensional Hodge theorem.  It does not prove that
the harmonic projection vanishes in all bidegrees.

## 4. Failed Proof Step

The missing all-bidegree theorem is the decorated PBW Stokes identity.
Equivalently, for every \(\lambda\in(\mathsf D^+_{a,b})^*\) satisfying
\[
  \lambda D^{\square}_{a,b}=0,
\]
one must prove
\[
  \lambda(R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b})=0.
\]
In potential form this says: if
\[
  W\longmapsto\lambda C^+_{a,b}(W)
\]
has zero periods on the necklace graph and therefore equals
\[
  \phi_\lambda([WYX])-\phi_\lambda([WXY]),
\]
then
\[
  \lambda(E^+_{a,b})=\phi_\lambda(T_{a,b}).
\]

The edge proof closes because each PBW column has the four-term form
\[
  \Theta_r-\Theta_{r+1}-\hbar\Phi_r+\hbar\Phi_{b-1-r},
\]
and the Moyal target is the same discrete derivative with linear weight.
For \(a,b\geq3\), PBW matchings may identify nonadjacent vertices and split
traces.  The Capelli scalar term cancels the adjacent-crossing endpoint
family, but no formula was constructed assigning every positive diagram
stratum to a square-cell Stokes boundary with the required Moyal odd
coefficient.  The computed \(K_{5,5}\) and \(K_{6,6}\) are certificates, not
natural square-cell formulas.

Thus the all-bidegree identity is still open.  The obstruction theorem
below is the correct insertion if the PBW Stokes identity is not supplied.

## 5. Exact Obstruction Theorem

**Theorem.**  For fixed \((a,b)\), the following are equivalent.

1. The free indexed radial/Weyl quantum shear primitive exists in bidegree
   \((a,b)\).
2. \(\eta_{a,b}\in\operatorname{im}B_{a,b}\).
3. \(R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b}\in\operatorname{im}D^{\square}_{a,b}\).
4. \(\Pi^{\square}_{a,b}R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b}=0\).
5. Every \(\lambda\in(\mathsf D^+_{a,b})^*\) with
   \[
     \lambda D^{\square}_{a,b}=0
   \]
   satisfies
   \[
     \lambda(R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b})=0.
   \]
6. Equivalently, every signed row
   \[
     (\phi,-\lambda)\in\ker B_{a,b}^*
   \]
   satisfies
   \[
     \lambda(E^+_{a,b})=\phi(T_{a,b}).
   \]

If these conditions fail, the exact obstruction is a row
\[
  \lambda\in\ker(D^{\square}_{a,b})^*
\]
with
\[
  \lambda(R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b})\ne0,
\]
equivalently a potential row
\[
  (\phi,-\lambda)\in\ker B_{a,b}^*
\]
with
\[
  \lambda(E^+_{a,b})-\phi(T_{a,b})\ne0.
\]
After rescaling, the obstruction value may be normalized to \(1\).

**Proof.**  The equivalence of (1) and (2) is the definition of the free
normal-diagram moment-ideal primitive in this bidegree.  Choosing
\(\partial c^{\mathrm{cl}}=T_{a,b}\) changes \(\eta_{a,b}\) by
\(B_{a,b}(c^{\mathrm{cl}})\), leaving the residual
\((0,R^{\mathrm{free}}_{a,b})\).  By the square-cycle lemma,
\(\ker\partial=\operatorname{im}\partial_2\), so
\[
  \operatorname{im}(C^+_{a,b}|_{\ker\partial})
  =
  \operatorname{im}D^{\square}_{a,b}.
\]
This proves (2) iff (3).  The finite-dimensional Hodge decomposition for
\(D^{\square}_{a,b}\) proves (3), (4), and (5) equivalent.  Finally,
\((\phi,-\lambda)\in\ker B^*_{a,b}\) is precisely
\[
  \lambda C^+_{a,b}(W)=\phi(\partial W)
  \qquad(W\in\mathsf W_{a,b}).
\]
Evaluating this row on \(\eta_{a,b}\) gives
\[
  \phi(T_{a,b})-\lambda(E^+_{a,b}).
\]
Therefore every such row annihilates \(\eta_{a,b}\) iff the potential
identity holds.  A nonzero value is the dual representative of the cokernel
class.
