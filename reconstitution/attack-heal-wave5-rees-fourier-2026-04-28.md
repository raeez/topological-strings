## Claim Attacked

Attack target: the shared indices on the polynomial one-\(\psi\)
descendants \(\Psi_{a,b}\) and the Matlis principal parts
\(\rho_{a,b}\) might be promoted to an ordinary
\(\mathfrak h\)-module identification, perhaps by saying
``Rees'', ``Fourier'', or ``\(D_\hbar\)''.

Status: same-action identification is false. A limited
Fourier--Rees label bridge is true after changing polarization and
recording the Rees degree factor. It is not an
\(\mathfrak h\)-module bridge.

## Contradiction Search

Polynomial descendant action:
\[
  z_1\cdot\Psi_{a,b}=b\,\Psi_{a,b-1},\qquad
  z_2\cdot\Psi_{a,b}=-a\,\Psi_{a-1,b}
\]
after scalar reduction. Thus \(z_1,z_2\) act locally nilpotently on
every ordinary polynomial descendant vector.

Matlis coadjoint action:
\[
  z_1\cdot\rho_{a,b}=-(b+1)\rho_{a,b+1},\qquad
  z_2\cdot\rho_{a,b}=(a+1)\rho_{a+1,b}.
\]
Neither linear Hamiltonian is locally nilpotent on any nonzero element
of \(\mathcal P\). Local nilpotence is invariant under module
isomorphism and survives extension to \(\C((\hbar))\) when the same
Hamiltonian action is kept.

Fourier attack: for the Rees Weyl algebra
\[
  \mathcal D_\hbar=
  \C[[\hbar]]\langle z_i,\partial_i\rangle/
  ([\partial_i,z_j]=\hbar\delta_{ij}),
\]
the Fourier automorphism
\[
  \mathsf F(z_i)=\partial_i,\qquad \mathsf F(\partial_i)=-z_i
\]
does identify the Fourier twist of
\(\mathcal O_\hbar=\C[z_1,z_2][[\hbar]]\) with the delta/local
cohomology module
\[
  \Delta_\hbar=H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2[[\hbar]].
\]
It sends labels by
\[
  z_1^az_2^b\mapsto
  \partial_1^a\partial_2^b\rho_{0,0}
  =(-1)^{a+b}\hbar^{a+b}a!b!\rho_{a,b}.
\]
This is the honest bridge.

It does not preserve the Hamiltonian action. Since
\[
  X_{z_1}=\partial_2,\qquad X_{z_2}=-\partial_1,
\]
Fourier sends
\[
  X_{z_1}\mapsto -z_2,\qquad X_{z_2}\mapsto z_1.
\]
These transformed operators are locally nilpotent on the delta module.
The Matlis coadjoint action uses the original \(X_{z_i}\) on Laurent
principal parts, hence raises pole order and is not locally nilpotent.

## Exact Module Statements

True:
\[
  \mathsf F(\mathcal O_\hbar)\cong \Delta_\hbar
  \quad\text{as Fourier-twisted Rees Weyl modules}.
\]

True:
\[
  z_1^az_2^b \leftrightarrow
  (-1)^{a+b}\hbar^{a+b}a!b!\rho_{a,b}
\]
after Fourier twist; after inverting \(\hbar\) this gives a filtered
label bridge, and at \(\hbar=0\) only an associated-graded label match.

False:
\[
  \operatorname{span}\{\Psi_{a,b}\}\cong \mathcal P
\]
as modules for the same reduced Hamiltonian Lie algebra.

Obstructed:
any \(D_\hbar\), Fourier, or Rees bridge that keeps the original
\(\mathfrak h\)-action and identifies polynomial descendants with
Matlis principal parts. The obstruction is local nilpotence of
\(z_1,z_2\) on the polynomial side versus non-local-nilpotence on the
Matlis side.
