## Habitat

Let
\[
  A_\psi=T(x,y,\psi),\qquad d\psi=xy-yx,
\]
with
\[
  \operatorname{wt}(x)=(1,0),\qquad
  \operatorname{wt}(y)=(0,1),\qquad
  \operatorname{wt}(\psi)=(1,1).
\]
Let \(A_{\psi,K}=A_\psi/F^{>K}A_\psi\), where \(F^{>K}\) is the two-sided dg
ideal generated by words of total Koszul weight \(>K\).  Let \(L\) bound CE
length.

Write \(T_{K,L}\) for the stable single-cycle chain complex in this window:
the image of the trace-cycle map
\[
  \Theta_{B,K,L}\colon
  CC_{\mathrm{red},\leq L}(A_{\psi,K})[1]
  \longrightarrow
  C_*^{\mathrm{CE}}(\mathfrak{gl}_B(A_{\psi,K}))
\]
for any \(B\geq B(K,L)\).  The trace-cycle formula is
\[
  \Theta_{B,K,L}(a_0|\cdots|a_r)
  =
  \sum_{i_0,\ldots,i_r=1}^{B}
  (E_{i_0i_1}a_0)\wedge\cdots\wedge(E_{i_ri_0}a_r),
  \qquad r+1\leq L.
\]
The sign convention is fixed by
\[
  d_{\mathrm{CE}}\Theta_1(\psi)=\Theta_1(xy-yx).
\]
With this convention the CE bracket on matrix units gives the reduced cyclic
differential.  This is a direct computation; no dg LQT theorem is imported.

The stable Hopf window is
\[
  \mathsf H^{\mathrm{st}}_{K,L}(A_\psi)
  =
  \bigoplus_{\substack{m\leq L\\\mathrm{wt}\leq K}}
  \mathbf S^m(T_{K,L}),
\]
with product induced by block sum and coproduct induced by the CE coproduct.
The Eulerian projection in this Hopf object is
\[
  P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}
  =
  \log^*(\mathrm{id})
  =
  \sum_{m=1}^{L}\frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{m}
  (\mathrm{id}-u\epsilon)^{*m}.
\]

## Separated Blocks

Set \(B=B(K,L)\) and \(M=LB\).  Decompose
\[
  \C^M=V_1\oplus\cdots\oplus V_L,\qquad \dim V_j=B.
\]
Let
\[
  \iota_j\colon \mathfrak{gl}_B(A_{\psi,K})
  \hookrightarrow
  \mathfrak{gl}_M(A_{\psi,K})
\]
be the \(j\)-th diagonal block inclusion, and put
\[
  \theta_j=\iota_{j,*}\Theta_{B,K,L}\colon T_{K,L}\to
  C_*^{\mathrm{CE}}(\mathfrak{gl}_M(A_{\psi,K})).
\]

Define the separated-block subcomplex
\[
  C^{\mathrm{sep}}_{M,K,L}
  \subset
  C^{\mathrm{CE}}_{*,\leq L}(\mathfrak{gl}_M(A_{\psi,K}))
\]
to be the span of wedges
\[
  \theta_{j_1}(z_1)\wedge\cdots\wedge\theta_{j_m}(z_m),
  \qquad
  0\leq m\leq L,
\]
with distinct block labels \(j_1,\ldots,j_m\), total CE length \(\leq L\),
and total Koszul weight \(\leq K\).  Since different diagonal block Lie
subalgebras commute, the CE differential has no cross-block bracket term on
this span.  Hence \(C^{\mathrm{sep}}_{M,K,L}\) is a subcomplex.  In the
stable range each \(\theta_j\) is injective on \(T_{K,L}\), and the CE complex
of the direct sum of the occupied block Lie algebras is the graded tensor
product of the block CE complexes.  Thus the only relations in this separated
span are the usual Koszul signs from permuting block factors.

## The Read-Back Map

Define
\[
  \alpha_{M,K,L}\colon C^{\mathrm{sep}}_{M,K,L}
  \longrightarrow
  \mathsf H^{\mathrm{st}}_{K,L}(A_\psi)
\]
by forgetting the block labels and multiplying the corresponding connected
stable factors:
\[
  \alpha_{M,K,L}
  \bigl(\theta_{j_1}(z_1)\wedge\cdots\wedge\theta_{j_m}(z_m)\bigr)
  =
  z_1\cdots z_m.
\]
The right side lies in the graded symmetric product.  If the wedge factors are
permuted, both sides change by the same Koszul sign.  Thus the formula is
well-defined on the separated CE span.

The map \(\alpha_{M,K,L}\) is a chain map.  The proof is the derivation
identity for the CE differential on a direct sum of commuting block
subalgebras:
\[
  d_{\mathrm{CE}}
  \bigl(\theta_{j_1}(z_1)\wedge\cdots\wedge\theta_{j_m}(z_m)\bigr)
  =
  \sum_q \pm
  \theta_{j_1}(z_1)\wedge\cdots\wedge
  \theta_{j_q}(d z_q)\wedge\cdots\wedge\theta_{j_m}(z_m).
\]
Applying \(\alpha\) gives the same signed Leibniz rule as the differential on
\(\mathbf S(T_{K,L})\).

## The Stabilization Map

For \(m\leq L\), let
\[
  (L)_m=L(L-1)\cdots(L-m+1)
\]
be the number of injections \(\{1,\ldots,m\}\hookrightarrow\{1,\ldots,L\}\),
with \((L)_0=1\).  Characteristic zero is used here.

Define
\[
  \beta_{M,K,L}\colon
  \mathsf H^{\mathrm{st}}_{K,L}(A_\psi)
  \longrightarrow
  C^{\mathrm{sep}}_{M,K,L}
\]
on a homogeneous symmetric monomial \(z_1\cdots z_m\) by
\[
  \beta_{M,K,L}(z_1\cdots z_m)
  =
  \frac{1}{(L)_m}
  \sum_{f:\{1,\ldots,m\}\hookrightarrow\{1,\ldots,L\}}
  \theta_{f(1)}(z_1)\wedge\cdots\wedge\theta_{f(m)}(z_m).
\]
The injection average is the necessary correction.  If two adjacent inputs
are exchanged, the source monomial changes by the graded symmetric sign and
the injection sum changes by the same sign after relabeling the injection.
Therefore \(\beta_{M,K,L}\) descends from \(T_{K,L}^{\otimes m}\) to
\(\mathbf S^m(T_{K,L})\).  The unsymmetrized first-\(m\)-blocks formula is
not well-defined on \(\mathbf S^m(T_{K,L})\); it is only an ordered-model
shortcut.

The map \(\beta_{M,K,L}\) is a chain map.  Each \(\theta_j\) is a chain map:
\(\Theta_{B,K,L}\) is the matrix-unit trace-cycle chain map and \(\iota_j\)
is a dg Lie algebra inclusion.  For distinct blocks, cross-brackets vanish,
so the wedge product of the \(\theta_{f(q)}(z_q)\) is a chain product.  A
finite average of chain maps is a chain map.

Finally,
\[
  \alpha_{M,K,L}\beta_{M,K,L}(z_1\cdots z_m)
  =
  \frac{1}{(L)_m}
  \sum_f z_1\cdots z_m
  =
  z_1\cdots z_m.
\]
Thus
\[
  \alpha_{M,K,L}\beta_{M,K,L}=\mathrm{id}_{\mathsf H^{\mathrm{st}}_{K,L}}.
\]
The reverse composite \(\beta\alpha\) is the projection from the labeled
separated-block span to the averaged separated representative.  Equivalently,
after passing to block-permutation coinvariants, \(\beta\alpha\) is the chosen
section-projection.

## The Idempotent

Since \(P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}\), \(\alpha\), and \(\beta\) are chain maps,
\[
  P^{\mathrm{sep}}_{M,K,L}
  =
  \beta_{M,K,L}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}\alpha_{M,K,L}
\]
is a chain map.  Since \(\alpha\beta=\mathrm{id}\) and
\(P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}^2=P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
  (P^{\mathrm{sep}}_{M,K,L})^2
  &=
  \beta P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}\alpha
  \beta P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}\alpha        \\
  &=
  \beta P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}^2\alpha       \\
  &=
  P^{\mathrm{sep}}_{M,K,L}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Its image is \(\beta(\operatorname{im}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}})\).  Since
\(\operatorname{im}P_{1\mathrm{cyc}}=T_{K,L}\), this is the averaged
separated single-cycle representative.  In the block-permutation coinvariant
formulation, it is exactly the separated single-cycle subspace.

## Stability Range

One occupied block must have size
\[
  B(K,L)=\max(K,L+2).
\]
The \(K\)-part is the stable trace-identity range used in
Proposition `prop:stable-trace-invariants`: Procesi 1976,
*The invariant theory of \(n\times n\) matrices*, together with Razmyslov
1974, *Trace identities of full matrix algebras over a field of characteristic
zero*, implies that no new trace relation of total positive word degree
\(\leq K\) appears once the matrix size is at least \(K\).

The \(L+2\)-part is the safe homological stabilization imported from
Loday--Quillen 1984, Theorem 6.9: the stabilization
\[
  H_i(\mathfrak{gl}_{n-1}(A))\to H_i(\mathfrak{gl}_{n}(A))
\]
is an isomorphism for \(i<n-1\).  For CE length at most \(L\), the safe
finite-window recording is \(n\geq L+2\).  The chain-level matrix-unit
injectivity of \(\Theta_{B,K,L}\) only needs enough distinct indices for
cycles of length \(\leq L\); \(B\geq L+2\) dominates that elementary bound.

There are at most \(L\) connected Hopf factors in a CE-length-\(\leq L\)
monomial.  Therefore \(M=L\,B(K,L)\) supplies enough disjoint stable blocks
for every term in the window.

## Attack--Heal Pass

1. Ordered-block ambiguity.  Attack: the unsymmetrized
   \(\beta(z_1\cdots z_m)=\theta_1(z_1)\wedge\cdots\wedge\theta_m(z_m)\)
   does not descend to the graded symmetric product.  Heal: average over all
   injections and divide by \((L)_m\).

2. Read-back ambiguity.  Attack: forgetting block labels could depend on the
   wedge order.  Heal: the target is graded symmetric; the Koszul sign from
   permuting CE wedge factors is the same sign used in
   \(\mathbf S(T_{K,L})\).

3. Chain-map defect.  Attack: CE differentials can bracket factors from
   different factors.  Heal: separated factors lie in distinct diagonal block
   Lie subalgebras, whose cross-brackets vanish.  The differential is the
   direct-sum CE differential.

4. Rank leakage.  Attack: \(M=L\max(K,L+2)\) might confuse total rank with
   stable rank per occupied block.  Heal: the stable rank is \(B(K,L)\) per
   occupied block; the total rank is \(M=LB(K,L)\) because at most \(L\)
   connected factors can appear.

5. Same-rank fold.  Attack: folding the separated blocks back into one
   same-rank block is not a chain map.  Status: not healed here; the exact
   obstruction remains
   \[
     \Gamma_N=d_{\mathrm{same}}F_N-F_Nd_{\oplus},
     \qquad
     \Gamma_N(\Theta_1^N(a)\wedge\Theta_1^N(b))
     =
     \pm\Theta_1^N(ab-(-1)^{|a||b|}ba).
   \]
   Thus the proved object is the separated-block zigzag, not a same-rank
   endomorphism.
