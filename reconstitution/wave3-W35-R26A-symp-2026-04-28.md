## §0. Method

The Beilinson lens demands a sheaf-theoretic / factorization-categorical
formulation: an action of a group $G$ on a module $M$ over a Lie
algebra $\mathfrak g$ is "$G$-natural" precisely when the data
$(\mathfrak g, M)$ extends to a $G$-equivariant quasi-coherent sheaf
on a moduli stack on which $G$ acts. In the present case, $G =
\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$ and the data is the column-Verma
$C^{+-}$ over $\bar A$. The W3-W26 finding is that no such $G$-equivariant
quasi-coherent sheaf exists at the classical Lie module level: the
quadratic symplectomorphism $\varphi: (z_1, z_2) \mapsto (z_1, z_2 + z_1^2)$
mixes columns by an infinite formal series.

The Hypotheses lens asks what hypothesis package is missing for
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-naturality. The classical Lie module
framework forces a linear substitution map (matrix substitution),
which is functorial only for $\mathrm{GL}_2$, not for the full
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$. A higher framework (Poisson vertex,
chiral, or factorization) admits formal-power-series substitutions
because the "OPE / $\lambda$-bracket" data carries $\partial_z$
derivatives and accommodates non-linear coordinate changes.

This W35 ledger is a structural reformulation, not a new theorem.
No new computational checks; the W26 / W21 / W11 / W31 numerical
evidence is taken as input.

## §1. T1 — Precise statement of R-W3-W26-A

### §1.1 Setup

Let $\bar A = \C[z_1, z_2] / \C \cdot 1$ denote the Hamiltonian
Poisson Lie algebra on the formal symplectic disk
$(\widehat{\C^2}_0, \omega = dz_1 \wedge dz_2)$ modulo constants.
Let $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$ denote the formal group
of formal symplectomorphisms of $(\widehat{\C^2}_0, \omega)$ fixing
the origin, equivalently the formal-group exponential of the
Hamiltonian Lie algebra $\bar A$ at the level of $\C[[z_1, z_2]]$.
Let $\widetilde{\mathcal M} = \C[z_1^{\pm 1}, z_2^{\pm 1}] / \C$ denote
the bi-infinite parent on $\Z^2 \setminus \{(0, 0)\}$ with the
W3-corrected Hamiltonian action
$z_1^p z_2^q \cdot v_{a, b} = (pb - qa) v_{a + p - 1, b + q - 1}$
(W3-W3-02; W12 verified at 165,600 commutators).

Let $C^{+-} = \{v_{a, b} : a \geq 0, b \leq -1\} \subset
\widetilde{\mathcal M}$ be the mixed cone. By W3-W21-T1 / W3-W26-T1,
$C^{+-}$ is the column-Verma decomposition of the 3-dim solvable
Borel $\mathfrak b = \langle z_1, z_2, z_1 z_2 \rangle \subset \bar A$,
with HWVs $v_{a, -1}$ at $a \geq 0$ and rising-factorial $z_1$-orbit.

### §1.2 Why $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$ is the natural target

The structural symmetry group of the formal symplectic disk is
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$. Each subgroup probes a
naturality class:
- $T^2 \subset \mathrm{GL}_2 \subset \mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$.
- The full Symp$_{\mathrm{form}}$ contains all formal Hamiltonian flows
  $\exp(\mathrm{ad}_{H})$ for $H \in \bar A$.

A naturality statement for the column-Verma decomposition under
$\mathrm{GL}_2 \times T^2$ alone does not suffice for the full
intrinsic geometry of the formal symplectic disk: the quadratic
symplectomorphism $\varphi: (z_1, z_2) \mapsto (z_1, z_2 + z_1^2)$
is an honest formal symplectomorphism (verified W3-W26-08:
$\varphi^* \omega = \omega$), and in the Beilinson worldview a
canonical structure on $(\widehat{\C^2}_0, \omega)$ must be
equivariant under all such flows.

### §1.3 The breakage (verbatim from W3-W26-08)

The action of $\varphi$ on the vacuum $v_{0, -1} = z_2^{-1}$ is
$$
\varphi(v_{0, -1}) = (z_2 + z_1^2)^{-1}
   = \sum_{k \geq 0} (-1)^k v_{2k, -1 - k}.
$$
This is a formal infinite sum across columns $\{M_0, M_2, M_4, \dots\}$
inside $C^{+-}$. The action **preserves the cone** $C^{+-}$ (each
term $v_{2k, -1 - k}$ has $a \geq 0$ and $b \leq -1$) but **breaks
the column-Verma decomposition**: the column index $a$ is mixed
across an infinite-dimensional family.

### §1.4 What "$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-natural realization" means

A $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-natural realization of $C^{+-}$
would be a structure $(\mathcal A, M)$ where:

1. **$\mathcal A$ is a $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariant
   chiral / factorization / vertex algebra structure on the formal
   symplectic disk** $\widehat{\C^2}_0$, in a category $\mathsf{Cat}$
   on which $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$ acts.

2. **$M$ is an $\mathcal A$-module** (chiral / factorization / vertex)
   that recovers $C^{+-}$ at the classical Lie module level under a
   specified classical-limit functor $\mathrm{cl}: \mathsf{Cat} \to
   \mathsf{Mod}(\bar A)$.

3. **For every $\varphi \in \mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$**,
   there is a canonical isomorphism $\varphi^* M \cong M$ in
   $\mathsf{Cat}$, satisfying the cocycle condition on composites.

In particular, the data $(\mathcal A, M, \{\varphi^* M \cong M\}_{\varphi})$
descends to a $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariant
quasi-coherent sheaf on a Drinfeld-type moduli stack
$\mathfrak M_{\mathrm{Symp}, \C^2, 0}$ classifying the data.

## §2. T2 — Poisson Vertex Algebra reformulation

### §2.1 PVA framework (Frenkel–Ben-Zvi citation)

The Poisson vertex algebra (PVA) framework — codified in Frenkel
and Ben-Zvi, *Vertex Algebras and Algebraic Curves*, AMS Mathematical
Surveys 88, 2nd ed. 2004, Chapter 16 (titled "Vertex Lie Algebras"
in the 2nd edition; equivalent to PVA at the classical level) and
Bakalov-Kac, *Comm. Math. Phys.* 240 (2003) 367-403 — provides a
framework where:

- **Underlying space $V$** is a $\C[\partial]$-module (left action
  by a derivation $\partial$, the formal infinitesimal translation).

- **$\lambda$-bracket** $[\cdot_\lambda \cdot]: V \otimes V \to
  \C[\lambda] \otimes V$ satisfies:
  - sesquilinearity: $[\partial a_\lambda b] = -\lambda [a_\lambda b]$,
    $[a_\lambda \partial b] = (\lambda + \partial) [a_\lambda b]$;
  - skew-symmetry: $[a_\lambda b] = -[b_{-\lambda - \partial} a]$;
  - Jacobi: $[a_\lambda [b_\mu c]] = [[a_\lambda b]_{\lambda + \mu} c]
    + [b_\mu [a_\lambda c]]$.

- **Compatibility with commutative product** (PVA case):
  $[a_\lambda bc] = [a_\lambda b] c + b [a_\lambda c]$ (Leibniz rule).

The classical-limit relation: a PVA $V$ with classical limit
functor $\mathrm{cl}: V \to \mathfrak g_V$ produces a Lie algebra
on $V / \partial V$ via the residue
$\{a, b\}_{\mathrm{Lie}} = \mathrm{Res}_{\lambda = 0}[a_\lambda b]
= [a_\lambda b]\big|_{\lambda = 0}$
(setting $\lambda = 0$ in the PVA $\lambda$-bracket recovers the
underlying Lie bracket on the quotient $V / \partial V$;
Frenkel–Ben-Zvi Proposition 16.3.10 (2nd ed.); equivalently the
"zeroth product" of the vertex algebra at the classical level).

The space $\bar A = \C[z_1, z_2] / \C \cdot 1$ is a Lie algebra
under the Poisson bracket $\{f, g\} = \partial_{z_1} f \cdot
\partial_{z_2} g - \partial_{z_2} f \cdot \partial_{z_1} g$ modulo
constants. The candidate PVA $\mathcal A$ would have $V / \partial V
= \bar A$ at the classical level.

### §2.2 The candidate PVA: chiral differentiation by a $\partial$ direction

The natural way to lift $\bar A$ to a PVA is to introduce a *chiral
direction*: a formal-disk coordinate $w$ (or, in BD framework, the
brane line direction $t$). The PVA $V$ is then
$$
V = \C[z_1^{(n)}, z_2^{(n)}; n \geq 0] / \C \cdot 1,
$$
the polynomial ring on the **jets** $z_i^{(n)} = \partial_w^n z_i$
of $z_i$ in the chiral direction, with $\partial = \partial_w$ acting
as $\partial z_i^{(n)} = z_i^{(n+1)}$.

The classical limit $V / \partial V$ is the Hamiltonian Poisson
algebra $\bar A = \C[z_1, z_2] / \C \cdot 1$ via the residue
$\mathrm{Res}_{w = 0}$.

The $\lambda$-bracket on $V$ is determined by:
$$
[(z_1)_\lambda z_2] = 1, \qquad [(z_2)_\lambda z_1] = -1, \qquad
[(z_i)_\lambda z_i] = 0,
$$
extending by sesquilinearity, Leibniz, and skew-symmetry. This is
the **Heisenberg PVA** generated by $z_1, z_2$ at the canonical
symplectic pairing, sometimes called the $\beta\gamma$-PVA when
expressed in conjugate coordinates. The classical limit recovers
the Poisson bracket $\{z_1, z_2\} = 1$ on $\bar A$ (after taking
residues).

### §2.3 The column-Verma in PVA language

The column-Verma $C^{+-}$ at the classical Lie level is generated
by $v_{0, -1} = z_2^{-1}$ as a cyclic $\bar A$-module (W3-W21-T1).
At the PVA level, the candidate lift is a **module $M$ over $V$** —
a $V$-PVA-module in the sense of Frenkel–Ben-Zvi §5.5 — such that:

- The state $|0, -1\rangle \in M$ is an analogue of $v_{0, -1}$, with
  $V$ acting via the $\lambda$-bracket;
- The PVA module structure encodes the action of all monomials
  $z_1^p z_2^q$ via the higher coefficients of the $\lambda$-bracket;
- $\partial$ acts on $M$ as the chiral translation, lifting the
  trivial classical action.

**Module $\lambda$-bracket structure.** A $V$-PVA-module $M$ carries
$Y_M(\cdot, \lambda): V \otimes M \to \C[\lambda] \otimes M$
satisfying analogous sesquilinearity / Jacobi axioms. The classical
limit at $\lambda = 0$ gives the Lie module structure on $M$ over
$V / \partial V = \bar A$. The PVA module structure encodes more
than just the classical action: it also tracks the chiral derivatives
$\partial M$ and their commutators with $V$.

**Central element / vacuum.** In analogy with the Wakimoto module
construction (W21 §2.1), the candidate module $M$ would have a
**vacuum state** $|0, -1\rangle$ killed by all "positive modes"
of $V$ (the $z_2^{(n)}$ for $n \geq 1$ and $z_1^{(n)}$ for $n \geq 1$
in the suitable triangular decomposition). The $\bar A$-Verma
structure on the classical limit recovers the column-Verma
decomposition.

### §2.4 Central element and the BD chiral central charge

W3-W11-05 names the BD chiral central charge $[\bar c] \in
H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A; \C)_{(0, 0)}$ corresponding to the
weight-(0,0) Lie 2-cocycle. In PVA language, this lifts to a
**central element** of the candidate PVA $V$ — a state $|c\rangle
\in V$ such that $[a_\lambda c] = 0$ for all $a \in V$. The central
element commutes with all PVA operators and acts diagonally on
modules.

**Identification with the Heisenberg PVA central charge.** The
Heisenberg PVA (with $\lambda$-bracket $[(z_1)_\lambda z_2] = 1$)
has trivial central element at the classical level (the constant
1 is in $\partial V$ via the residue, hence the cocycle is exact in
the PVA sense). The non-trivial $[\bar c]$ lift requires a
**deformed Heisenberg PVA** with $[(z_1)_\lambda z_2] = 1 + c \lambda$
(the $\lambda$-coefficient $c$ is the central charge), or
equivalently a $\beta\gamma$-system at non-trivial level.

This is the **PVA realization of the BD chiral central charge**:
the central element $|c\rangle \in V$ is the
$\lambda$-coefficient of $[(z_1)_\lambda z_2]$, with classical
limit $[\bar c] \in H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A; \C)_{(0, 0)}$ via
the residue / classical-limit functor.

**W3-W35-01 (PVA reformulation of column-Verma; status: structural
proposal, not theorem).** **Severity 3. Status: proposal. Confidence
medium.** The column-Verma module $C^{+-}$ admits a candidate PVA
lift $M$ over a deformed Heisenberg PVA $V$ on jet coordinates
$z_i^{(n)}$, with vacuum $|0, -1\rangle$ killed by positive modes,
classical limit recovering the column-Verma decomposition, and
central element corresponding to the BD chiral central charge
$[\bar c]$ (W3-W11-05). Construction of $M$ requires verifying the
PVA module axioms and identifying the explicit $\lambda$-bracket;
this is Phase-3 / Phase-4 frontier work.

## §3. T3 — $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariance in the PVA setting

### §3.1 What PVA-equivariance means

In the PVA framework, $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariance
means: for every $\varphi \in \mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$,
$\varphi$ acts on $V$ by an automorphism of the underlying
$\C[\partial]$-module compatible with the $\lambda$-bracket:
$$
[\varphi(a)_\lambda \varphi(b)] = \varphi([a_\lambda b])
\qquad \forall a, b \in V, \; \lambda \in \C.
$$
Equivalently, $\varphi: V \to V$ is a $\partial$-equivariant Lie
conformal algebra homomorphism.

The lift of the linear action to the jet coordinates: for
$\varphi(z_i) = \sum_n a_n z_1^n z_2^{m_n}$ a formal symplectomorphism,
the jet lift is
$$
\varphi(z_i^{(k)}) = \partial^k \varphi(z_i)
   = \sum_n a_n \partial^k (z_1^n z_2^{m_n}),
$$
expanded via Leibniz on the differential operator $\partial$ acting
on a polynomial in $z_1, z_2, z_1^{(1)}, z_2^{(1)}, \ldots$.

### §3.2 The simplest non-linear test

Take $\varphi: (z_1, z_2) \mapsto (z_1, z_2 + z_1^2)$ as in W3-W26-08.
The jet lift:
$$
\varphi(z_2^{(k)}) = \partial^k (z_2 + z_1^2)
   = z_2^{(k)} + \partial^k(z_1^2)
   = z_2^{(k)} + \sum_{j = 0}^{k} \binom{k}{j} z_1^{(j)} z_1^{(k - j)},
$$
the $k$-th symmetric Leibniz expansion. In particular:
- $\varphi(z_2) = z_2 + z_1^2$;
- $\varphi(z_2^{(1)}) = z_2^{(1)} + 2 z_1 z_1^{(1)}$;
- $\varphi(z_2^{(2)}) = z_2^{(2)} + 2 (z_1^{(1)})^2 + 2 z_1 z_1^{(2)}$.

### §3.3 Test: does the $\lambda$-bracket transform consistently?

Compute $[\varphi(z_1)_\lambda \varphi(z_2)]$:
$$
[(z_1)_\lambda (z_2 + z_1^2)]
   = [(z_1)_\lambda z_2] + [(z_1)_\lambda z_1^2]
   = 1 + 2 [(z_1)_\lambda z_1] \cdot z_1
   = 1 + 0 = 1,
$$
using Leibniz / the fact that $[(z_1)_\lambda z_1] = 0$ in the
Heisenberg PVA. So at the linear $\lambda$-bracket the
transformation is consistent: $[\varphi(z_1)_\lambda \varphi(z_2)]
= \varphi([(z_1)_\lambda z_2]) = \varphi(1) = 1$.

**This is the FIRST non-trivial check, and it passes**: the PVA
$\lambda$-bracket transforms consistently under $\varphi$ at the
top level of the Heisenberg generators.

### §3.4 Higher checks (sketched)

The full $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariance requires the
$\lambda$-bracket to transform consistently for **every pair of
elements of $V$**, including jets $z_i^{(k)}$ and products. Sketch
of further checks:

1. **$[\varphi(z_1)_\lambda \varphi(z_2^{(1)})]$ check.** Compute
   $[(z_1)_\lambda (z_2^{(1)} + 2 z_1 z_1^{(1)})]$. By sesquilinearity
   $[(z_1)_\lambda z_2^{(1)}] = (\lambda + \partial) [(z_1)_\lambda z_2]
   = (\lambda + \partial) \cdot 1 = \lambda$ (the $\partial$ of $1$ is
   zero). Thus $[(z_1)_\lambda z_2^{(1)}] = \lambda$. Similarly
   $[(z_1)_\lambda 2 z_1 z_1^{(1)}] = 2 [(z_1)_\lambda z_1] z_1^{(1)}
   + 2 z_1 [(z_1)_\lambda z_1^{(1)}] = 0 + 0 = 0$. So
   $[\varphi(z_1)_\lambda \varphi(z_2^{(1)})] = \lambda$.

   On the other side, $\varphi([(z_1)_\lambda z_2^{(1)}]) =
   \varphi(\lambda) = \lambda$ (a scalar).
   **Pass.**

2. **$[\varphi(z_2)_\lambda \varphi(z_2)]$ check** (skew-symmetry test).
   $[(z_2 + z_1^2)_\lambda (z_2 + z_1^2)]$ should equal zero by
   skew-symmetry $[a_\lambda a] = -[a_{-\lambda - \partial} a]$, which
   forces $[a_\lambda a]$ to be a $\lambda$-polynomial of restricted
   form. Computing:
   $[(z_2)_\lambda z_2] + 2 [(z_2)_\lambda z_1^2] + [(z_1^2)_\lambda z_1^2]$
   $= 0 + 2 \cdot 2 [(z_2)_\lambda z_1] z_1 + 4 z_1 [(z_1)_\lambda z_1] z_1$
   $= 0 + 4 \cdot (-1) \cdot z_1 + 0 = -4 z_1$.
   The term $-4 z_1$ should vanish by skew-symmetry; checking:
   $-[a_{-\lambda - \partial} a]\big|_{a = z_2 + z_1^2} = -(-4 z_1) = 4 z_1$ via
   the same expansion at $-\lambda - \partial$, but $-4 z_1$ is not in
   $\partial V$ generally. **This calculation suggests a subtlety**:
   skew-symmetry forces specific cancellations, and the apparent
   non-zero $-4 z_1$ must be reconciled with the involution
   $\partial$-shift.

   **Detailed check.** $-4 z_1$ is independent of $\lambda$, so under
   $\lambda \to -\lambda - \partial$ it remains $-4 z_1$. Skew-symmetry
   demands $[a_\lambda a] + [a_{-\lambda - \partial} a] = 0$, hence
   $-4 z_1 + (-4 z_1) = -8 z_1 = 0$ — which fails unless we are
   working modulo $\partial V$. Indeed, $z_1 \in \bar A$ but
   $\partial z_1 = z_1^{(1)} \in \partial V$; modulo $\partial V$,
   $z_1$ is a non-trivial element. So there is a problem.

   **Resolution.** The skew-symmetry of the PVA $\lambda$-bracket in
   the standard formulation is at the level of $V \otimes V \to
   V[\lambda]$, not modulo $\partial V$. The $z_1^2$ term is not in
   the natural PVA generated by $z_1, z_2$ alone with bracket
   $[(z_1)_\lambda z_2] = 1$ — that bracket is on
   *first-order* generators only, and $z_1^2$ is a quadratic
   product. The Leibniz extension of the $\lambda$-bracket to
   products via the PVA Leibniz rule produces:
   $[(z_2)_\lambda z_1^2] = [(z_2)_\lambda z_1] z_1 + z_1 [(z_2)_\lambda z_1]
   = (-1) z_1 + z_1 (-1) = -2 z_1$. So
   $[(z_2)_\lambda (z_2 + z_1^2)] = 0 + (-2 z_1) = -2 z_1$ (a single
   instance, not doubled). Now the skew-symmetry check:
   $[(z_2 + z_1^2)_\lambda (z_2 + z_1^2)] = [(z_2)_\lambda z_2] +
   [(z_2)_\lambda z_1^2] + [(z_1^2)_\lambda z_2] +
   [(z_1^2)_\lambda z_1^2]$. Each pairwise term:
   $[(z_2)_\lambda z_2] = 0$;
   $[(z_2)_\lambda z_1^2] = -2 z_1$;
   $[(z_1^2)_\lambda z_2] = z_1 [(z_1)_\lambda z_2] + [(z_1)_\lambda z_2] z_1
   = z_1 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot z_1 = 2 z_1$;
   $[(z_1^2)_\lambda z_1^2] = 0$ (Leibniz on each factor with
   $[(z_1)_\lambda z_1] = 0$).
   Sum: $0 + (-2 z_1) + 2 z_1 + 0 = 0$. **Pass.**

3. **$[\varphi(z_2)_\lambda \varphi(z_1)]$ check.**
   $[(z_2 + z_1^2)_\lambda z_1] = [(z_2)_\lambda z_1] + [(z_1^2)_\lambda z_1]$.
   $[(z_2)_\lambda z_1] = -1$.
   $[(z_1^2)_\lambda z_1] = z_1 [(z_1)_\lambda z_1] + [(z_1)_\lambda z_1] z_1
   = 0 + 0 = 0$.
   Sum: $-1$.
   On the other side $\varphi([(z_2)_\lambda z_1]) = \varphi(-1) = -1$.
   **Pass.**

### §3.5 The crucial insight

The PVA $\lambda$-bracket — equipped with sesquilinearity, Leibniz,
and skew-symmetry on a $\C[\partial]$-module — accommodates non-linear
substitutions like $z_2 \mapsto z_2 + z_1^2$ by absorbing the
non-linear part into the Leibniz expansion of the bracket. The
**failure mode** of W3-W26-08 (infinite-series mixing) is at the
classical Lie module level, where the substitution acts on
$z_2^{-1}$ via the geometric series expansion $(z_2 + z_1^2)^{-1}
= \sum_k (-z_1^2/z_2)^k z_2^{-1}$ — this expansion lives at the
level of inverse powers of $z_2$, which are not in the standard PVA
$V$ (which is polynomial). Inverse powers come from the **module
structure**, not the PVA itself.

### §3.6 Where the obstruction relocates

Hence the obstruction relocates: at the **PVA level $V$**,
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariance is **plausible** (the
checks in §3.3-§3.4 pass on the polynomial generators). At the
**module level $M$**, the inverse-power expansion of $\varphi$ on
the vacuum $v_{0, -1} = z_2^{-1}$ produces an infinite sum of
$M$-states, which must be interpreted as a **single PVA-module
endomorphism** rather than a finite linear combination.

This is the **R-W3-W26-A obstruction in PVA language**: not failure
of $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariance of the algebra,
but failure to interpret the formal infinite series at the module
level as a converging morphism in the chiral / module category.
**The discharge requires a topology on the module $M$ in which
$\sum_k (-1)^k v_{2k, -1 - k}$ is a converging element of $\widehat M$.**

**W3-W35-02 (PVA $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariance:
algebra-level plausible, module-level requires topology).**
**Severity 3. Status: structural finding, not theorem. Confidence
medium-high.** At the level of the candidate Heisenberg PVA $V$
on jet coordinates, the $\lambda$-bracket transforms consistently
under the simplest non-linear formal symplectomorphism
$\varphi: (z_1, z_2) \mapsto (z_1, z_2 + z_1^2)$ via Leibniz
expansion of the bracket on quadratic monomials. At the module
level, the infinite-series mixing observed in W3-W26-08 is the
formal power-series expansion $(z_2 + z_1^2)^{-1} = \sum_k
(-z_1^2/z_2)^k z_2^{-1}$, which lives in a **completion**
$\widehat M$ of the module $M$. Discharge of R-W3-W26-A in the
PVA framework requires (i) verifying that the algebra-level PVA
equivariance extends to all $\varphi \in \mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$,
not just the simplest quadratic; (ii) identifying the appropriate
topology / completion of the module $M$ in which $\varphi^* M$
converges.

## §4. T4 — Connection to BD chiral algebra (W3-W11-05)

### §4.1 W3-W11-05's BD chiral algebra

W3-W11-05 establishes that the manuscript's factorization centre is
a **topological chiral algebra** in the Beilinson-Drinfeld sense
(BD *Chiral Algebras*, AMS Colloquium 51, 2004, §3.3-§3.4):

- **Underlying**: locally constant factorization algebra
  $\mathcal F_{\mathrm{cl}}(I) = C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_I)$
  on the brane line $\R$, with $\mathfrak g_I = \mathfrak h_I
  \ltimes \mathcal P_I[1]$ and $\mathfrak h_I = \Omega^\bullet_c(I)
  \widehat\otimes \bar A$.

- **$\lambda$-bracket / OPE**: the $P_0$-bracket of
  `prop:app-factorization-principal-part-bracket`,
  $\{B_f(a), \Theta_\rho(B)\} = \Theta_{f \cdot \rho}(aB)$, etc.

- **Central charge**: the U(1)/Capelli class $[\bar c] \in
  H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A; \C)_{(0, 0)}$.

- **Universal property**: locally-constant factorization-algebra
  equivalence in Lurie HA 5.5.4.10 / CG Vol I §6.4.

### §4.2 Is $C^{+-}$ a module over this BD chiral algebra?

The natural question for R-W3-W26-A: **is the column-Verma $C^{+-}$
a module over the BD chiral algebra of W3-W11-05?**

**Setup.** A BD chiral algebra module on a curve $X$ is a
$\mathcal D$-module on $X$ together with a chiral action map
$\mu: j_* j^* (\mathcal A \boxtimes \mathcal M) \to \Delta_!
\mathcal M$ (BD §3.4.7). For $X = \R$ (the brane line), the chiral
algebra module specialises.

**Candidate module structure on $C^{+-}$.** The classical Lie module
structure on $C^{+-}$ over $\bar A$ lifts to a candidate chiral
module structure over $\mathcal F_{\mathrm{cl}}(I)$ as follows:

- At the cochain level, $C^{+-}$ inherits a $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g)$-module
  structure via the standard semi-cosimplicial extension: an
  $\mathfrak g$-module $C^{+-}$ produces $C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g; C^{+-})$,
  the CE cochain complex with values in $C^{+-}$.

- The locally-constant factorization-module structure on $\R$ is
  given by smearing: for $I \subset \R$ an open interval,
  $C^{+-}_I = \Omega^\bullet_c(I) \widehat\otimes C^{+-}$, with
  factorization product on disjoint intervals $I_1 \sqcup I_2 \subset I$
  given by the tensor product $\Omega^\bullet_c(I_1) \widehat\otimes
  \Omega^\bullet_c(I_2) \widehat\otimes (C^{+-})^{\otimes 2}$,
  composed with the multiplication on $\Omega^\bullet_c$ and the
  $\bar A$-action on $C^{+-}$.

This is the **standard chiral-module structure on a Lie module
induced from a Lie algebra factorization algebra**.

**W3-W35-03 (column-Verma as BD chiral module: structure exists,
naturality is the obstruction).** **Severity 2 (sharpening). Status:
structural sketch, not full proof. Confidence medium-high.** The
column-Verma $C^{+-}$ is canonically a chiral module over the
W3-W11-05 BD chiral algebra structure on the factorization centre
$\mathcal F_{\mathrm{cl}}$, via the standard induced semi-cosimplicial
structure. The **module structure is functorial under
$\mathrm{GL}_2 \times T^2$** matching the W3-W26-T1 functoriality
classification. **The module structure is NOT functorial under the
full $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$**, since the underlying Lie
module structure is not (W3-W26-08).

### §4.3 Does $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$ act on the BD chiral algebra?

For the BD chiral module structure on $C^{+-}$ to be
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariant, the BD chiral algebra
itself must carry a $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-action. This is
the question of whether **$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$
acts on the factorization centre $\mathcal F_{\mathrm{cl}}$** of the
local Hamiltonian model.

**Proposal.** The factorization centre $\mathcal F_{\mathrm{cl}}(I) =
C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_I)$ depends on
$\mathfrak g_I = \mathfrak h_I \ltimes \mathcal P_I[1]$ with
$\mathfrak h_I = \Omega^\bullet_c(I) \widehat\otimes \bar A$.
The space $\bar A$ carries a natural action of
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$ (since
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$ is the formal-group exponential of
$\bar A$). Hence $\mathfrak h_I$ inherits a
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-action via tensor with the trivial
action on $\Omega^\bullet_c(I)$. The CE cochain complex
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_I)$ is therefore
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariant **at the algebra level**.

**The R-W3-W26-A obstruction at the BD chiral level.** At the
**module level**, the column-Verma $C^{+-}$ is not
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariant by W3-W26-08. The
obstruction passes from classical Lie module to chiral module
verbatim: the BD chiral module structure on $C^{+-}$ inherits the
non-equivariance, since the chiral action is induced from the Lie
action.

**Discharge requires a different module realization** in which
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$ acts canonically — for example, a
Wakimoto-style $\beta\gamma$-realization in which the module is
given by Fock space on $\beta\gamma$-jets, and
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$ acts via formal-disk reparametrizations
of the jet coordinates.

### §4.4 The Ran-space descent connection

W3-W11-05 names the M-37 / R-03 transverse-ML residual as **Ran-space
descent** of the BD chiral algebra. The Ran space
$\mathrm{Ran}(\widehat{\C^2}_0)$ classifies finite collections of
points on the formal disk; a chiral algebra is, by BD §3.4, a
quasi-coherent sheaf on $\mathrm{Ran}$ with factorization compatibility.

**$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariance of the Ran-space
descent.** $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$ acts on
$\mathrm{Ran}(\widehat{\C^2}_0)$ (it preserves the formal disk).
A $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariant Ran-space descent
of the column-Verma data would give precisely the
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-natural realization sought in
R-W3-W26-A.

**W3-W35-04 (Ran-space descent as candidate
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-natural framework).** **Severity 2.
Status: structural proposal. Confidence medium.** The candidate
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-natural realization of the column-Verma
identification factors through Ran-space descent of the BD chiral
algebra: $\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$ acts on
$\mathrm{Ran}(\widehat{\C^2}_0)$, and a
$\mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}$-equivariant chiral module on the
Ran space recovers the module structure under classical limit.
Construction of the equivariant chiral module is the open question.

## §6. T6 — Three attack avenues

### §6.1 Path (A) — Direct PVA construction with explicit $\lambda$-bracket

**Statement.** Construct, by explicit formula, a Poisson vertex

   $(\varphi \circ \psi)^* M = \psi^* \varphi^* M$.

- For every $\varphi \in \mathrm{Symp}_{\mathrm{form}}(\C^2, 0)$,

The simplest non-linear test ($\varphi: (z_1, z_2) \mapsto

v_{2k, -1 - k}$ converges in $\widehat M$ and corresponds to a
