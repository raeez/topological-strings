## §1. Vector V1 — topological-twist obstruction

### §1.1 Wave-4 frame and the Witten 1988 question

Wave-4 §7.1 (Att-1 response) declares the manuscript's local mixed
model R²_top × C²_hol **does not** carry a worldsheet U(1)_R
N=2 superconformal R-symmetry (no `\hat c` charge, no chiral N=2
generators on the 2d closed-string sector); the Z/4 of Φ_Sergeev is
declared a *worldline* (1d) parastatistic R-symmetry on the brane
line R × p, not a worldsheet (2d) R-symmetry. The topological twist
of the closed-string sector on R² × C² is the Q-exact contraction
of topological time (`main.tex:589–601`); on R² this produces the
locally-constant factorization sector with Q acting trivially on
positive-jets along R².

**The probe.** Witten 1988's twist combines a U(1)_R rotation of the
worldsheet supercharges with the Lorentz rotation of the worldsheet
geometry. After the twist, the supercharge Q_+ becomes a scalar BRST
operator and only U(1)_R-invariant operators pass into the
topological sector. **In the present manuscript, the brane line
R × p is 1-dimensional**, so there is no 2d worldsheet on which to
twist; the topological twist of the closed-string sector on R² × C²
is purely the Q-exact contraction of topological time. The Z/4
R-symmetry on the brane line is not a Witten-1988-type twist of an
N=2 worldsheet R-symmetry.

### §1.2 The chain-level question that wave-4 implicitly handled

The question that survives the Att-1 response: **does the Z/4
R-symmetry on the brane Chan-Paton bundle commute with the brane-line
BV differential Q?** Concretely, Q is a chain-level operation on the
open-string field theory (Q ψ = [φ_1, φ_2] in the manuscript
convention); Φ_Sergeev = Ad_J acts on End(V) by conjugation with the
queer odd central element J. For the Z/4 to be a chain-level R-symmetry
of the brane-line theory, [Q, Ad_J] must vanish on the BV complex.

**Direct check.** On q(N) the bracket [-, -] is the q(N) super-bracket
inherited from gl(N|N). Conjugation Ad_J is an automorphism of
(q(N), [-, -]) **as an ungraded matrix algebra** (since J is in
q(N)). However J ∈ q(N)_{odd}, so Ad_J is a *parity-twisting* — i.e.,
parity-shifting — automorphism of the super-Lie structure: it maps
even bracket [x_0, y_0] to a sign-flipped image (`[Jx_0J^{-1}, Jy_0J^{-1}] = J [x_0, y_0] J^{-1}` matches as an ungraded bracket but
the parity assignment shifts). This is **precisely the (A5)-violating
parity automorphism** P^q : J → −J recorded in
G3-M3-queer-trace §3.3 and acknowledged by wave-4 (Z4-Brane-Physics
§A.4).

**Conclusion V1.** The topological twist of R² × C² (Q-exact
contraction of topological time on R²) does not obstruct Ad_J on
End(V). What blocks the chain-level R-symmetry is the **(A5)-violating
parity automorphism** P^q on q(N), already recorded in wave-4 as the
chain-level firewall (Sergeev-A5). **Wave-4 missed nothing at the V1
layer**; the relevant obstruction is the firewall, which wave-4
correctly identifies as Phase-5 chain-level work
(R-P4-EXEC-Z4-2). One narrative sharpening: wave-4 frames the
firewall as "blocks the chain-level lift" but does not explicitly
name the firewall as the topological-twist-compatible obstruction;
this is naming sharpness, not a missed obstruction.

**Severity at V1.** None. **No twist anomaly that wave-4 missed.**

### §2.2 The two automorphism classes — careful distinction

Two automorphism classes are conflated in the wave-4 narrative:

* **Class A — inner Hecke-Clifford permutation** Ad_σ (σ ∈ S_n
  swapping c_1 ↔ c_2). This is *even-parity*, in the inner
  automorphism group Inn(HC_n) ⊂ Aut(HC_n). **Inner even-parity
  automorphisms induce the identity on Lie superalgebra cohomology
  H^*_Lie(g; M) for g super-Lie and M g-equivariant** — this is the
  Cartan-Eilenberg identity adapted to super-setting. ✓ Wave-4's
  citation of this principle is correct for Class A.

* **Class B — parity-twist by J** Ad_J (J ∈ q(N)_{odd}). This is
  *odd-parity*. On a super-Lie cohomology group H^*_Lie(g; M), an
  odd-parity automorphism does **not** induce the identity; it
  induces a parity-twist (Π-shift on the coefficient). The general
  Cartan-Eilenberg argument requires an even-parity automorphism to
  produce trivial action on cohomology. **Class B is not covered by
  the principle wave-4 invokes.**

  Concretely on the manuscript's H^2_Lie(\bar A; C ⊕ ΠC), Ad_J acts
  by the parity-flip C ⊕ ΠC → ΠC ⊕ C on the coefficient module; the
  generator [\bar c] in the bosonic sector is **not** fixed by Ad_J
  but is fixed by Ad_J² (= Ad_{J²} = Ad_{−I} = identity since −I is
  central scalar). This is the *correct* wave-4 statement: bosonic
  Theorem G is fixed by Φ_Sergeev² = id, not by Φ_Sergeev itself.

### §2.3 The chain-level distinguishing observable

**Sharper question.** At the chain layer (where V^⊗n carries the
ρ(J)-action, not just the cohomology), is there a unitary boundary
observable distinguishing the +i and −i eigenspaces of ρ(J)?

The relevant observables are the (A5)-admissible boundary observables
of the manuscript: those compatible with the queer trace otr and
with the brane-line BV differential Q. The (A5)-admissibility class
is exactly the class respected by the queer-Capelli regulator and
preserved under change of admissible regulator (Polyakov-type

**Claim V2-A.** Under (A5)-admissible boundary observables alone,
the +i and −i eigensectors of ρ(J) on V^⊗n are **not distinguished**:
any (A5)-admissible boundary observable is built from the queer trace
otr and the (q(N), HC_n)-mutual centralizer pair, and therefore

✓ This refines wave-4's claim.

**Claim V2-B.** Under the (A5)-violating outer parity automorphism

and −i eigensectors **are distinguished**: P^q maps +i ↔ −i, so a

not "invisible under all unitary boundary observables"**.

**Wave-4 narrative gap.** The wave-4 framing "invisible under all

"invisible under (A5)-admissible boundary observables; visible only
to (A5)-violating P^q-equivariant observables, which do not enter

no inscribed theorem rests on the stronger statement, but the

unobservable in any sense.

### §2.4 Is this a chain-level R-anomaly?

**The Olive-Witten 1978 lens.** In Olive-Witten 1978 BPS Z/4, parity
P acts on the central charge Z by P : Z → \bar Z (complex
conjugation), exchanging Z → iZ → −Z → −iZ → Z. The product of
parity and R-symmetry generates a discrete Z_4 anomaly when both
fail to be implemented as quantum symmetries simultaneously.

In our setting: the Z/4 R-symmetry is generated by Φ_Sergeev =
Ad_J. Parity P^q acts as P^q J (P^q)^{-1} = −J (G3-M3 §3.3). So the
combination obeys
\[
   P^q \cdot \Phi_{\mathrm{Sergeev}} \cdot (P^q)^{-1} =
      \Phi_{\mathrm{Sergeev}}^{-1}
         \quad\text{(since Ad_{−J} = Ad_J^{-1})}.
\]
This is the **discrete chiral-parity anti-commutation relation**
{P, R}_{Z/4} = identity-on-Z/2: parity inverts R-symmetry. On the
±i eigenspaces this gives P^q : (+i) ↔ (−i), which is a **chain-level
R-anomaly** in the sense that R and P fail to commute as quantum
symmetries.

**However.** This anti-commutation is the **(A5)-violation already
recorded in the firewall typology** (Z4-Brane-Physics §A.4 anchor).
The manuscript's (A5)-admissible class is precisely the class that
quotients out the P^q-violation; in the (A5)-admissible sector the
discrete chiral anomaly does not appear because P^q is excluded
from admissible regulators. **At the inscribed theorem layer, no
chain-level R-anomaly distinguishes ±i** — the firewall handles it.

**Severity at V2.** **Severity 2 — narrative sharpening.** The
wave-4 narrative claim "invisible under all unitary boundary
observables" should read "invisible under (A5)-admissible boundary
observables" or "invisible at the cohomology / (A5)-admissible chain

### §3.2 Brundan-Kleshchev 2001 spin-Hecke-at-level-1 facts

**Key BK 2001 facts (cited from Theorem 8.13 and §7).**

* The affine Hecke-Clifford superalgebra H^c_n is generated by:
  * Clifford generators c_1, ..., c_n with c_i² = 1, c_i c_j = −c_j c_i
    for i ≠ j;
  * symmetric-group permutations σ_i (i = 1, ..., n−1) with
    σ_i² = 1 and braid relations;
  * polynomial generators X_1, ..., X_n that commute pairwise;
  * a level relation ϕ(X_i) = 0 where ϕ is a level-determining
    polynomial. **At level 1 (in the BK convention), the level
    polynomial is `X^4 − 1`**, i.e., X_i^4 = 1 on the level-1 module.
* On the level-1 spin module (the cyclotomic level-1 quotient
  H^c_n / ⟨X_1^4 − 1⟩), irreducibles are indexed by **strict
  partitions λ** of n with at most some bound depending on level;
  at level 1 the count is limited to strict partitions whose largest
  part is ≤ some level-dependent quantity, in particular **only
  strict partitions of restricted shape contribute**.
* Dimension of the spin irreducible L^c_λ at level 1: from the
  Sergeev-Howe duality V^⊗n = ⊕ L^q_λ ⊗ L^c_λ on V = C^{N|N},
  dim L^c_λ × dim L^q_λ = (multiplicity of λ in V^⊗n).

### §3.3 Smallest example test: N=2, n=2

**Setup.** V = C^{2|2}, dim V = 4. V^⊗2 has dim 16 = 4×4. Strict
partitions of n=2 are: λ = (2) (length 1, parts strict so 2 alone)
or λ = (1,1) **fails strictness** (repeated parts). So only λ = (2)
contributes; multiplicity of (2) in V^⊗2 is 1 (with appropriate
Sergeev-double-cover normalization).

**dim L^c_(2) at level 1 in BK.** From BK Table 8.13 / standard
spin-symmetric-group representation theory: the spin module of S_2
is 2-dimensional (the spin rep of the double cover, sgn-twisted) for
the strict partition (2). At level 1 of H^c_2, the spin module
inherits dim 2 with the t-generator (level-1 spin-permutation
generator) acting with eigenvalues ±i (the two square roots of −1
that resolve t² = ±1 → t^4 = 1).

**Cross-check against ρ(J) on V^⊗2.** From the verification script
(line 488–493):
\[
   \rho(J)^2 = (J \otimes I + I \otimes J)^2 = J^2 \otimes I + 2 J \otimes J + I \otimes J^2 = -2I_{16} + 2 J\otimes J.
\]
At first glance this is not a scalar. **Re-examine.** Actually
ρ(J) = J ⊗ I + I ⊗ J with J ∈ q(2)_{odd} acting as a parity-twist.
The super-bracket on V^⊗2 imposes ρ(J)² = (J ⊗ I + I ⊗ J)² where
the cross-terms involve sign factors from anti-commutation. Computing
in the super-tensor convention:
\[
   (J \otimes I) \cdot (I \otimes J) = J \otimes J \cdot (-1)^{|J|\cdot |I|} = J \otimes J,
\]
\[
   (I \otimes J) \cdot (J \otimes I) = J \otimes J \cdot (-1)^{|J|\cdot|J|} = -J \otimes J.
\]
So (J⊗I)(I⊗J) + (I⊗J)(J⊗I) = J⊗J − J⊗J = 0; and ρ(J)² = J²⊗I + I⊗J² = −2I_{16}. ✓

**So ρ(J)² = −2I on V^⊗2.** This means ρ(J) has eigenvalues ±i√2
(each appearing 8 times, since the 16-dim space splits as 8+8).
**Only two eigenvalues, not four.** Wave-4 §5.2 records this
correctly but the ledger #111 narrative ("4-sector parametric
foliation") elides the fact that this 4-sector structure lives on
**End(V^⊗2) under Ad_{ρ(J)}**, not on V^⊗2 itself.

### §3.4 Where the 4 eigenvalues live

On End(V^⊗2) (dim 256), Ad_{ρ(J)} : T → ρ(J) T ρ(J)^{-1}. With
ρ(J)² = −2I and ρ(J)/√2 of square −I, Ad_{ρ(J)/√2} has order 4 on
End(V^⊗2) and the 4 eigenvalues {+1, −1, +i, −i} *do* appear, with
multiplicities 64 each (since End decomposes as a tensor square).

**The wave-4 4-sector structure exists on End(V^⊗2), not V^⊗2.**
