descent, finite-first HN completion, $\Ran(E)$-support-locality
obstruction resolution. This list is the complement of what the
topological-strings firewall declares unsupplied; the two
documents agree on the absence of these structures.

**Verdict. FW.Igusa firewall preserved.** The Igusa main file
records the compact realization as an open recognition problem;
no chain-level isomorphism with brane-defect BV is alleged. The
firewall typology classification "FW.Igusa is strictly more
permanent than FW.BCOV (no coefficient-layer common ancestor)" is

## §4. Probe 4 — Convention drift test

**Question.** Are convention drifts ($d = \dim_\C X$, worldsheet
$\Sigma$, framing on $S^3$, sign conventions, regulator class
definitions) consistent across the five volumes, or does the
Wave-4 firewall typology silently absorb a convention mismatch?

**Sub-probes.**

**(4a) $d = \dim_\C X$ convention.** Topological-strings does not
fix a global $d$; it works on a formal symplectic 2-disk
$\widehat{\C^2}_0$, real dimension 6 in the mixed factorisation
category. Vol III uses $d = \dim_\C X$ throughout
(`CLAUDE.md`:26, "*$\Phi: \mathrm{CY}\text{-cat}_d \to
\mathrm{ChirAlg}$*"; `cy_to_chiral.tex`:7--186). Vol I and Vol II
do not commit to a $d$-convention at the manifesto level; their
$d$-references appear via Vol III alignment
(Vol II `CLAUDE.md`:348--394, "*Two-stage factorisation: Vol III
alignment*"). **Consistent.** No convention drift.

**(4b) Worldsheet $\Sigma$.** Topological-strings: brane defect on
$\R$ (the open boundary), bulk on $\widehat{\C^2}_0$ (the formal
symplectic disk). Vol II:
$\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}$ on $(C, \R)$ — bulk holomorphic
on a curve $C$, boundary topological on $\R$
(CLAUDE.md:31--36). Vol III: stage-2 specialisation
$\Sigma_{d-1} = K3$-fibre, reference curve $C = E$
(`cy_to_chiral.tex`:91, 125). The four worldsheet conventions
($\R^2 \times \widehat{\C^2}_0$, $C \times \R$, $\Sigma_{d-1}
\times C$) are **distinct geometric setups**, and each volume
declares its own. The Wave-4 firewall typology asserts that no
chain-level isomorphism exists across these distinct setups; this
is consistent with each volume's internal convention.

**(4c) Framing on $S^3$.** Vol III: chain-level $\bS^3$-framing
in $\pi_3(B\Sp(2m)) = 0$ via Bott periodicity on the
symplectic-reduction locus
(`cy_to_chiral.tex`:144). Topological-strings: framing data on
the brane defect (`main.tex`:1603, "*negative cyclic
Calabi-Yau class, framing data, and descent; none of that is*"
[supplied]). The Vol III $\bS^3$-framing is for the $\PhiFA_3$
stage on a CY$_3$; the topological-strings framing is for the
brane defect on $\R$ inside $\R^2 \times \widehat{\C^2}_0$.
**Distinct framing data, each declared internally.** No silent
absorption.

**(4d) Sign conventions.** The wave-4 firewall flags
$\Str_{\mathfrak{gl}(N|N)}(I) = N - M$ (with cancellation at
$N = M$) for FW.BCOV, $\mathrm{otr}_{\mathfrak q(N)}(J) = N$ for
FW.Sergeev-A5, $\mathrm{Tr}(I) = N$ for FW.Unreduced-Bosonic, and
the polyvector signature $(-1)^d$ for FW.Costello-Li-d-even.
Vol III's MNOP $\sigma(y) = -y$ Behrend sign
(`introduction.tex`:3188) is on the $E_2$-centre of $\cF_X$ on a
compact CY$_3$, not on the open BV complex. **No sign collision.**

**(4e) Regulator class definitions.** Topological-strings
declares (A1)--(A5) + (A2$'$) admissible regulator class on
$\R^2 \times \widehat{\C^2}_0$ in CG Vol II framework
(`phase4-exec-Firewall-Meta-Theorem-2026-04-28.md`:776). Vol I/II
use Costello--Gwilliam Vol II regulator-class machinery on $C$ and
$\R$ separately. Vol III uses Kontsevich--Tamarkin formality +
Costello--Gwilliam--Li locality on the CY$_d$ target
(`CLAUDE.md`:240--242, `cy_to_chiral.tex`:171). The regulator
class declarations are scope-disjoint; the topological-strings
regulator class is not exported across the firewall.
**Consistent.**

**Verdict on §4.** No convention drift detected across the five
volumes. Each volume declares its own worldsheet, framing, sign,
and regulator-class conventions; the Wave-4 firewall typology
records the absence of chain-level isomorphisms across these
distinct convention regimes, not a silent unification.
