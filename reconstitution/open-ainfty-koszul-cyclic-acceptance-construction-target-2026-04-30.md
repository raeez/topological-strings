# Open A-infinity Koszul Cyclic Acceptance Construction Target

Date: 2026-04-30. Scope: local mixed holomorphic-topological SFT on
`\R^2_top x \C^2_hol`. Writable surface: this report only.

## Verdict

The strongest true theorem surface is not the raw slogan
`stable trace = Koszul dual closed observables`. The stable core is a
three-layer statement:

1. proved cyclic/Koszul trace algebra for the Dirac brane model;
2. proved/admissible CE/PV and Tate bar-cobar criteria after explicit
   completion and conilpotence data;
3. construction target for an open `A_\infty` acceptance datum whose
   obstruction complex is exact.

The universal open-side property is not yet proved for the raw polynomial
PBW stable trace source. The exact obstruction theorem is already visible:
the raw source fails at the full cyclic contraction, the `Psi -> rho`
module bridge, the strict Casimir kernel, and the full-disk
pronilpotence hypotheses. A positive theorem must build an admissible
principal-part/Fourier-Rees open `A_\infty` object and prove exactness of
the cone/Roos/LQT obstruction complex.

## Stable Core

Source dg/category. For an interval `I` on the brane line, the one-object
open string dg category has endomorphism complex
`Omega_c^*(I) \otimes Lambda(epsilon_1,epsilon_2) \otimes End(C^N)`,
with product induced by wedge, Ext multiplication, and matrix
multiplication. The cyclic trace is
`int_I eta [epsilon_1 epsilon_2]u Tr_N(M)`. The local Ext calculation is
`Ext_R^*(k,k) = Lambda_C(epsilon_1,epsilon_2)`, `|epsilon_i|=1`.
Anchors: `main.tex:2589-2671`, `local-dictionary.tex:99-114`.

Ghost-zero Dirac reduction. The field shift sends the two Ext normal
directions to the ordinary matrix coordinates `phi_1, phi_2`, with
gauge multiplier `A`. In Dirac-normalized variables
`S_partial = int_R Tr(phi_1 d phi_2 + A[phi_1,phi_2])`, and the reduced
BV/Koszul differential is `Q psi = [phi_1,phi_2]`.
Anchors: `main.tex:118-144`, `main.tex:2673-2784`,
`claim-strength-ledger.tex:866-878`.

Cyclic chain model. The proved necklace model is
`p_K = Cyc(T(C x \oplus C y \oplus C psi))`, with `|x|=|y|=0`,
`|psi|=-1`, `Q psi = xy-yx`, scalar word retained, and constant
necklace bracket. The normal-ordering quotient is a dg Lie map only with
the scalar word present and only gives a cut-dependent no-psi
straightening homotopy. There is no full cyclic contraction killing
all psi-words.
Anchors: `main.tex:4650-4712`, `main.tex:4746-4825`,
`theorem-lanes.tex:55-103`.

Stable trace and primitive homology. In fixed word degree `d`, stable
`GL_N` trace invariant theory has range `N >= d`; the stable algebra is
the free commutative algebra on nonempty cyclic words. The primitive
one-psi homology is one-dimensional in each nonconstant bidegree and is
generated by `Psi_{k,l}`. The full primitive cyclic-word homology is
`C[x,y] \oplus C[x,y] theta`, with no primitive sector for psi-degree
`p >= 2`.
Anchors: `main.tex:4298-4315`, `main.tex:4422-4512`,
`main.tex:4975-5050`, `main.tex:5061-5099`,
`theorem-lanes.tex:105-150`.

Closed target. The closed Hamiltonian BF algebra is
`g = h \ltimes h_cont^vee[1]`, `h = C[[z_1,z_2]] / C.1`, and closed
observables are continuous CE cochains. The CE/PV generator dictionary is
`c^f -> theta^f` and `u_f -> O_f`; boundary Hamiltonians come from the
shifted-cotangent coordinate, not from the CE ghost.
Anchors: `main.tex:157-168`, `main.tex:196-234`,
`theorem-lanes.tex:152-180`, `claim-strength-ledger.tex:860-864`,
`local-dictionary.tex:1232-1240`.

Admissible Koszul layer. The bar-cobar/Koszul upgrade is valid only in
an admissible Tate, relative, nilpotent, or weighted envelope. The
load-bearing hypotheses are lower-central Tate-pronilpotence,
conilpotent CE chains, PBW completeness, completed symmetric associated
graded, exact continuous dualization, and vanishing Milnor/Roos errors.
Anchors: `main.tex:5223-5294`, `main.tex:5353-5367`,
`main.tex:5411-5460`, `theorem-lanes.tex:1875-1937`,
`open-obligations.tex:102-116`, `claim-strength-ledger.tex:842-858`.

## Acceptance Target

Definition target. An accepted open `A_\infty`/Koszul/cyclic comparison is
the following datum in the complete filtered ML category of finite
windows `W = (D,L,M)`:

1. A source tower of one-object cyclic dg categories
   `B_{N,I,W}` with endomorphism complexes
   `Omega_c^*(I) \otimes Lambda(epsilon_1,epsilon_2) \otimes End(C^N)`,
   cyclic trace as above, and Dirac/Koszul reduction
   `Q psi = [phi_1,phi_2]`.
2. A reduced cyclic dg algebra
   `A_psi = T(x,y,psi)`, `d psi = xy-yx`, together with its reduced
   cyclic complex `CC_red(A_psi)` and the necklace `H_0` shadow
   `Cyc(A_psi)`.
3. A degreewise stable trace/LQT comparison map
   `lambda_LQT,W: Prim C_*^CE(gl_N(A_psi,W)) -> CC_red(A_psi,W)[1]`
   for `N >= D`, compatible with cyclic trace functions and with
   connected single-trace projection.
4. A completed augmented filtered cyclic `A_\infty` algebra `A_op`,
   obtained by homotopy transfer from the finite-window source tower.
   Its associated graded indecomposables are not the raw formula
   `pi_Ham Conn Prim_st(R_N^{GL_N},Q)` as an algebra object; that formula
   is only the linear symbol. The target indecomposables must be either
   `bar A \oplus P[1]` in the principal-part model or
   `bar A \oplus bar A_desc[1]` plus a proved Fourier-Rees/Moyal bridge
   to `P[1]`.
5. The closed target
   `B_CE/PV = C^bullet_CE,cont(g_adm)`, with admissible
   `g_adm`, the generator dictionary `c^f -> theta^f`, `u_f -> O_f`,
   and continuous Schouten bracket.
6. A continuous degree-one twisting cochain
   `tau_op: Bar(A_op) -> B_CE/PV`, with
   `d tau + tau b + sum_{r>=2} m_r(tau^r) Delta^(r)=0`. For a dg
   closed target this reduces to the displayed binary equation in
   `theorem-lanes.tex:2041-2047`; for the full `A_\infty` target all
   higher `m_r` terms must be retained.
7. A completed cobar morphism
   `kappa_tau: Omega Bar(A_op) -> B_CE/PV` whose associated-graded symbol
   sends boundary Hamiltonian generators to `O_f` and principal-part
   cotangent generators to the corresponding `theta` coordinates.
8. Exactness of the obstruction complex
   `C_tau = Cone(gr kappa_tau) + lim^1-source + lim^1-target`, plus
   exact continuous dualization on the same tower.
9. A cyclicity package: the scalar word is retained before scalar
   reduction; graded cyclicity uses
   `Tr(ab)=(-1)^{|a||b|}Tr(ba)`; two psi letters skew; the open trace is
   continuous; the BV pairing has degree `-1` after the de Rham, Ext, and
   field-shift conventions.

## Obstruction Theorem Target

Precise obstruction theorem. Let `A_PBW` be the raw scalar-reduced stable
polynomial trace/Koszul source with one-psi classes `Psi_{a,b}`, and let
`g_strict = h \ltimes h_cont^vee[1]` be the strict product/direct-sum
formal Hamiltonian disk coefficient pair. There is no universal
open-side filtered `A_\infty`/Koszul/cyclic comparison
`Omega Bar(A_PBW) -> C^bullet_CE,cont(g_strict)` satisfying the linear
symbol `Psi_{a,b} -> rho_{a,b}` and the strict endpoint kernel
requirements.

The obstruction tuple is

```text
O_open =
  ( O_cyc,
    O_Psi-rho,
    O_Cas,
    O_lc,
    H^*(Cone(gr kappa_tau)),
    lim^1_M H((Omega C_op)_{<=M}),
    lim^1_M H((B_CE/PV)_{<=M}),
    H^*(Cone(lambda_LQT)) ).
```

For the raw source, the first four components are already nonzero or
blocked by proved failures:

- `O_cyc != 0`: primitive one-psi classes obstruct a full cyclic
  normal-ordering contraction (`main.tex:4801-4825`).
- `O_Psi-rho != 0`: the same-label map to Matlis principal parts is not
  an `h`-module map (`theorem-lanes.tex:1813-1848`); the Moyal version
  has explicit finite-window mismatches (`scripts/check_one_psi_homology.py:627-651`).
- `O_Cas != 0`: no strict product/direct-sum Casimir tensor exists
  (`theorem-lanes.tex:1785-1811`).
- `O_lc != 0`: the naive low-degree/pronilpotent split of the full disk
  is not a Lie semidirect product (`theorem-lanes.tex:1850-1873`).

The positive theorem must therefore replace the raw source or the raw
target, not demote the comparison. The acceptance gate is:
construct the principal-part/Fourier-Rees open `A_op`, construct
`lambda_LQT` and `tau_op`, then prove all components of `O_open` vanish
in the chosen admissible finite-window ML topology. If a component does
not vanish, the deliverable is the obstruction theorem naming that
component and its finite-window witness.
