## §1. Russian-school criteria recapped from `INVARIANTS.md §IV`

The Russian-school voice (Gelfand, Etingof, Kazhdan, Nekrasov, Polyakov,
Beilinson, Drinfeld) plus mathematical-physics frontier (Witten,
Costello, Gaiotto) demands:

1. **Terse, declarative prose.** Sentences carry information; when they
   stop carrying information, they end. No padding.

2. **Structure does the work.** Definitions, lemmas, propositions,
   theorems, remarks, in that order. Headings are signposts.

3. **The reader is serious and adult.** No hand-holding, no
   pre-softening.

4. **Precise attribution.** Name the theorem, the author, the year:
   *Beilinson--Drinfeld (2004)* beats *a classical result shows.*

5. **Physical intuition and formal rigor coexist.**

6. **Concrete example before abstraction.**

7. **Honest epistemic status.** *proved / conjectured / heuristic /
   computed / expected / folklore.*

8. **When something is subtle, say so.** Do not soften with *somewhat
   delicate.*

9. **Proof-obligation discipline.** Mark exact obligation while
   repairing it.

**Forbidden registers (non-exhaustive).**
- Marketing verbs: *unlock, transform, empower, revolutionize, accrue
  value, flywheel, best-in-class, game-changing, robust, comprehensive,
  seamless, delve.*
- Hedging: *we believe, essentially, arguably, in our view, to our
  knowledge, roughly speaking.*
- Academic bloat: *it is noteworthy that, one might observe, it is
  perhaps worth pointing out, it is instructive to consider.*
- Theatrical framing: *imagine a world where, in an increasingly
  complex landscape, as we enter a new era of, at the intersection of.*
- Conversational filler: *great question, happy to help, let me know if,
  hope this helps, feel free to.*
- Bullet-list inflation, emojis.

The voice is recognizable on page one of any of: Gelfand, Beilinson--
Drinfeld, Etingof--Gelaki--Nikshych--Ostrik, Kazhdan, Polyakov 1987,
Nekrasov 2003, Witten on TQFT, Costello 2011, Costello--Gwilliam,
Gaiotto on class S.
