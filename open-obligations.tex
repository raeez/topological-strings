\section{Admissibility criteria and obstruction complexes}\label{sec:open-obligations}

The coordinate CE/PV theorem and staged admissibility programme is
governed by constructions, hypotheses, and obstruction complexes.  Each
item below is either a construction already used in the local theorem, an
admissibility criterion for a frontier extension, or an obstruction complex
whose vanishing is the exact condition for the next theorem.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \emph{Finite cotangent CE/PV data.}
  The finite model is attached to a finite-dimensional Lie algebra
  \(\mathfrak l\), its ordinary dual \(\mathfrak l^\vee\), and the
  cotangent Lie algebra
  \[
    \mathfrak g_{\mathfrak l}
    =\mathfrak l\ltimes \mathfrak l^\vee[1].
  \]
  The finite comparison is
  \[
    \Phi_{\mathfrak l}\colon
    C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE}}(\mathfrak g_{\mathfrak l})
    \xrightarrow{\sim}
    \mathrm{PV}(\mathbf S(\mathfrak l)),
    \qquad c\mapsto\theta,\quad u\mapsto O .
  \]

  \item \emph{Completed formal-disk CE/PV admissibility.}
  The comparison for
  \(\mathfrak h=\C[[z_1,z_2]]/\C\cdot1\) and
  \(\mathfrak g=\mathfrak h\ltimes
  \mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1]\) is accepted first as a pro
  coordinate-window CE/PV comparison.  It becomes a completed bracketed
  statement only after a bracket-admissible realization \(B\subset
  \mathrm{PV}_{\mathrm{coord}}(A_{\partial,\mathrm{coord}})\) has a
  specified complete Hausdorff topology, continuous inclusion in the
  coordinate product, continuous multiplication, continuous
  \(d_\pi^{\mathrm{coord}}\), a continuous closed coordinate contraction
  bracket, and the transported continuous source structure on
  \(\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}^{-1}(B)\).  Boundary Hamiltonian uses also
  require \(B\) to contain the relevant \(O_f\)'s and
  \(d_\pi^{\mathrm{coord}}O_f\)'s.  It becomes a kernel statement only
  after a kernel-admissible datum \((B,\mathcal K_B)\) is supplied, so that
  the diagonal tensor
  \[
    \Theta_B=\sum_I H_I\otimes\theta^I+\sum_I\eta^I\otimes O_I
  \]
  converges in \(\mathcal K_B\) and satisfies
  \[
    d\Theta_B+\frac12[\Theta_B,\Theta_B]=0.
  \]
  The admissible formal-local interface datum is
  \[
    \mathfrak U_{\mathfrak h}
    =(\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}},\{\Phi^B_{P_0}\}_B,\{\Theta_B\}_B).
  \]
  It is not a categorical universal object unless a category of
  admissible completions and a universal property are supplied.
  The
  coordinate obstruction terms are
  \[
    \mathfrak o^{\mathrm{br}}_{M,N}(x,y)
      =\pi_{M,N}[x,y]_M-[\pi_{M,N}x,\pi_{M,N}y]_N,
  \]
  \[
    \mathfrak o^{\mathrm{coad}}_{M,N}(x,\lambda)
      =\pi^\vee_{M,N}(x\cdot_M\lambda)
       -(\pi_{M,N}x)\cdot_N(\pi^\vee_{M,N}\lambda),
  \]
  Their vanishing gives the pro coordinate-window comparison
	  \[
	    \Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}\colon
	    C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{coord}}(\mathfrak g)
	    \xrightarrow{\sim}
	    \mathrm{PV}_{\mathrm{coord}}(A_{\partial,\mathrm{coord}}).
	  \]
  On a bracket-admissible \(B\) the restricted map
  \[
    \Phi^B_{P_0}\colon
    \Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}^{-1}(B)\xrightarrow{\sim}B
  \]
  is the corresponding completed \(P_0\)-comparison.
  The Casimir transition term
  \[
    \mathfrak o^{\mathrm{Cas}}_{M,N}
      =(\pi_{M,N}\otimes\pi^\vee_{M,N})C_M-C_N
  \]
  diagnoses the failure of a strict global kernel, propagator, endpoint,
  ambient-product bracket, and bar-cobar/enveloping comparison in the unweighted
  product/direct-sum topology.  It is not an additional hypothesis for
  each coordinate-window CE/PV identity.

  \item \emph{Admissible Tate bar-cobar/Quillen envelope.}
  For an admissible Tate replacement
  \(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{adm}}\), put
  \[
    B_{\mathrm{adm}}=
    C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{adm}}).
  \]
  The admissible Koszul duality theorem in the envelope is
  \[
    B_{\mathrm{adm}}^!\simeq
    \widehat U(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{adm}}),
  \]
  together with the bar-cobar Quillen equivalence under lower-central
  Tate-pronilpotence, PBW completeness, and the completed symmetric
  associated graded.

  \item \emph{Reduced CE/PV central-operation generator map.}
  The reduced formal central-operation map is determined on CE/PV
  generators by
  \[
    c^I\mapsto\theta^I,\qquad u_I\mapsto O_I .
  \]
  The boundary Hamiltonian observable is sourced by the shifted
  cotangent coordinate \(u_I\).  This type check is part of the theorem:
  \(c^I\) gives the vector-field operation, while \(u_I\) gives the
  Hamiltonian boundary coordinate.

  \item \emph{Matlis/local-cohomology cotangent source.}
  The continuous cotangent module is
  \[
    \mathcal P=
    \operatorname{Ann}_{\mathrm{res}}(\C\cdot1)
    \subset H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2,
  \]
  with basis
  \[
    \rho_{a,b}=z_1^{-a-1}z_2^{-b-1}dz_1dz_2,\qquad a+b>0.
  \]
  The coadjoint action is
  \[
    z_1^p z_2^q\cdot\rho_{a,b}
    =
    -(pb-qa+p-q)\rho_{a-p+1,b-q+1}.
  \]

  \item \emph{Primitive higher-\(\psi\) Hamiltonian extraction.}
  The full primitive Koszul complex for \(Q\psi=[x,y]\) extracts the
  Hamiltonian sectors:
  nonconstant \(H_0\) classes, primitive one-\(\psi\) classes
  \(\Psi_{a,b}\), and no primitive \(p\ge2\) homology.  The one-\(\psi\)
  action is
  \[
    \{\overline{\operatorname{Tr}(\phi_1^p\phi_2^q)},
      \widetilde\Psi_{a,b}\}
    =(pb-qa)\widetilde\Psi_{a+p-1,b+q-1},
  \]
  with negative targets zero and scalar targets killed in the
  Hamiltonian quotient.

  \item \emph{Principal-part versus polynomial-descendant criterion.}
  The polynomial one-\(\psi\) and principal-part modules have opposite
  linear-Hamiltonian behavior:
  \[
    \{O_{1,0},\widetilde\Psi_{a,b}\}
      =b\,\widetilde\Psi_{a,b-1},
    \qquad
    \{O_{0,1},\widetilde\Psi_{a,b}\}
      =-a\,\widetilde\Psi_{a-1,b},
  \]
  while
  \[
    z_1\cdot\rho_{a,b}=-(b+1)\rho_{a,b+1},
    \qquad
    z_2\cdot\rho_{a,b}=(a+1)\rho_{a+1,b}.
  \]
  This local-nilpotence obstruction is the necessary condition for any
  proposed polynomial realization of \(\mathcal P\): it must change the
  target, the action, or the category.

  \item \emph{\(\mathcal D_\hbar\) Fourier--Rees bridge.}
  With
  \[
    \mathcal D_\hbar
    =
    \C[[\hbar]]
      \langle z_1,z_2,\partial_1,\partial_2\rangle/
      ([\partial_i,z_j]=\hbar\delta_{ij}),
  \]
  the Fourier delta module
  \(\Delta^{\mathsf F}_\hbar=\mathcal D_\hbar/(z_1,z_2)\) gives
  \[
    z_1^az_2^b\mapsto
    \partial_1^a\partial_2^be_0
    \mapsto
    (-1)^{a+b}\hbar^{a+b}a!b!\rho_{a,b}.
  \]
  Before \(\hbar\) is inverted, the image is the Rees sublattice
  \(\bigoplus\hbar^{a+b}\C[[\hbar]]\rho_{a,b}\).  The same-action
  obstruction forbids a flat Rees family that identifies polynomial
  one-\(\psi\) descendants with \(\mathcal P\) as modules for the
  original Hamiltonian action.

  \item \emph{One-antifield Moyal target.}
  The reduced distributional principal-part target has bracket
  \[
    \{B_f(a),\Theta_\rho(B)\}_\hbar
      =
      \Theta_{\operatorname{ad}^{\vee}_{f,\hbar}\rho}(aB),
    \qquad
    \{\Theta_\rho(B),\Theta_\sigma(C)\}_\hbar=0.
  \]
  A strict quantum descendant lift of the classical label shadow is
  excluded by the leading-term calculation
  \[
    z_1\cdot_0\widetilde\Psi_{1,0}=0,\qquad
    z_1\cdot\rho_{1,0}=-\rho_{1,1}.
  \]

  \item \emph{Weighted finite-window regulator independence.}
  A regulator-admissible sector is a strict Mittag-Leffler family on
  finite windows with graphwise bounded Hamiltonian and cotangent legs.
  For admissible weights \(w,w'\), diagonal transports
  \(R^{w_0}_{w,w'}\) identify the local-functional complexes and carry
  the obstruction class
  \[
    \mathrm{Ob}_w\in
    H^1_{\mathrm{adm}}(\mathcal O_{\mathrm{loc}}(\mathcal E_w),
      Q+\{I_0^w,-\})
  \]
  to \(\mathrm{Ob}_{w'}\).  Thus QME vanishing is invariant under
  admissible finite-window weight.

  \item \emph{Endpoint admissibility.}
  An endpoint for \(w_q(d)=q^d\) is accepted by conditions (E1)--(E5):
  strict Mittag-Leffler quotients, a coefficient kernel projecting to
  every finite Casimir, transport of differential, bracket, coadjoint
  action, propagator, interaction, counterterms, and the generator map
  \(c\mapsto\theta,\ u\mapsto O\), admissible filtered cohomology, and
  RG preservation.  The endpoint obstruction tuple is
  \[
    \mathfrak o_{\mathrm{BV,end}}
    =
    (\mathfrak o_{\mathrm{Cas}},
     \mathfrak o_{\mathrm{def}},
     \mathfrak o_{\mathrm{RG}},
     \mathfrak o_{\mathrm{QME}}).
  \]
  The strict product/direct-sum pair fails the criterion because no
  tensor \(K\) projects to all finite Casimirs
  \(C^M_{\mathfrak h}\).

  \item \emph{Mixed brane-defect QME complex.}
  The local-functional obstruction lives in
  \[
    H^1\bigl(\mathcal O_{\mathrm{loc}}(\mathcal E_w),
      Q+\{I_0,-\}\bigr).
  \]
  Its scalar component is computed by
  \[
    \{I_\partial(a,f),I_\partial(b,g)\}_{\mathrm{open}}
    =
    I_\partial(ab,\{f,g\}_{\bar A})
      +N\,\omega(f,g)\int_\R a(t)b(t)\,dt ,
  \]
  or, in Weyl normalization,
  \[
	    \operatorname{Tr}(A\,XY)-\operatorname{Tr}(A\,YX)
	      =\hbar N\,\operatorname{Tr}(A)
	      \quad\bmod\ \mathcal W_N\widehat\mu(\lie{sl}_N).
	  \]
	  The brane stack is still ordinary \(\mathfrak{gl}_N\), but the quantum
	  moment-map ideal is traceless after the scalar comoment vanishes.
  With the central ghost \(\gamma_{\mathbf 1}\), the representative is
  \[
    \operatorname{Ob}_{\mathrm{sc}}
    =
    \hbar N\,\omega(f,g)
    \int_\R a(t)b(t)\gamma_{\mathbf 1}(t)\,dt .
  \]
	  A scalar-reduced ordinary \(\mathfrak{gl}_N\) lift requires
	  \(W_{\mathrm{ext}}\) with leading class
	  \[
	    [w_{\mathrm{ext}}]=-\hbar N[\bar c]
	    \qquad\text{in }H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A;\C)[[\hbar]].
	  \]
	  Alternatively, the unreduced central-character source absorbs the
	  scalar symbol before scalar reduction, while a Casimir-balanced
	  supertrace model makes the scalar representative zero.

  \item \emph{Unreduced factorization-current lift.}
	  The unreduced cotangent lift is a datum:
	  distributional principal-part boundary fields, product centrality
	  homotopies, \(P_0\)-bracket homotopies, a coefficient kernel, a
	  brane-defect propagator, and a QME solution.  Polynomial boundary
	  representatives are ruled out by the local-nilpotence theorem; the
	  accepted target is the principal-part current sector; the centrality
	  homotopies must be supplied as additional unreduced lift data.  In
	  convolution form an unreduced coupling
	  \[
	    \kappa\colon C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE}}(\mathfrak g)
	    \longrightarrow Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}(A_{\mathrm{BV}})
	  \]
	  must first satisfy
	  \[
	    \operatorname{Curv}(\kappa)
	    =d\kappa+\frac12[\kappa,\kappa]=0,
	  \]
		  and then pass the descent and QME tests.  A chain-level
		  normal-ordering lift is separate data: either a cut-dependent
		  no-\(\psi\) straightening as in
		  Theorem~\ref{thm:normal-ordering-straightening-homotopy}, or a
		  genuinely larger target retaining the descendant module together with
		  maps \(N,i,H\) satisfying the relevant deformation of
		  \(QH+HQ=\mathrm{id}-iN\).  Only in such a larger target could one
		  attempt to form a correction.  After matching the bracket convention of
		  Construction~\ref{constr:boundary-local-dual-principal-parts}, the
		  defect
		  \[
		    \{O_f,\Theta_\rho\}-\Theta_{f\cdot\rho}
		  \]
		  would have to be \(Q\)-exact; no fixed formula for that homotopy is
		  asserted before the target and signs are constructed.
		  Until these maps are constructed in the chosen target, the
		  principal-part cotangent lift is an obligation rather than a theorem.
		  Moreover, the explicit normal-ordering straightening now proved in the
		  body is only the cut-dependent no-\(\psi\) calculation.  A full cyclic
		  contraction with \(N\) killing all one-\(\psi\) descendants is
		  impossible by Lemma~\ref{lem:no-full-cyclic-normal-ordering-contraction};
		  any genuine unreduced lift must retain those descendant classes or
		  replace the target by a larger homotopy-coherent object.

  \item \emph{Moyal and graph normalization.}
  The Weyl/Moyal convention is
  \[
    f*g=m\circ\exp\left(\frac{\hbar}{2}
    (\partial_{z_1}\otimes\partial_{z_2}
     -\partial_{z_2}\otimes\partial_{z_1})\right)(f\otimes g).
  \]
  The normalized bracket is
  \[
    \{f,g\}_\hbar
    =\sum_{s\ge0}
      \frac{\hbar^{2s}}{2^{2s}(2s+1)!}P^{2s+1}(f,g).
  \]
  The first and third raw commutator weights are \(1\) and \(1/24\);
  any Costello realization must reproduce these coefficients after its
  sign, orientation, automorphism, and counterterm conventions are fixed.
  These coefficients are necessary tests for a Costello realization, not
  sufficient data for analytic BV/QME existence.

  \item \emph{Global and cross-volume descent.}
  A target theorem supplies a matched-conventions datum
  \[
  \mathfrak C_{\mathrm{tar}}=
  (d,Y,\eta,(\Sigma_{d-1},C),L,\tau,\lambda,\mathfrak f,\Lambda,
  \hbar_{\mathrm{tar}},\mathcal O_{\mathrm{tar}},
  \chi_{\mathrm{cl}},\chi_{\mathrm{op}},\chi_{\mathrm{anom}},
  \mathcal K_{\mathrm{obs}}).
  \]
  Its admissibility vector is
  \[
    \operatorname{Ob}_{\mathfrak C_{\mathrm{tar}}}
    =
    (\operatorname{per}_{\mathrm{tar}},
     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{WR}},
     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{anom}},
     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{QME}},
     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{conv}},
     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{hyp}},
     \operatorname{ob}_{6r},
     \operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{MD}}).
  \]
  Here \(\operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{conv}}\) records convergence of the
  transported obstruction tower, \(\operatorname{ob}_{\mathrm{hyp}}\)
  records hyperdescent and the absence of hidden \(\varprojlim^1\)
  defects, and \(\operatorname{ob}_{6r}\) records any asserted
  six-relation factorization constraint.  A compact BCOV,
  MNOP/chiral-volume, Vol III, Igusa/BKM, or sister-volume theorem may
  use the formal-local theorem only after the target supplies this
  vanishing obstruction vector, chosen null-homotopies, and comparison
  morphisms identifying its formal restriction with the local Hamiltonian
  BF/Moyal normalization.  The transfer theorem is target data, not a
  consequence of the formal disk.
\end{enumerate}
