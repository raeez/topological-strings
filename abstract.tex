\begin{abstract}
We work in the formal-local mixed holomorphic-topological theory on
\[
  X=\R^2_{\mathrm{top}}\times\C^2_{\mathrm{hol}},
  \qquad
  L=\R_{\mathrm{brane}}\times\{s=z_1=z_2=0\},
\]
completed at the Dirac brane whose holomorphic normal disk is
\((\operatorname{Spf}\C[[z_1,z_2]],dz_1\wedge dz_2)\).  The closed
sector is Hamiltonian BF theory for
\[
  \mathfrak h=\C[[z_1,z_2]]/\C\cdot1,\qquad
  \mathfrak g=\mathfrak h\ltimes
  \mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1],
\]
and the brane sector is the Dirac/Koszul matrix probe
\[
  S_\partial=\int_\R
  \operatorname{Tr}(\phi_1\,d\phi_2+A[\phi_1,\phi_2]),
  \qquad Q\psi=[\phi_1,\phi_2].
\]

The proved core is a reduced coordinate theorem.  CE cochains on the
shifted cotangent Hamiltonian Lie algebra identify with the coordinate
polyvector model for reduced \(P_0\) central operations,
\[
  c^I\mapsto \theta^I,\qquad u_I\mapsto O_I,
\]
after constants on the Hamiltonian side and the empty trace on the brane
side have been removed.  Bracket and kernel forms require the stated
bracket-admissible and kernel-admissible completions.  The theorem is not
the unreduced factorization centre of the full open BV algebra.

The cotangent coefficients are Grothendieck--Matlis principal parts,
not polynomial one-\(\psi\) descendants.  The stable trace computation
supplies an algebraic PBW special-fibre shadow; the deformation-level
cotangent module is
\(\mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}\), realized by residue principal
parts.  The Weyl/Moyal--Capelli computation gives the formal quantum
coefficient target and the scalar class \(\hbar N[\bar c]\) in the
ordinary scalar-reduced \(\mathfrak{gl}_N\) branch.

Compact Calabi--Yau, global BCOV, CoHA, quintic, OSV/GV,
Abel--Jacobi, MNOP, Igusa, Borcherds/BKM, and sister-volume claims are
external comparison targets.  They are not consequences of the local
Hamiltonian BF/Dirac-brane theorem proved here.
\end{abstract}
