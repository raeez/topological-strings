\begin{abstract}
We study a local mixed A/B topological string model on
$\R^2\times\C^2$ with brane $\R\times p$.  The ghost-number-zero classical open
sector reduces to a one-dimensional gauged first-order matrix model, while the
closed Hamiltonian sector is the cotangent BF theory for Hamiltonian vector
fields on the holomorphic symplectic surface.  We construct the classical
bulk-to-boundary map by
evaluating closed Hamiltonians on the matrix normal coordinates of the brane
and prove that, after removing scalar constants, it intertwines the closed
Poisson bracket with the open trace bracket.  In the stable large-$N$ limit the
scalar-reduced degree-zero Hamiltonian trace algebra, together with its
nonconstant primitive one-antifield Koszul homology, matches the PBW special
fibre of the Koszul dual of the closed Hamiltonian Chevalley-Eilenberg algebra.
\end{abstract}
