\begin{abstract}
We study a local mixed A/B topological string model on
$\R^2\times\C^2$ with brane $\R\times p$.  The open theory reduces to a
one-dimensional gauged first-order matrix model, while the closed local model
is the cotangent BF theory for Hamiltonian vector fields on the holomorphic
symplectic surface.  We construct the classical bulk-to-boundary map by
evaluating closed Hamiltonians on the matrix normal coordinates of the brane
and prove that, after removing scalar constants, it intertwines the closed
Poisson bracket with the open trace bracket.  In the stable large-$N$ limit the
scalar-reduced degree-zero Hamiltonian trace algebra, together with its
distinguished first Koszul descendants, matches the PBW special fibre of the
Koszul dual of the closed Chevalley-Eilenberg algebra.
\end{abstract}
