\begin{abstract}
We prove a coordinate Chevalley--Eilenberg/polyvector theorem and an
admissible bar-cobar/enveloping criterion at the mixed
holomorphic-topological brane on the symplectic disk
\((\operatorname{Spf}\C[[z_1,z_2]],dz_1\wedge dz_2)\).
On a stack of \(N\) probes the coordinates become matrices
\(\phi_1,\phi_2\in\mathfrak{gl}_N\), and the open sector starts from
the Dirac first-order theory
\[
  \int_\R\operatorname{Tr}\bigl(\phi_1\,d\phi_2
  +A[\phi_1,\phi_2]\bigr),
\]
with Koszul field \(\psi=A^*\) and differential
\(Q\psi=[\phi_1,\phi_2]\).  The closed side is the shifted cotangent
Hamiltonian Lie algebra
\[
  \mathfrak h=\C[[z_1,z_2]]/\C\cdot1,\qquad
  \mathfrak g=\mathfrak h\ltimes
  \mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1].
\]
Every finite-dimensional Lie algebra \(\mathfrak l\) carries the
cotangent Chevalley--Eilenberg/polyvector identity
\[
  C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE}}
  (\mathfrak l\ltimes \mathfrak l^{\vee}[1])
  \simeq \mathrm{PV}(\mathbf S(\mathfrak l)),
  \qquad c^I\mapsto\theta^I,\quad u_I\mapsto O_I,
\]
and the completed formal disk is reconstructed by the coordinate map
\(\Phi_{\mathrm{coord}}\) through finite coordinate tests and pro-window
coordinate cochains.  The strict product completion is only a dg
commutative coordinate theorem.  A \(P_0\)-realization is asserted after a
bracket-admissible subalgebra \(B\subset
\mathrm{PV}_{\mathrm{coord}}(A_{\partial,\mathrm{coord}})\) has been
chosen, giving \(\Phi^B_{P_0}\).  A Maurer--Cartan kernel
\(\Theta_B\) is asserted only for kernel-admissible \(B\), where the
diagonal tensor converges.  The Casimir transition obstruction belongs to
the stronger kernel, propagator, and bar-cobar endpoint problem, not to
the coordinate CE/PV identity.

The cotangent variables are Grothendieck--Matlis residue currents
\[
  \mathcal P=\operatorname{Ann}_{\mathrm{res}}(\C\cdot1)\subset
  H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2
  =\bigoplus_{a,b\geq0}\C\,\rho_{a,b},\qquad
  \mathcal P=\bigoplus_{a+b>0}\C\,\rho_{a,b},
\]
while the stable polynomial one-\(\psi\) classes \(\Psi_{a,b}\) are the
PBW special-fibre open labels.  These two modules remain
separate; the bridge between their shared labels is computed by Koszul,
Matlis, and Moyal methods.  The Rees Weyl algebra
\(\mathcal D_\hbar\) supplies the Fourier bridge
\[
  z_1^az_2^b\mapsto\partial_1^a\partial_2^be_0
  \mapsto (-1)^{a+b}\hbar^{a+b}a!b!\rho_{a,b},
\]
and the same-action obstruction shows that this is a Fourier-polarized
label comparison, not a polynomial realization of principal parts.

The proved formal-local results are the Dirac brane probe calculation,
the finite cotangent CE/PV identity, the coordinate formal-disk CE/PV
theorem, the bracket-admissible \(P_0\) realization after a choice of
\(B\), the Matlis principal-part coadjoint module, the scalar \(U(1)\)
anomaly, primitive higher-\(\psi\) Koszul homology, the
\(\mathcal D_\hbar\) Fourier--Rees bridge and same-action obstruction,
and the formal Weyl/Moyal coefficient calculation, whose first and
third raw commutator weights are \(1\) and \(1/24\).  The conditional
results are the kernel-admissible Maurer--Cartan tensor, the
admissible-envelope Tate bar-cobar/Quillen criterion
\(B_{\mathrm{adm}}^!\simeq\widehat U(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{adm}})\),
regulator independence inside the admissible finite-window sector,
	endpoint stabilization criteria, and the finite-\(N\) radial comparison
	and Costello graph/QME realization criteria.  The unreduced factorization-center
lift is an obstruction datum, not a proved full
\(Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}\)-theorem.  No global BCOV,
compact Calabi--Yau, MNOP/chiral-volume, Vol III, Igusa/BKM, or
sister-volume consequence follows here. Such a target use requires a
matched-conventions descent theorem and vanishing, with null-homotopies,
of the corresponding period, anomaly, QME, convergence, hyperdescent,
six-relation, and operadic obstruction classes.
\end{abstract}
