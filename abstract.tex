\begin{abstract}
We build the theorem infrastructure for a universal formal-local
Koszul duality theory at the mixed holomorphic-topological brane on the
symplectic disk \((\operatorname{Spf}\C[[z_1,z_2]],dz_1\wedge dz_2)\).
On a stack of \(N\) probes the coordinates become matrices
\(\phi_1,\phi_2\in\mathfrak{gl}_N\), and the open sector starts from
the Dirac first-order theory
\[
  \int_\R\operatorname{Tr}\bigl(\phi_1\,d\phi_2
  +A[\phi_1,\phi_2]\bigr),
\]
with Koszul field \(\psi=A^*\) and differential
\(Q\psi=[\phi_1,\phi_2]\).  The closed side is the shifted cotangent
Hamiltonian Lie algebra
\[
  \mathfrak h=\C[[z_1,z_2]]/\C\cdot1,\qquad
  \mathfrak g=\mathfrak h\ltimes
  \mathfrak h^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1].
\]
Finite cotangent windows carry the Chevalley--Eilenberg/polyvector
identity
\[
  C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE}}
  (\mathfrak h_{\le M}\ltimes D_{\le M}[1])
  \simeq \mathrm{PV}(\mathbf S(\mathfrak h_{\le M})),
  \qquad c^I\mapsto\theta^I,\quad u_I\mapsto O_I,
\]
and the completed formal disk is obtained by the same generator map
after the bracket, coadjoint, and Casimir transition obstructions
vanish.

The cotangent variables are Grothendieck--Matlis residue currents
\[
  \mathcal P=\operatorname{Ann}_{\mathrm{res}}(\C\cdot1)\subset
  H^2_{\mathfrak m}(\C[[z_1,z_2]])\,dz_1dz_2
  =\bigoplus_{a+b>0}\C\,\rho_{a,b},
\]
while the stable polynomial one-\(\psi\) classes \(\Psi_{a,b}\) are the
PBW special-fibre open labels.  The paper keeps these two modules
separate and computes the bridge between them by Koszul, Matlis, and
Moyal methods.  The Rees Weyl algebra
\(\mathcal D_\hbar\) supplies the Fourier bridge
\[
  z_1^az_2^b\mapsto\partial_1^a\partial_2^be_0
  \mapsto (-1)^{a+b}\hbar^{a+b}a!b!\rho_{a,b},
\]
and the same-action obstruction shows that this is a Fourier-polarized
label comparison, not a polynomial realization of principal parts.

The formal-local package consists of finite CE/PV, completed disk
CE/PV, the admissible Tate bar-cobar/Quillen envelope
\(B^!\simeq\widehat U(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{adm}})\), weighted
finite-window regulator independence, endpoint admissibility criteria,
primitive higher-\(\psi\) Koszul extraction, the
\(\mathcal D_\hbar\) bridge and obstruction, the unreduced
factorization-current lift datum, and the scalar QME obstruction
complex.  The Moyal normalization fixes the first and third raw
commutator weights \(1\) and \(1/24\).  Global BCOV, compact
Calabi--Yau, Vol III, Igusa/BKM, and sister-volume uses enter through
matched-conventions descent data and vanishings of the corresponding
period, anomaly, QME, and operadic obstruction classes.
\end{abstract}
