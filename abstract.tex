\begin{abstract}
This paper proves a local large-$N$ Koszul-duality theorem for the Hamiltonian
sector of the mixed A/B topological string on $\R^2\times\C^2$ with brane
$\R\times p$. The ghost-zero open BV theory reduces to the gauged first-order
matrix model
$$\int_\R \mathrm{Tr}\bigl(\phi_1\,d\phi_2+A[\phi_1,\phi_2]\bigr).$$
After scalar reduction and degreewise stabilization, its Hamiltonian algebra is
$$\mathbf S\bigl(\bar A\oplus\bar A_{\mathrm{desc}}[1]\bigr),
  \qquad \bar A=\C[z_1,z_2]/\C\cdot1,$$
where $\bar A_{\mathrm{desc}}$ is represented by the nonconstant primitive
one-antifield Koszul cycles. The closed Hamiltonian sector is cotangent BF
theory for the Tate Lie algebra
$$\mathfrak g=(z_1,z_2)\C[[z_1,z_2]]
  \ltimes\bigl((z_1,z_2)\C[[z_1,z_2]]\bigr)^\vee_{\mathrm{cont}}[1].$$
Its local observables are the continuous Chevalley-Eilenberg cochains
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g)$, and their Koszul dual is
the completed enveloping algebra $\widehat U(\mathfrak g)$. The PBW special
fibre identifies degree by degree with the stable open Hamiltonian algebra.
Boundary evaluation
$$f(z_1,z_2)\longmapsto
  \overline{\mathrm{Tr}\,f(\phi_1,\phi_2)}$$
identifies the degree-zero generators and intertwines the closed Hamiltonian
Poisson bracket with the scalar-reduced open trace bracket.
\end{abstract}
