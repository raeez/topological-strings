\begin{abstract}
This paper studies a local constrained system. The formal holomorphic normal
surface has coordinates $z_1,z_2$ and symplectic form $dz_1\wedge dz_2$.
On a stack of $N$ probes these coordinates become matrices
$\phi_1,\phi_2$. The open ghost-zero theory is the Dirac first-order system
$$\int_\R \mathrm{Tr}\bigl(\phi_1\,d\phi_2+A[\phi_1,\phi_2]\bigr).$$
Here $A$ is the multiplier for the first-class constraint
$[\phi_1,\phi_2]=0$, and the BV/Koszul variable $\psi=A^*$ records it by
$Q\psi=[\phi_1,\phi_2]$. This is the open local model for a mixed
holomorphic-topological BV sector on $\R^2\times\C^2$ with brane
$\R\times p$.

After removing constant Hamiltonians and passing to the degreewise stable
connected trace sector, the classical open Hamiltonian algebra is
$$\mathbf S\bigl(\bar A\oplus\bar A_{\mathrm{desc}}[1]\bigr),
  \qquad \bar A=\C[z_1,z_2]/\C\cdot1,$$
where $\bar A_{\mathrm{desc}}$ is represented by the nonconstant primitive
one-antifield Koszul cycles. The closed Hamiltonian sector is cotangent BF
theory for the reduced Tate Lie algebra
$$\widehat{\mathfrak h}=\C[[z_1,z_2]]/\C\cdot1,\qquad
  \mathfrak g=\widehat{\mathfrak h}\ltimes
  \widehat{\mathfrak h}^{\vee}_{\mathrm{cont}}[1],$$
with bracket induced by the usual Poisson bracket after projection modulo
constants.
Its local observables are the continuous Chevalley-Eilenberg cochains
$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g)$. The reduced derived
Poisson center of the degree-zero open algebra is computed explicitly:
$$C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g)
  \cong
  \bigl(PV_{\mathrm{cont}}(\widehat{\mathbf S}(\mathfrak h)),
  [\pi_\partial,-]\bigr),$$
with $c_I\mapsto\partial/\partial O_I$ and $u_I\mapsto O_I$. Thus the boundary
Hamiltonian function is the image of the shifted cotangent CE coordinate, not
of the nonclosed CE coordinate dual to a Hamiltonian mode.

The Koszul dual of the closed CE algebra is the completed enveloping algebra
$\widehat U(\mathfrak g)$. Its PBW special fibre identifies degree by degree
with the connected stable open Hamiltonian trace sector as a filtered
graded-commutative product. The polynomial descendants are PBW special-fibre
labels; they do not realize the closed coadjoint deformation-level action.
Principal parts provide the correct coadjoint algebraic model. Boundary
evaluation on Hamiltonian modes
$$f(z_1,z_2)\longmapsto
  \overline{\mathrm{Tr}\,f(\phi_1,\phi_2)}$$
identifies the degree-zero generators and intertwines the closed Hamiltonian
Poisson bracket with the connected open trace bracket. This degree-zero
Hamiltonian current map has an interval-wise ordinary central-operation
representative after the de Rham jet contraction and satisfies the $P_0$
bracket identity on stable connected local cohomology. The full unreduced
BV/factorization-center lift remains a problem: compact-support functoriality
before contraction, $P_0$ center homotopies, principal-part boundary currents,
and descent must all be constructed.

The formal deformation-quantization target is the $\hbar$-adic Weyl/Moyal
deformation. The first-order bracket is proved, and the all-order Weyl/Moyal
local coefficients are computed algebraically. At finite $N$, Weyl-ordered
traces descend through quantum Hamiltonian reduction; the Harish-Chandra
radial-parts theorem proves the reduced Moyal commutator identity for the
Weyl/Capelli-renormalized generators. The stable connected quantum quotient and
the factorization-level quantum theorem remain open. The scalar
Hamiltonian/center-of-mass term and its Capelli quantum form are treated as a
named obstruction. The reduced open-line midpoint model gives the first and
third Weyl graph weights, but the paper does not construct the Costello
closed-open Feynman weights. The remaining analytic problem is to identify the
Costello boundary graph expansion, in this local Hamiltonian model, with the
Weyl/Moyal coefficients, including anomaly control, stable connected trace
normalization, and the first and third graph normalizations.
\end{abstract}
