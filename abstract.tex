\begin{abstract}
This paper studies a local constrained mechanical system. The formal
holomorphic normal surface has coordinates $z_1,z_2$ and symplectic form
$dz_1\wedge dz_2$. On a stack of $N$ probes these coordinates become
matrices $\phi_1,\phi_2\in\mathfrak{gl}_N$. The open ghost-zero theory is
the Dirac first-order system
$$\int_\R \mathrm{Tr}\bigl(\phi_1\,d\phi_2+A[\phi_1,\phi_2]\bigr).$$
Here $A$ is the multiplier for the first-class constraint
$[\phi_1,\phi_2]=0$, and the BV/Koszul variable $\psi=A^*$ records it by
$Q\psi=[\phi_1,\phi_2]$. This is the open local model for a mixed
holomorphic-topological BV sector on $\R^2\times\C^2$ with brane
$\R\times p$.

The infinitesimal canonical symmetries of the formal symplectic disk
form the reduced Tate Lie algebra
$\widehat{\mathfrak h}=\C[[z_1,z_2]]/\C\cdot 1$. Its shifted cotangent
semidirect extension
$\mathfrak g=\widehat{\mathfrak h}\ltimes
  \widehat{\mathfrak h}^{\vee}_{\mathrm{cont}}[1]$
is the dg Lie algebra of closed Hamiltonian BF theory. The classical
local theorem is the umbrella of six interlocking statements. There is a
universal cochain-level isomorphism of completed dg graded-commutative
$P_0$-algebras
$$\Phi:C^\bullet_{\mathrm{CE},\mathrm{cont}}(\mathfrak g)
  \xrightarrow{\sim}\mathrm{PV}\bigl(\widehat{\mathbf S}_{\mathrm{Tate}}
  (\widehat{\mathfrak h})\bigr)
  \simeq Z^{P_0}_{\mathrm{fact}}\bigl(\widehat{\mathbf S}_{\mathrm{Tate}}
  (\widehat{\mathfrak h})\bigr),$$
sending the CE coordinate $c^I$ dual to the Hamiltonian generator
$H_I\in\widehat{\mathfrak h}$ to the constant polyvector
$\theta^I=\partial/\partial O_I$, and the CE coordinate $u_I$ dual to the
shifted cotangent generator $\eta_{\delta_I}$ to the boundary Hamiltonian
function $O_I=\overline{\mathrm{Tr}\,H_I(\phi_1,\phi_2)}$. The boundary
trace observable is the image of the shifted cotangent CE coordinate, not
of the unclosed CE coordinate dual to a Hamiltonian mode. The continuous
dual $\widehat{\mathfrak h}^{\vee}_{\mathrm{cont}}$ is canonically the
principal-part residue module $\mathcal P=\bigoplus_{a+b>0}\C\,\rho_{a,b}$
with $\rho_{a,b}=z_1^{-a-1}z_2^{-b-1}\,dz_1\,dz_2$, paired by the
Grothendieck residue. The polynomial Koszul descendants $\Psi_{a,b}$ are
PBW special-fibre representatives carrying a raising action; the principal
parts $\rho_{a,b}$ are deformation-correct coadjoint representatives
carrying the lowering action; the Rees parameter $\hbar$ of the formal
Moyal deformation interpolates them.

The cochain isomorphism upgrades to a quasi-isomorphism of locally
constant $P_0$ factorization algebras on the brane line $\R$, in the
connected Hamiltonian sub-sector, with strict factorization product on
disjoint supports and equal-time delta-function bracket. It deforms to
all orders in $\hbar$ on the degree-zero Hamiltonian sector: the
Capelli/Weyl-renormalized stable trace $J_N(f)$ realizes the all-order
Moyal target, the Harish-Chandra radial-parts identity gives the reduced
Moyal commutator at all $N$, and the Costello first/third graph
normalizations are fixed by Kontsevich uniqueness. The scalar
Hamiltonian/center-of-mass mode carries a single nontrivial class
$[\bar c]\in H^2_{\mathrm{Lie}}(\bar A,\C)$ realised identically by the
classical Poisson cocycle $\omega(z_1^kz_2^l,z_1^mz_2^n)=
(kn-lm)\mathbf 1[(k+m,l+n)=(1,1)]$ and the quantum Capelli shift
$YX-XY+\hbar NI=0$; the equivalence holds modulo $[\bar c]$.

The unreduced Tate-coefficient cotangent lift is closed by five
interlocking routes. The weighted Tate completion with exponential
weight $w(d)=q^d$, $q>1$, makes the formal Casimir converge as a kernel
in $\mathfrak h_w\,\widehat\otimes\,\mathfrak h^{\vee,w}_{\mathrm{cont}}$,
extends the Costello heat-kernel propagator, and is preserved by the
RG flow on polynomial-degree-bounded interactions. The nilpotent
truncation $\mathfrak m^3/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ closes the full cotangent
factorization-center lift unconditionally on the truncated sector, in
classical and degree-zero quantum form. The naive truncation
$\mathfrak m^2/\mathfrak m^{N+1}$ fails because $H_2=\mathfrak{sl}_2$
is semisimple. The Tate $\infty$-categorical Quillen adjunction
$\Omega\dashv\mathrm{Bar}$, transferred via Hinich/Berger--Moerdijk
and Lefevre--Hasegawa, promotes the strict cochain $\Phi$ to an
equivalence of presentable $\infty$-categories of completed dg
$P_0$-algebras (independent of Casimir convergence). The chain-level
primitive projection
$\pi_{\mathrm{prim}}\colon\mathrm{Obs}^{\mathrm{cl}}_{\mathrm{open},N}(I)
\to\Omega^\bullet_c(I)\,\widehat\otimes\,\bar A$
is constructed from Procesi--Razmyslov cycle merging and the de Rham
contraction, with $P_0$-bracket homotopy identically zero on every
trace-count stratum, and is independent of any analytic propagator.
The Costello-locality compatibility of the Hadamard parametrix on a
Mittag-Leffler coefficient module is closed by the exact tensor
factorization of the Costello propagator $P^w_{\epsilon,L}=
P^{\mathrm{base}}_{\epsilon,L}\,\widehat\otimes\,C_{\mathfrak h,w}$:
the spacetime piece carries all analytic content, the coefficient
piece is a purely algebraic Casimir with empty wavefront, and
H\"ormander's tensor wavefront theorem makes the Mittag-Leffler limit
on the coefficient side commute with bulk locality. The all-order
quantum derived-center isomorphism extends unconditionally to the
weighted cotangent direction at all orders in $\hbar$. The umbrella
theorem is therefore unconditional in all six statements, with no
remaining sub-residual. Cross-volume convention contracts to
\textasciitilde/calabi-yau-quantum-groups (Vol III) and
\textasciitilde/igusa-cusp-form ($\Delta_5$/BKM) are local-only and
explicitly non-transferable without separate matched-conventions proofs.
\end{abstract}
