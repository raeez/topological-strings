\section{Cross-volume convention contract}\label{app:convention-contract}

This appendix records the bidirectional convention contract with the
cross-repository frontiers cited in
\texttt{CLAUDE.md}: the Vol III CY-to-chiral programme
(\texttt{\textasciitilde/calabi-yau-quantum-groups}), the Igusa
square-root / BKM Borcherds programme
(\texttt{\textasciitilde/igusa-cusp-form}), and the Vol I / Vol II
chiral bar-cobar programmes
(\texttt{\textasciitilde/chiral-bar-cobar}, \texttt{\textasciitilde/chiral-bar-cobar-vol2}).
It is bidirectional: each cross-volume transfer is recorded both
forward (constituents of the present umbrella that a sister volume
could use) and backward (machinery from a sister volume that this
manuscript could use to close residuals), with the convention
discrepancy and the matched-conventions proof obligation that would
license the transfer named in each direction.

The umbrella Theorem~\ref{thm:main-local} is the local
$d=\dim_{\C}X=2$ closed-open derived-centre theorem, with six
interlocking constituents (a)--(f). Each constituent has a different
cross-volume profile, recorded below. The bidirectional dictionary
makes the cross-volume interface auditable: each transfer states the
convention discrepancy, the proof that would license the transfer, and
the reachability assessment. Downstream cross-volume work has a
concrete target.
