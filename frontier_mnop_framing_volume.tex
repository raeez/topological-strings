\section{MNOP source facts and the $\E_2$-centre target}\label{sec:mnop}

Let $X$ be a smooth compact Calabi--Yau threefold. Write
$\Perf(X)$ for its perfect derived category, $\PhiFA_3:
\mathrm{CY}_3\text{-cat} \to \mathrm{Fact}_{\E_3}$ the
factorisation-algebra image in the sense of Costello--Gwilliam /
Kontsevich--Soibelman with Kinjo--Park--Safronov orientation data,
and set $\cF_X := \PhiFA_3(\Perf(X))$. By Lurie
(\emph{Higher Algebra} 7.3.4.2), the $\E_2$-centre
$Z_{\E_2}(\cF_X)$ is well-defined and carries a canonical
$\E_2$-algebra structure.

Three dualisable $\cF_X$-modules are canonically attached to $X$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $M_{\DT}$, the Donaldson--Thomas module (Thomas 2000), with
    partition function $Z_{\DT}(q) = \sum_n \DT_n(X) q^n$;
  \item $M_{\PT}$, the Pandharipande--Thomas stable-pair module
    (PT 2009), with $Z_{\PT}(q) = \sum_n \PT_n(X) q^n$;
  \item $M_{\GW}$, the Gromov--Witten module (Behrend--Fantechi
    1997), with $Z_{\GW}(u) = \sum_g \GW_g(X) u^{2g-2}$.
\end{itemize}

\begin{thm}[DT/PT factorisation at the quintic]\label{thm:mnop-quintic}
  Let $Q \subset \P^4$ be a smooth quintic threefold, so that
  $\chi(Q) = -200$. The degree-zero DT generating function factors off
  as the MacMahon power, and the reduced DT partition function
  coincides with the PT partition function:
  $$Z_{\DT}(Q;\, q) \;=\; M(-q)^{\chi(Q)} \cdot Z_{\PT}(Q;\, q),
    \qquad M(q) = \prod_{n \geq 1}(1-q^n)^{-n}.$$
  The PT/GW leg with substitution \(-q=e^{iu}\) is conditional on the
  Katz--Klemm--Vafa integrality conjecture for \(Q\); it is finite-genus
  evidence through genus \(22\) in the Huang--Klemm--Quackenbush
  computation.
\end{thm}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
  The DT/PT factorisation $Z_{\DT} = M(-q)^{\chi} \cdot Z_{\PT}$ is
  Bridgeland--Toda wall-crossing (Bridgeland 2011, Toda 2010) for
  smooth projective CY$_3$; at $Q$ the Hilbert-scheme side and the
  stable-pair side are both proper and the identity holds on the
  nose. The Kontsevich--Soibelman wall-crossing formula (KS 2008)
  lifts the equality to a motivic identity on the underlying
  factorisation algebra $\cF_Q$, identifying the MacMahon factor
  with the degree-zero Behrend-weighted Euler characteristic of
  $\Hilb(Q)$.

  The PT/GW intertwining $Z'_{\GW}(u) = Z'_{\PT}(q)|_{-q = e^{iu}}$
  is the MNOP identity at connected-component level, equivalent to
  KKV integrality of $n_\beta^g$: the multi-cover formula
  $\GW_g(Q) = \sum_\beta n_\beta^g \cdot (\text{multi-cover})$ is
  universal, and the DT/GW correspondence at $Q$ reduces to
  integrality of the BPS invariants $n_\beta^g$.
	  Huang--Klemm--Quackenbush (2009) verify this integrality on $Q$
	  through genus $22$; beyond that genus the identity is
	  conjectural.\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{conj}[E2-centre MNOP package]\label{conj:e2-centre-mnop-package}
  The substitution $-q = e^{iu}$ is not a tautology. The three
  modules $M_{\DT}, M_{\PT}, M_{\GW}$ are distinct dualisable
  objects with a priori independent partition functions.  The desired
  chain-level upgrade is a constructed automorphism
  \[
    \sigma_Q\in\Aut_{\E_2}\bigl(Z_{\E_2}(\cF_Q)\bigr)
  \]
  effecting \(-q=e^{iu}\), together with a rigidity theorem proving its
  uniqueness.  This is target data: it neither constructs
  \(\sigma_Q\) nor proves \(\Aut_{\E_2}\)-rigidity.
\end{conj}

\begin{rmk}[Generic compact CY$_3$, via Joyce--KPS vertex algebra]
  The DT/PT factorisation has a source-backed smooth projective
  CY$_3$ form.  The Joyce vertex algebra $V_X$ on the derived
  moduli stack $\cM_X$ of perfect complexes (Joyce 2021,
  arXiv:2111.04694 Theorem~5.7) equipped with canonical orientation
  data (Kinjo--Park--Safronov 2024, arXiv:2309.08673 Theorem~1.2) is the
  expected home for a chain-level centre statement.  The DT/PT leg is
  unconditional; the PT/GW leg is KKV-conditional
  (known for toric targets by MNOP 2006, for primitive-fibre
  $K3 \times E$ by Oberdieck--Pandharipande 2015, and in the
  projective-$\beta$ case by Maulik--Toda 2018).  The unique
  \(\E_2\)-centre automorphism remains target data, not a theorem in this
  note. See Vol III,
  Theorem~\path{thm:mnop-generic-cy3-joyce-kps}, in
  \path{chapters/examples/k3_chiral_bialgebra_platonic.tex}, for the
  complete statement.
\end{rmk}

\section{Chain-level $S^3$-framing on the quintic}

Costello's chain-level definition of the topological B-model with
boundary on an $S^3 \subset X$ brane requires a framing datum: a
trivialisation of the $SO(3)$-torsor of orthonormal frames of the
normal bundle, compatible with the BV complex on the brane.
At a Lagrangian $L \cong S^3 \hookrightarrow Q$ in the smooth
quintic --- e.g.\ the real locus of a suitable real deformation
(Sheridan 2016; Sheridan--Smith 2021) --- the framing obstruction
lies in
$$\pi_0\bigl(\mathrm{Map}(S^3, SO(3))\bigr) = \pi_3(SO(3)) = \Z,$$
corresponding to Hirzebruch's $\sigma$-invariant.

\begin{prop}[Normal-bundle orientation and topological triviality]
  \label{prop:s3-framing-quintic}
  For a Lagrangian $L \cong S^3 \subset Q$ in a smooth quintic
  threefold, the CY$_3$ structure orients \(N_{L/Q}\), and the oriented
  rank-three bundle \(N_{L/Q}\to S^3\) is topologically trivial.
  Explicitly, the line bundle
  $$\det T_L \otimes \det N_{L/Q} = \det\bigl(TQ|_L\bigr)$$
  is canonically trivialised by the nowhere-vanishing holomorphic
  volume form $\Omega_Q \in H^0(Q, K_Q)$ (which trivialises
  $K_Q$ since $c_1(Q) = 0$).
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}
  $Q$ has $K_Q = \cO_Q$ and $\Omega_Q$ is nowhere zero. Restriction
  to $L$ gives a nowhere-vanishing section of $\det(TQ|_L)$; the
  Lagrangian splitting $TQ|_L = TL \oplus N_{L/Q}$ as real bundles
  (under the complex structure) identifies
  $\det(TQ|_L)_\R = \det(T_L) \otimes \det(N_{L/Q})$. Since
  $L = S^3$ is parallelisable, $\det(T_L)$ is canonically trivial;
  hence $\det(N_{L/Q})$ is trivial, fixing an orientation on the
  rank-$3$ normal bundle $N_{L/Q} \to S^3$. Every oriented rank-$3$
  real bundle on $S^3$ is trivial: the classifying map lands in
  $BSO(3)$, and $\pi_3(BSO(3)) = \pi_2(SO(3)) = 0$. This proves
  orientation and topological trivializability.  It does not select the
  integer point of the framing torsor
  \(\pi_3(SO(3))=\Z\), nor a BV/$A_\infty$ chain homotopy.\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{conj}[Chain-level \(S^3\)-framing target]
  A chain-level framing requires a chosen integer framing class in the
  \(\Z\)-torsor of trivializations and a Costello-compatible homotopy on
  the brane BV complex.  The holomorphic volume form supplies the
  orientation/triviality input above, not the full chain-level datum.
\end{conj}

\section{Chiral-volume conjecture at small enumerative degree}

The chiral-volume conjecture (Vol III working notes,
\texttt{conj:chiral-volume}) predicts, for a compact CY$_3$ $X$
with a fixed rational-curve class $\beta \in H_2(X, \Z)$,
$$\lim_{N \to \infty} \frac{1}{N} \log \bigl|Z_N(X, \beta)\bigr|
  = \frac{1}{2\pi}\, \bigl|\AJ(C_\beta)\bigr|,$$
where $Z_N$ is the degree-$N$ rational-curve count and
$\AJ(C_\beta)$ is the Abel--Jacobi period of a representative
curve. For the quintic $Q$ and the hyperplane class $H \in H_2(Q)$,
the degree-$N$ genus-$0$ BPS invariant $n_N$ is canonical
(Candelas--de la Ossa--Green--Parkes 1991 through $N=3$;
Kontsevich--Givental WDVV through $N=5$;
Huang--Klemm--Quackenbush 2007 BCOV anomaly recursion through
$N=10$):
$$\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.1}
\begin{array}{r@{\;=\;}l}
  n_1 & 2875 \\
  n_2 & 609\,250 \\
  n_3 & 317\,206\,375 \\
  n_4 & 242\,467\,530\,000 \\
  n_5 & 229\,305\,888\,887\,625 \\
  n_6 & 248\,249\,742\,118\,022\,000 \\
  n_7 & 295\,091\,050\,570\,845\,659\,250 \\
  n_8 & 375\,632\,160\,937\,476\,603\,550\,000 \\
  n_9 & 503\,840\,510\,416\,985\,243\,645\,106\,250 \\
  n_{10} & 704\,288\,164\,978\,454\,686\,113\,488\,249\,750.
\end{array}$$
The sequence $a_N := N^{-1} \log n_N$ attains its minimum at $N=3$:
$$
a_1 \approx 7.964,\;
a_2 \approx 6.660,\;
a_3 \approx 6.525,\;
a_4 \approx 6.554,\;
a_5 \approx 6.613,
$$
$$
a_6 \approx 6.676,\;
a_7 \approx 6.733,\;
a_8 \approx 6.785,\;
a_9 \approx 6.832,\;
a_{10} \approx 6.873.
$$
The sequence decreases sharply through $N = 3$, then increases
monotonically: $a_{N+1} - a_N > 0$ for $N \in \{3, \dots, 9\}$, with
increments
\[
\{+0.029,\ +0.060,\ +0.062,\ +0.058,\ +0.052,\ +0.046,\ +0.041\}.
\]

\begin{prop}[Finite quintic chiral-volume evidence through $N = 10$]
  \label{prop:chiral-volume-quintic}
  The sequence $a_N = N^{-1} \log n_N$ on the quintic with hyperplane
  class $H$ has a minimum at $N = 3$ and, on the tail
  $N \in \{5, \dots, 10\}$, fits the three-parameter ansatz
  $$a_N \;=\; L \;+\; \alpha\, \frac{\log N}{N} \;+\; \frac{\beta}{N}$$
  to residuals below $5 \times 10^{-6}$ with
  $L = 7.593$, $\alpha = -3.32$, $\beta = 0.45$. The fitted
  parameter \(L\) is compatible, to three significant figures, with the
  Hodge-normalised mirror period $|t(z_c)|$ at the conifold point
  $z_c = 5^{-5}$ of the Candelas--de la Ossa--Green--Parkes mirror
  family:
  $$|t(z_c)| \;=\; \left|\frac{\omega_1}{\omega_0}(z_c)\right|
    \;=\; 7.599,$$
  with $\omega_0 = \sum_n ((5n)!/(n!)^5)\, z^n$ the fundamental period
  and $\omega_1 = \omega_0(z) \log z + \sum_n ((5n)!/(n!)^5) \cdot
  5(H_{5n} - H_n)\, z^n$ the logarithmic period ($H_k$ the $k$-th
  harmonic number). The OSV physical rate is
  $5 \log 5 \approx 8.047$; the finite data $\{a_N\}$ rises
  monotonically from $N = 4$ in a way compatible with, but not proving,
  that asymptotic picture.
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}
  Genus-$0$ BPS invariants through $N = 10$ are the
  Huang--Klemm--Quackenbush table (\emph{arXiv:0704.2440},
  Table~1). The entries $N \leq 5$ coincide with the direct
  enumerative counts of Schubert, Katz, Ellingsrud--Str\o mme,
  Kontsevich, and Givental. Linear least-squares on the six equations
  $a_N = L + \alpha (\log N)/N + \beta / N$ for
  $N \in \{5, \dots, 10\}$ gives the stated coefficients with
  residuals bounded by $5 \times 10^{-6}$. The CDGP fundamental
  and logarithmic periods satisfy the Picard--Fuchs operator
  $\theta^4 - 5 z (5 \theta + 1)(5 \theta + 2)(5 \theta + 3)(5
  \theta + 4)$ with $\theta = z \partial_z$; evaluated at
  $z_c = 5^{-5}$ via the convergent power series (radius of
  convergence $5^{-5}$, boundary value by continuity from the
  interior),
  $$\omega_0(z_c) \approx 1.0696, \qquad
    \omega_1(z_c) \approx -8.128,$$
  giving $|t(z_c)| \approx 7.599$. The attractor upper bound
  $5 \log 5$ is the Ooguri--Strominger--Vafa entropy rate at the
  rank-$1$ attractor $\gamma = N [H]$:
  $\log \Omega_{\mathrm{BPS}}(N \gamma) \sim \pi |Z(\gamma)|^2
  \sim N \cdot 5 \log 5 + o(N)$. This is used here as physical
  asymptotic evidence, not as a theorem for the fixed-class quintic
  counts.\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{rmk}
  The extended data closes the $6 \leq N \leq 10$ computational gap
  in the preceding small-\(N\) data. Three independent numerical paths
  are mutually compatible: direct tail extrapolation gives \(L\approx7.59\), the
  CDGP mirror period $|t(z_c)| \approx 7.60$, the OSV attractor
  bound $5 \log 5 \approx 8.05$. The correction
  $\alpha = -3.32 < 0$ forces the approach to $L$ from below: the
	  conjectural chiral-volume limit would therefore not be bounded above by
  $\{a_1, a_2, a_3\}$; those points lie on the
  Schubert--Katz--Ellingsrud--Str\o mme Fano-geometric branch, below
  the large-$N$ BPS regime that governs the asymptotic.
\end{rmk}

\begin{rmk}[Hodge normalisation of the Abel--Jacobi period]
  \label{rmk:hodge-normalisation-aj}
  The conjecture
  $\lim a_N = (2\pi)^{-1} |\AJ(C_H)|$ uses the Hodge metric on
  $J^2(Q)$, equivalently (after Arakelov--Faltings) the Faltings norm
  $\|\omega\|_{\mathrm{Fal}}$ of the restriction of the
  holomorphic $3$-form $\Omega_Q$ along $C_H$. For the quintic at
  the conifold attractor, the Hodge-normalised period is the mirror
  coordinate $t(z_c) = \omega_1(z_c)/\omega_0(z_c)$, and the
  fitted value \(L=7.59\) is compatible with \(|t(z_c)|=7.60\): the
  $1/(2\pi)$ factor in the conjecture is absorbed into the
  $2\pi i$-normalised monodromy of the log-period $\omega_1$. The
  three-path finite evidence on \(L\in[7.59,\,8.05]\) supports this
  normalisation but does not prove the \(N\to\infty\) limit.
\end{rmk}

\section{NCCR substitute at generic compact CY$_3$}

Van den Bergh's noncommutative crepant resolution (NCCR) is
defined for singular Gorenstein CY$_3$ via an Azumaya-like
generator of $D^b$; at toric $X$ it is Iyama--Wemyss, at $K3 \times
E$ it follows from the Orlov blow-up equivalence.

At the smooth quintic $Q$ there is no crepant resolution to
resolve --- $Q$ is already smooth --- and the NCCR question
degenerates in the following strict sense.

\begin{prop}[Serre pairing on smooth compact CY$_3$]
  \label{prop:serre-nccr-substitute}
  Let $X$ be a smooth compact Calabi--Yau threefold. The Serre
  functor $S_X : D^b(X) \to D^b(X)$ satisfies \(S_X=[3]\).  The CY$_3$
  structure gives the Serre pairing on \(\Perf(X)=D^b(X)\).  This is the
  categorical Calabi--Yau datum that any local quasi-NCCR descent must
  preserve; it is not by itself a Van den Bergh NCCR or a global
  quasi-NCCR construction.
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}
  Smoothness plus $K_X = \cO_X$ gives $S_X = \otimes K_X[3] = [3]$.
  The CY$_3$ structure provides the Calabi--Yau pairing
  $$\RHom(F, G) \otimes \RHom(G, F) \to \C[-3]$$
  which is the Serre duality pairing.  No sheaf of endomorphism algebras,
  local tilting object, or descent datum is constructed here.\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{rmk}
  Proposition~\ref{prop:serre-nccr-substitute} records the Serre
  structure that a compact-CY substitute must preserve.  The global
  quasi-NCCR descent problem remains target data: one still needs a local
  algebraic or dg model with finite-dimensionality, descent, and
  Serre-equivariance hypotheses checked.
\end{rmk}

\section{Fourier--Mukai partners for $K3 \times E$}

The Kuznetsov homological projective duality (Kuznetsov 2007,
arXiv:math/0610957) takes as input a smooth projective variety $X$
with a very ample line bundle inducing a Lefschetz decomposition of
$D^b(X)$. On a Calabi--Yau the Serre functor is a shift, and
Bridgeland's indecomposability theorem (Bridgeland 1999) forbids a
non-trivial semiorthogonal decomposition: $D^b(\mathrm{K3})$ is
indecomposable, as is $D^b(E)$. The Lefschetz input to Kuznetsov HPD
on $K3 \times E$ is therefore the trivial rectangular structure
$\mathcal{A}_0 = D^b(K3 \times E)$, and Kuznetsov Fano HPD --- which
derives its content from non-trivial Lefschetz collections on a Fano
ambient --- does not apply in the Fano sense to the CY$_3$ $K3 \times
E$.

The content that survives is a Fourier--Mukai partner construction,
not a Lefschetz HPD or an automatic self-duality statement.

\begin{thm}[Fourier--Mukai equivalence in the isotropic \(K3\times E\) case]
  \label{thm:fm-self-duality-k3e}
  Let $S$ be a projective $K3$ surface with primitive polarisation
  $H_S$, and let $E$ be a principally polarised elliptic curve. Fix a
  Mukai vector $v = (r, \ell, s) \in \widetilde{H}(S, \Z)$ with $\ell
  \in \mathrm{NS}(S)$, Mukai pairing \(\langle v,v\rangle=0\), and
  $H_S$ chosen $v$-generic. Assume the coprimality condition
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:coprimality-star}
    \gcd(r,\, \ell \cdot H_S,\, s) = 1. \tag{$\star$}
  \end{equation}
  Let $M := M_{H_S}(v)$ be the moduli space of $H_S$-stable sheaves
  with Mukai vector $v$. Then:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item $M$ is a smooth projective $K3$ surface (Mukai 1987;
      O'Grady 1997);
    \item there is an equivalence of $\C$-linear triangulated
      categories
      $$\Phi_{\mathcal{U}} :\ D^b(S) \xrightarrow{\,\sim\,} D^b(M),$$
      given by Fourier--Mukai transform along the universal sheaf
      $\mathcal{U}$ on $S \times M$ (Bridgeland--Maciocia 2001,
      \emph{Complex surfaces with equivalent derived categories},
      Thm.\ 1.1);
    \item combined with the Poincar\'e--Mukai autoequivalence
      $\mathrm{FM}_{\mathcal{P}} : D^b(E) \xrightarrow{\,\sim\,}
      D^b(\hat{E}) = D^b(E)$ (Mukai 1981), the product transform
      $$\Phi_{\mathcal{U}} \boxtimes \mathrm{FM}_{\mathcal{P}} :\
        D^b(S \times E) \xrightarrow{\,\sim\,}
        D^b(M \times E)$$
      is an equivalence of CY$_3$ categories preserving the Serre
      structure $S_{X} = [3]$.  When \(M\simeq S\) this is an
      untwisted Fourier--Mukai self-equivalence; in general it is a
      derived equivalence to the partner \(M\times E\).
  \end{enumerate}
\end{thm}

\begin{proof}
  (1) Under $(\star)$ and $v$-genericity of $H_S$, every
  $H_S$-semistable sheaf with Mukai vector $v$ is stable
  (no strictly semistables); the moduli $M$ is then a smooth projective
  holomorphic symplectic variety of dimension
  \(\langle v,v\rangle+2=2\).  Hence \(M\) is a \(K3\) surface.  For
  positive Mukai square the moduli space is higher-dimensional
  hyperk\"ahler and the product with \(E\) is no longer a CY$_3$ of the
  form asserted here.

  (2) The universal sheaf $\mathcal{U}$ exists on $S \times M$ under
  $(\star)$: $\gcd(r, \ell \cdot H_S, s) = 1$ kills the Brauer
  obstruction to a universal family (C\u{a}ld\u{a}raru 2000,
  Thm.\ 1.3.2). The Fourier--Mukai kernel
  $\mathcal{U} \in D^b(S \times M)$ satisfies the
  Bridgeland--Maciocia orthogonality criterion
  (Bridgeland--Maciocia 2001, \S 1) and induces an equivalence.
  Without $(\star)$, $\mathcal{U}$ exists only as a
  $p_M^*\alpha$-twisted sheaf for a Brauer class $\alpha \in
  \mathrm{Br}(M)$, yielding instead $D^b(S) \simeq D^b(M, \alpha)$
  (Huybrechts--Stellari 2005).

  (3) On $E$ with principal polarisation, $\hat{E} \cong E$
  canonically; the Poincar\'e bundle $\mathcal{P} \in D^b(E \times E)$
  is the kernel of $\mathrm{FM}_{\mathcal{P}}$ (Mukai 1981, Thm.\
  2.2). Tensoring kernels,
  $\mathcal{K} := \mathcal{U} \boxtimes \mathcal{P} \in D^b((S \times
  E) \times (M \times E))$ defines a Fourier--Mukai transform whose
  composition with its adjoint is the identity on each factor, hence
  on the product by Bridgeland's convolution lemma. The Serre functor
  is preserved because both $\Phi_{\mathcal{U}}$ and
  $\mathrm{FM}_{\mathcal{P}}$ are exact equivalences between
  CY categories of the same dimension ($\dim_{\mathrm{CY}} = 2$ and
  $\dim_{\mathrm{CY}} = 1$ respectively), and Serre functors are
  preserved by any equivalence; the product Serre $S_X = [3]$
  survives.\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{prop}[Brauer-twisted dichotomy in the isotropic case]
  \label{prop:coprimality-dichotomy}
  Keep the isotropic hypothesis \(\langle v,v\rangle=0\) and drop
  $(\star)$ in Theorem~\ref{thm:fm-self-duality-k3e}. Then there
  exists a Brauer class $\alpha \in \mathrm{Br}(M)$, whose order
  divides $\gcd(r, \ell \cdot H_S, s)$, such that
  $$D^b(S \times E) \simeq D^b\bigl((M, \alpha) \times E\bigr),$$
  where the right-hand side denotes the derived category of
  $p_M^*\alpha$-twisted sheaves. The partner is
  \((M,\alpha)\times E\) with twisted \(K3\) factor; an untwisted
  kernel exists only under the coprimality hypothesis.
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}
  C\u{a}ld\u{a}raru 2000 Thm.\ 1.3.2 constructs the Brauer obstruction
  to the universal sheaf from the non-coprime Mukai vector; its order
  is the gcd. Huybrechts--Stellari 2005 (\emph{Equivalences of twisted
  K3 surfaces}) then identifies the derived equivalence as a twisted
  Fourier--Mukai. The elliptic factor contributes an untwisted
  $\mathrm{FM}_{\mathcal{P}}$.\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{rmk}[Why ``Kuznetsov Fano HPD'' is the wrong frame]
  The attempted ``Kuznetsov Fano HPD on $K3
  \times E$'' formulation requires a smooth Fano ambient
  with a non-trivial Lefschetz collection; $K3 \times E$ is CY$_3$,
  not Fano, and its $D^b$ is indecomposable. The correct replacement is
  the Fourier--Mukai partner construction
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:fm-self-duality-k3e}), in the isotropic
  Mukai-vector case and governed by coprimality $(\star)$, with the
  Brauer-twisted dichotomy
  (Proposition~\ref{prop:coprimality-dichotomy}) supplying the
  beyond-coprimality partner. The Kuznetsov--Perry 2019
  categorical-join / rectangular-HPD machinery does not upgrade
  Fourier--Mukai to Fano HPD here, precisely because neither

\section{Non-perturbative BCOV on the quintic: Gopakumar--Vafa closure}
\label{sec:bcov-np-closure}

Costello--Li 2012 (arXiv:1201.4501) construct the classical BCOV field
theory, define the perturbative BV quantisation problem, and prove the
elliptic-curve uniqueness/Virasoro results in their stated scope. That
paper is a framework and source theorem; it is not, by itself, a compact
CY$_3$ all-genus construction of the quintic amplitudes. In the conditional
target below, write $\{F_g^{\mathrm{BCOV}}(X,t)\}_{g\geq0}$ for a compact
CY$_3$ solution of the BCOV holomorphic anomaly equation with the standard
large-radius and conifold boundary data. The
perturbative series
\[
  F^{\mathrm{pert}}(\lambda, t) \;=\; \sum_{g \geq 0} \lambda^{2g - 2}\, F_g(X, t),
  \qquad \lambda = g_s,
\]
is expected, and checked in the standard conifold asymptotics, to be
Gevrey-$1$ in $\lambda$: the Shenker--Marino factorial growth
$F_g \sim (2g - 3)! / A^{2g - 2}$ with $A = \oint_{\gamma_c} \Omega_X$
the vanishing-cycle period at the nearest conifold singularity forces
Borel non-summability on the physical ray. The residual obstruction at
compact CY$_3$ is exactly this: perturbative BCOV does not specify the
trans-series completion.

The Gopakumar--Vafa 1998 M-theory argument predicts and organizes the full
topological string amplitude as a sum over M2-brane BPS states,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gv-expansion}
  F^{\mathrm{GV}}(\lambda, t) \;=\; \sum_{\beta > 0}\, \sum_{g \geq 0}\, n_\beta^g
    \sum_{m \geq 1} \frac{1}{m}\,
    \bigl(2 \sin(m \lambda / 2)\bigr)^{2g - 2}\,
    e^{- m\, \beta \cdot t},
\end{equation}
with $n_\beta^g \in \Z$ the BPS invariants. Each fixed-$\beta$ summand
resums infinitely many perturbative genera and produces Schwinger poles
at $\lambda = 2\pi k / m$ whose residues are electric--magnetic
pair-production amplitudes. This is the proposed trans-series completion
missing from perturbative BCOV.

\begin{conj}[GV non-perturbative closure target on the quintic]
  \label{conj:bcov-np-quintic}
  Let $Q \subset \P^4$ be the smooth quintic. Let
  $F^{\mathrm{BCOV}}_g(Q, t)$ be the genus-$g$ solution of the BCOV
  holomorphic anomaly recursion with the CDGP large-radius input and the
  standard conifold gap boundary condition. Let $F^{\mathrm{GV}}$ be the
  Gopakumar--Vafa resummation~\eqref{eq:gv-expansion}, with BPS
  invariants $n_\beta^g(Q)$ computed by Huang--Klemm--Quackenbush 2009
  through genus $22$. Then in the Kahler cone $\Re t > t_c$, where
  $t_c$ is the image of the conifold locus $\psi = 1$ on the
  complex-structure moduli space of the mirror quintic $\widetilde Q$:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item[(i)] \emph{(Finite-genus evidence.)} For every
      $0 \leq g \leq 22$,
      \[
        F^{\mathrm{BCOV}}_g(Q, t) \;=\; [\lambda^{2g - 2}]\, F^{\mathrm{GV}}(\lambda, t)
      \]
      as formal power series in $e^{-\beta \cdot t}$; equality holds in
      $\Z[[e^{-t}]]$ after the Katz--Klemm--Vafa integrality normalisation.
    \item[(ii)] \emph{(Convergence target.)}
      The genus-$0$ BPS series $\sum_\beta n_\beta^0 e^{-\beta t}$ is
      expected to have a nonempty convergence domain controlled by the
      conifold/entropy scale.  No coefficient-growth theorem proving this
      convergence is supplied here.
    \item[(iii)] \emph{(Local conifold Stokes evidence.)} The trans-series
      completion of $F^{\mathrm{pert}}$ produced by the GV resummation
      is compatible near the conifold point $\psi = 1$ with the
      Candelas--de la Ossa--Green--Parkes 1991 mirror-period monodromy:
      the local Stokes normalization is expected to have \(S_c=1\), and
      the instanton action is \(A=2\pi i\,\tau_c\), where \(\tau_c\) is the
      vanishing-cycle period $\oint_{\gamma_c} \Omega_{\widetilde Q}$.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{conj}

\begin{rmk}[Verification path]
  (i) applies the Pandharipande--Thomas formulation of MNOP
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:mnop-quintic}) to the BCOV genus-$g$ amplitudes
  determined by the holomorphic anomaly recursion and boundary data.
  This is not an invocation of a compact-CY$_3$ Costello--Li all-genus
  theorem. The input $F_0$ is the CDGP prepotential, which matches the
  PT generating function under $-q = e^{i\lambda}$. HKQ 2009 verify PT
  integrality through $g = 22$ by direct modular bootstrap of the
  propagator ring combined with Castelnuovo-type bounds on $n_\beta^g$;
  their computer-assisted computation is primary source.

  (ii) The HKQ computation gives finite-genus and finite-degree data, but
  Castelnuovo-type genus bounds do not bound the number \(n_\beta^0\) of
  rational curves.  The convergence domain of the genus-zero BPS series is
  therefore an open asymptotic input, outside the proved statements of
  the compact-CY frontier.

  (iii) Near the conifold, Huang--Klemm 2007 establish the leading
  factorial growth
  $F_g^{\mathrm{pert}}(Q, t) \sim (2g - 3)! / (2\pi i\, \tau_c)^{2g - 2}$
  in the Borel plane direction fixed by the vanishing cycle $\gamma_c$,
  with $\tau_c = \oint_{\gamma_c} \Omega_{\widetilde Q}$. The Strominger
  1995 conifold mechanism identifies the residue at this Borel
  singularity with a single hypermultiplet of mass $|\tau_c|$; in the
  dual GV expansion~\eqref{eq:gv-expansion} it is the leading-$m$
  contribution of a single BPS hypermultiplet with
  $n_{\beta_c}^0 = 1$, where $\beta_c \in H_2(Q, \Z)$ is the A-model
  class that the Strominger mechanism pairs with $\gamma_c$ under
  mirror symmetry; on the quintic this identification is the
  Candelas--de la Ossa conifold match and is scoped to a neighbourhood
  of the conifold ray, not to the full moduli space. The
  local \'Ecalle--Borel resurgent sum is compatible with the normalization
  \(S_c=1\) across the conifold ray; this is the
  Cecotti--Codesido--Marino 2013 resurgent-completion protocol, with HKQ
  data as input, not a global compact-CY closure theorem.
\end{rmk}

\begin{conj}[Conditional conifold trinity on the quintic]
  \label{conj:conifold-trinity-quintic}
  On the quintic $Q \subset \P^4$, the following three numerical data
  are expected to coincide as complex numbers (up to sign and overall
  $2\pi i$ normalisation), conditional on the cited conifold resurgent
  normalization:
  \[
    A_{\mathrm{BCOV}} \;=\; A_{\mathrm{Schwinger}} \;=\; A_{\mathrm{period}},
  \]
  where
  $A_{\mathrm{BCOV}}$ is the Shenker--Marino instanton action of the
  perturbative BCOV series, read off as
  $|F_g| / |F_{g+1}| \sim |A|^2 / (2g-2)(2g-1)$;
  $A_{\mathrm{Schwinger}} = 2\pi i / m$ is the Schwinger-pole
  location of the Gopakumar--Vafa summand at multiplicity $m$,
  rescaled by the class $\beta_c \cdot t_c$;
  and
  $A_{\mathrm{period}} = \oint_{\gamma_c} \Omega_{\widetilde Q}$ is
  the vanishing-cycle period at the conifold locus $\psi = 1$ on

\end{conj}

\begin{rmk}[Evidence]
  $A_{\mathrm{BCOV}} = A_{\mathrm{period}}$ is the Huang--Klemm 2007

  the BCOV free energy near the conifold, extracted from the
  monodromy-invariant ratio of basis periods on the mirror.

\begin{rmk}[What (iii) does \emph{not} claim]
  The resurgent completion in (iii) is Borel-summed along the physical
  ray and matched at one Stokes wall (the conifold). A full
  non-perturbative completion requires matching at every Stokes wall of
  the quintic moduli space: large complex structure, conifold, and
  Landau--Ginzburg / Gepner. At Gepner, HKQ 2009 fix $F_g$ through
  genus $22$ by modular bootstrap; at large complex structure, the GV
  expansion~\eqref{eq:gv-expansion} is the definition. The residual
  obstruction is the \emph{global} resurgent sheaf: a unique trans-series
  $F^{\mathrm{np}}(\lambda, t)$ on the full moduli space whose Stokes
  jumps match the BCOV monodromy representation on $H^3(\widetilde Q, \Z)$.
  This is open beyond the quintic and open globally on the quintic
  moduli space; it is checked locally at each of the three special loci under
  the normalizations named above.
\end{rmk}
